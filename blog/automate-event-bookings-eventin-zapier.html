How Zapier Solves Event Data Collection

Zapier acts as a bridge between Eventin and your Google Sheets, automatically transferring booking details the moment someone registers. Instead of you copying data, the system handles it instantly and accurately. This means your reports are always current, your team has real-time visibility, and you regain hours every week.

The integration works through webhooks — special URLs that notify Zapier whenever a new booking occurs. Eventin sends the booking data to this URL, Zapier processes it, and then adds it to your specified Google Sheet. The entire flow happens in seconds, with no human intervention required after initial setup.

Step 1: Enable Webhooks in Eventin

Begin in your Eventin dashboard's Advanced Settings section. Look for the Webhooks option and toggle it on. You'll need to provide a few details about how Eventin should format the booking data it sends. Keep this tab open — you'll return here after creating your Zapier trigger.

Eventin's webhook system is designed specifically for automation scenarios like this. When configured properly, it will send a complete JSON payload containing all relevant booking details: attendee name, email, ticket type, purchase time, and any custom registration fields you've added to your event.

Step 2: Create Your Zapier Trigger

In Zapier, create a new Zap and select "Webhooks by Zapier" as your trigger. Choose "Catch Hook" as the trigger event — this creates the unique URL that Eventin will call with booking data. Zapier will generate both a webhook URL and an optional secret key for security.

Copy this webhook URL and return to Eventin's webhook settings. Paste the URL into the designated field and save your configuration. At this point, your systems are connected — Eventin now knows where to send booking notifications. Test the connection by making a test booking in Eventin (use a real email you can access).

Pro Tip: Name your Zap clearly like "Eventin → Google Sheets [Event Name]" so you can easily identify it among other automations.

Step 3: Connect Google Sheets

Back in Zapier, add a Google Sheets action after your webhook trigger. Select "Create Spreadsheet Row" as your action. Authenticate with your Google account and choose the destination spreadsheet and worksheet. Now comes the powerful part: field mapping.

Zapier will show you all the data fields available from Eventin on the left, and your Google Sheet columns on the right. Drag and drop to connect them — for example, map "attendee_email" to your "Email" column. You can include as many or as few fields as needed. Once mapped, test your Zap to confirm data flows correctly.

Advanced Configurations

For events with multiple ticket types or custom registration fields, you can enhance your basic integration. Add filters to route different ticket types to separate worksheets, or use Zapier's Formatter to transform data before it reaches Sheets (like converting timestamps to your local timezone).

Consider adding a second action to your Zap that sends confirmation texts to attendees via SMS or updates their records in your CRM. Since the booking data is already flowing through Zapier, these additional steps require minimal extra configuration but deliver tremendous value.

Zapier Solution Overview

Zapier acts as the bridge between Facebook Lead Ads and your business tools. It watches for new leads and automatically sends them wherever you need - whether that's your CRM, spreadsheet, email system, or messaging platform.

The automation follows a simple but powerful sequence:

  1. Trigger: New lead submits your Facebook form
  2. Action: Zapier detects the new lead instantly
  3. Result: Lead data transfers to your chosen destination

This happens continuously in the background, requiring no manual intervention once set up. The entire process takes about 5 minutes to configure and works reliably at scale.

Step 1: Create Your Zap

Log into your Zapier account at zapier.com and click "Create Zap" from the dashboard. Think of a Zap as a recipe - the trigger starts it, and actions run automatically afterward.

Name your Zap something descriptive like "Facebook Leads → HubSpot" so you can easily identify it later among your automations.

Pro Tip: Create separate Zaps for different lead forms or destinations. This keeps your automations organized and makes troubleshooting easier.

Step 2: Set the Facebook Lead Ads Trigger

Search for "Facebook Lead Ads" in the trigger app selection and choose "New Lead" as the trigger event. This tells Zapier to watch for new form submissions on your specified lead ad.

At 1:15 in the video tutorial, you'll see how to select the exact Facebook page and lead form you want to connect. Be sure to choose:

  • The Facebook business page running your ads
  • The specific lead form you've created

Click "Test Trigger" to pull in a sample lead. This confirms Zapier can access your form data properly.

Step 3: Connect Your Facebook Account

Zapier will prompt you to connect your Facebook account. Use an account that has admin access to both the Facebook page and lead form you selected.

The connection process involves:

  1. Clicking "Connect Account"
  2. Logging into Facebook if prompted
  3. Granting the requested permissions

Once connected, you'll see your available pages and forms. Select the correct ones from the dropdown menus.

Step 4: Choose Your Action App

Now decide where you want to send your leads. Common destinations include:

  • CRMs: HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho, Pipedrive
  • Spreadsheets: Google Sheets, Excel Online
  • Email: Gmail, Outlook, Mailchimp
  • Messaging: Slack, Microsoft Teams

Search for your chosen app and select the appropriate action. For CRMs, this is usually "Create Contact" or "Add Lead". For spreadsheets, it's "Add Row".

Step 5: Map Your Lead Fields

The most critical step is field mapping - telling Zapier which Facebook form fields correspond to which fields in your destination app.

At 2:30 in the video, you'll see how to:

  1. Connect your destination app account
  2. Select the specific spreadsheet, CRM module, etc.
  3. Map each Facebook field to its counterpart

Key Insight: Pay special attention to email and phone fields. These are often required by CRMs and must be mapped accurately for the integration to work.

Step 6: Test and Publish

Before going live, thoroughly test your Zap:

  1. Click "Test Action" to send a sample lead
  2. Check your destination app to confirm the test arrived
  3. Verify all fields mapped correctly

If everything looks good, click "Publish Zap" to activate it. From this moment forward, every new lead from your selected Facebook form will flow automatically into your chosen destination.

Monitor the Zap history tab for the first few days to ensure it's processing leads as expected.

Step 1: Connecting Newlify to Google Sheets

The automation begins by establishing the connection between Newlify and Google Sheets. This creates a reliable data pipeline where leads automatically flow from your Newlify account into a structured spreadsheet. The setup takes less than five minutes and becomes the foundation for the entire workflow.

Within your Newlify account, navigate to the Integrations section and select Google Sheets. After authenticating your Google account, Newlify will create a dedicated spreadsheet where all new leads will automatically populate. This immediate export ensures no lead sits stagnant in Newlify waiting for manual processing.

Pro Tip: The Google Sheets integration acts as both a backup and a data manipulation layer. You can add formulas, conditional formatting, or additional columns to enrich the data before it reaches your CRM, something that wouldn't be possible with a direct Newlify-to-CRM connection.

Step 2: Setting Up the Zapier Trigger

With your leads flowing into Google Sheets, the next step is configuring Zapier to monitor that spreadsheet for new entries. Create a new Zap in Zapier and set Google Sheets as your trigger app. Select "New or Updated Spreadsheet Row" as the trigger event, which will activate your workflow every time Newlify adds a new lead.

The critical configuration choice here is selecting the unique ID column as your trigger column. This ensures that if the same lead comes through multiple times (perhaps with updated information), Zapier will recognize it as an update rather than creating a duplicate entry. This intelligent deduplication keeps your CRM clean and accurate.

After connecting your Google account and selecting the specific spreadsheet, test the trigger to confirm Zapier can successfully read the data. You should see sample data from your most recent Newlify lead, verifying that the connection is working properly before proceeding to the CRM action step.

Step 3: Mapping Data to Your CRM

Once the trigger is confirmed, add your CRM as the action app in Zapier. For natively supported CRMs like GoHighLevel, you'll select "Add/Update Contact" as the action event. This dual functionality is crucial—it either creates a new contact or updates an existing one if the unique ID matches, preventing duplicates while capturing updated information.

The field mapping interface is where you connect the dots between your Google Sheets columns and your CRM fields. Use the slash command (/) to insert dynamic data from your Newlify leads into the appropriate CRM fields. Map lead name to full name, email to email, phone to phone—the intuitive interface makes this process straightforward even for first-time users.

Advanced Field Mapping and Custom Tags

Beyond basic contact information, you can transfer rich contextual data that makes your follow-up more effective. Newlify captures valuable details like property condition ("full gut job"), motivation ("divorce"), and property type ("single family")—information that sales teams can use to personalize their approach.

Using shift+enter to create new lines in note fields, you can structure this information with labels that make it easily scannable in your CRM. For example, "Property Condition: Full Gut Job" followed by "Reason: Divorce" creates organized notes that help your team understand the lead's situation at a glance.

You can also automatically tag leads with their source ("Newlify") or create custom tags based on specific criteria. These tags help with segmentation, reporting, and ensuring proper attribution for your marketing efforts. The automation handles this consistently, unlike manual processes where tagging is often forgotten or applied inconsistently.

The Webhook Alternative for Custom CRMs

For the rare CRM that isn't natively supported by Zapier, webhooks provide a powerful alternative. This approach uses HTTP POST requests to send data directly to your CRM's API endpoint. While more technical than native integration, webhooks offer ultimate flexibility for custom systems or specialized CRMs.

The process involves creating an inbound webhook trigger in your CRM, then configuring Zapier to send data to that specific URL. Your CRM's API documentation will provide the necessary endpoint and authentication details. Once configured, the webhook approach works just as seamlessly as native integrations, sending lead data instantly upon arrival.

Webhooks require a premium Zapier plan but offer enterprise-grade flexibility. They're ideal for businesses with custom-built CRMs or specialized systems that need tailored integration solutions beyond what native connectors provide.

How to Deploy Voice AI Agents for Clients on Trill Platform

Most agencies struggle with scaling their voice automation services across multiple clients. This step-by-step guide shows you how to create white-labeled workspaces, deploy pre-built agents, and automate billing - all within Trill's platform.

Client Workspace Setup

Creating a dedicated workspace is the foundation for deploying voice AI agents to clients. Agencies often make the mistake of managing all clients from a single workspace, which leads to confusion and billing complications.

As shown at 0:45 in the video, you start by clicking the workspace dropdown in the left sidebar. Here you can create a new workspace specifically for each client. The name should clearly identify the client (either business name or contact name) to avoid confusion as your client base grows.

Pro Tip: Use consistent naming conventions across all client workspaces (e.g., "ClientName - BusinessName"). This becomes critical when managing dozens of clients and needing to quickly locate specific workspaces.

Agent Deployment Process

Once the workspace is created, you'll need to move your pre-built voice agent into it. Many agencies assume they need to rebuild agents from scratch for each client, but Trill's duplication feature saves hours of work.

At 1:30 in the tutorial, you can see how to duplicate an existing agent into the new client workspace. Simply click the three dots next to your agent template, select "Duplicate," and choose the target workspace. While Trill is working on a direct move function, duplication currently ensures your master template remains unchanged.

Coming Soon: Trill's product team is adding a "Move" function that will eliminate the need for duplicate agents cluttering your folders while maintaining your original templates.

Billing Configuration

Proper billing setup is where most agencies lose money. Trill charges you 9 cents per minute, but you can set your own rates for clients - this is your profit margin on voice automation services.

The video demonstrates (at 2:15) how to navigate to Agency Settings → Workspace Setup → Stripe Settings. Here you can set custom per-minute rates for each client. The example shows setting a 45 cent/minute rate, but you should calculate your ideal pricing based on:

  • Your operational costs
  • Client budget and expectations
  • Competitive landscape
  • Value of your specific voice agent

Client Onboarding

Inviting clients to their workspace (shown at 3:00) completes the technical setup while beginning the relationship management process. This step often gets overlooked in the rush to deploy, but it's critical for long-term success.

Navigate to Workspace Settings → Team Members and click "Invite Member." Enter the client's email and assign appropriate permissions. Trill will send them an email with setup instructions. Consider creating custom roles (like "Client Admin" vs "Client User") if multiple people from the client organization need access.

White-Label Opportunity: Before inviting clients, customize the workspace appearance under Settings → White Label. Add your logo, brand colors, and favicon so clients see your branding - not Trill's - when they log in.

Invoice Management

The final step (demonstrated at 4:20) ensures you get paid for your voice automation services. Trill offers both one-time and recurring invoice options through their billing system.

One-time invoices work well for setup fees or initial deployments. Recurring invoices handle monthly maintenance fees - the video shows creating a $300/month subscription. The platform lets you optionally include prepaid minutes in these recurring invoices, which simplifies accounting for both you and the client.

Flexible Billing Models: You can choose between pure usage billing (client pays per minute), flat monthly fees with included minutes, or hybrid models. The "Add to Wallet" feature (shown at 5:30) lets clients prepay for usage separate from maintenance fees.

How to Process Multiple Facebook Lead Ads in One Zapier Workflow

Most marketers create separate Zaps for each Facebook Lead Form - leading to maintenance nightmares. This centralized approach handles all your forms in one workflow using form ID filtering, saving hours of updates while allowing different actions for each lead type.

The Multiple Zap Problem

Marketing teams running Facebook Lead Ads typically create separate Zapier workflows for each form - one Zap for webinar registrations, another for ebook downloads, another for consultation requests. This creates three major problems:

First, every form change requires updating multiple Zaps. Second, monitoring lead quality becomes fragmented across different workflows. Third, common actions (like CRM updates) get duplicated across Zaps, increasing error risk.

83% of marketers using this approach report spending at least 2 hours per month just maintaining their separate Zaps, according to our agency data.

Centralized Trigger Setup

The secret lies in how you configure the Facebook Lead Ads trigger. Instead of selecting a specific form from the dropdown, leave the form field blank in your trigger setup.

This tells Zapier to capture submissions from all forms on your connected Facebook Page. At 1:32 in the video, you'll see exactly where to leave this field empty during trigger configuration.

Capturing Form IDs

Each Facebook Lead Form has a unique identifier that appears in the trigger data. After setting up your blank-form trigger, run a test to see sample data containing the form ID field.

These IDs typically look like long strings of numbers (e.g., 123456789012345). You'll use these exact IDs to filter and route your leads in subsequent steps.

Filter vs Path Routing

There are two primary ways to handle different form types in your centralized Zap:

Filter Approach

Add a Filter step after your trigger with conditions like "Form ID exactly matches 123456789". This stops unwanted forms from continuing down your workflow.

Path Approach

Use a Path step to create different action branches. At 3:15 in the video, you'll see how to set up paths for different form IDs, allowing unique actions for each lead type.

Step-by-Step Implementation

Step 1: Create New Zap

Start a new Zap with Facebook Lead Ads as the trigger app. Select "New Lead" as the trigger event.

Step 2: Configure Trigger

Connect your Facebook account, select your Page, but leave the Form field blank to capture all submissions.

Step 3: Test Trigger

Run a test to generate sample data containing form IDs from recent submissions.

Step 4: Add Filter or Path

Create conditions based on form IDs to route different form types appropriately.

Pro Tip: Use "contains" instead of "exactly matches" if you want to group forms with similar naming conventions.

Maintenance Benefits

Centralizing your lead processing creates significant operational advantages. Adding a new form? Just include its ID in your existing Filter or Path steps.

Need to update your CRM integration? Make the change once instead of across multiple Zaps. This approach typically reduces maintenance time by 65-80% based on our client implementations.

Advanced Routing Tips

Take your centralized workflow further with these pro techniques:

  • Use form IDs plus custom field values to create ultra-specific routing
  • Combine with Lead Ads' "ad_id" field to track which campaigns perform best
  • Add delay filters to batch process leads at specific times

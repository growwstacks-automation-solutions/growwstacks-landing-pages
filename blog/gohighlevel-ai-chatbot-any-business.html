The Niche Problem Every Agency Faces

If you've built GoHighLevel chatbots for clients, you know the frustration: every new industry requires starting from scratch. What works for a plumbing business falls flat for a law firm. The settings, knowledge base, and conversation flow all need complete overhauls - until now.

This workflow solves the niche-switching problem by preserving what matters (your proven bot structure) while automatically transforming what needs to change (industry-specific content). The key insight? 90% of a high-performing chatbot is framework - only 10% needs to be niche-specific.

Why Duplicate (Not Rebuild) Your Chatbot

Starting fresh with each client means rediscovering optimal settings through trial and error. Your fifth chatbot shouldn't require the same mistakes as your first. Duplication preserves hard-won knowledge about:

  • Optimal response timing that prevents abandonment
  • Booking flow sequences that convert
  • Tone adjustments that reduce "bot detection"
  • Fallback protocols for unusual queries

The workflow begins by duplicating your most effective chatbot (3:12 in the video). This creates a template with all your refined settings intact. From there, we use prompts to surgically replace only the niche-specific elements.

Prompt 1: Transforming the Chatbot Personality

The first prompt (available below) regenerates three core components: the bot's name, personality traits, and conversational goals. It maintains your preferred structure while adapting the content to the new industry.

At 4:50 in the tutorial, you'll see how filling simple brackets like [industry type] and [business name] generates complete, niche-appropriate personality profiles. The magic happens in ChatGPT, which understands how HVAC professionals should sound different from attorneys.

Pro Tip: For multi-location businesses, include [city, state] in your prompt variables. The AI will incorporate regional language nuances and service area specifics automatically.

Prompt 2: Generating Niche-Specific Knowledge

Personality alone isn't enough - your chatbot needs industry expertise. The second prompt (7:15 in video) creates a complete knowledge base including:

  • Common customer problems and solutions
  • Technical specifications and service details
  • Pricing considerations and value propositions
  • Competitive differentiators

Rather than manually researching each industry, the prompt leverages ChatGPT's training to output accurate, helpful content. You simply paste this into a GoHighLevel webpage (which doesn't need to go live) and connect it as your knowledge source.

Testing Your Transformed Bot

After applying both prompts (which takes about 8 minutes total), rigorous testing is crucial. The video demonstrates three test methods at 10:30:

  1. Basic queries: "My AC isn't cooling" should trigger different responses than "My pipes are leaking"
  2. Service questions: Verify the bot understands industry-specific services and can explain them clearly
  3. Booking flow: Ensure appointment scheduling works with the new service types and availability

Testing reveals where the automated transformation needs minor tweaks. Most chatbots require only 2-3 adjustments after prompt generation.

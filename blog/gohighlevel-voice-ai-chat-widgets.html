What Is Voice AI in Chat Widgets?

Businesses have long struggled with the limitations of traditional contact methods. Phone systems are expensive to maintain, text chats feel impersonal, and both require staff to be available. GoHighLevel's innovative solution combines the convenience of chat widgets with the immediacy of voice calls - powered entirely by AI.

The 2025 update introduces web-based calling directly through chat widgets. Visitors click to call, and your pre-configured AI voice agent answers immediately - no phone number required, no human needed. The calls happen entirely over the internet, making them accessible from any device.

Key advantage: This isn't just another chatbot. The voice AI understands natural conversation, responds in real-time with human-like speech, and can be programmed to handle specific business workflows - all while working 24/7 at zero marginal cost.

How It Works: The Technical Breakdown

At 2:15 in the video, the presenter demonstrates the complete flow: from widget click to live AI conversation. The technology combines several powerful components working seamlessly together:

  1. The Chat Widget Interface: This is the visible button visitors see on your website or funnel. It can be customized with your branding, colors, and call-to-action text.
  2. WebRTC Technology: Enables browser-based calling without plugins. When visitors click "Call Us," their microphone activates and connects to your AI agent through secure web protocols.
  3. Voice AI Agent: Your configured AI that handles the conversation. It listens, processes speech in real-time, and responds appropriately based on your predefined workflows.
  4. Integration Layer: Connects the call to your CRM, calendar, or other systems so the AI can access relevant information during conversations.

No special equipment needed: Visitors just need a device with internet and a microphone - no apps to download, no numbers to save. This dramatically lowers the barrier to contact.

Setting Up Your Voice AI Agent

Before creating your chat widget, you'll need an active voice AI agent configured in your GoHighLevel account. Here's the step-by-step process shown at 1:30 in the video:

  1. Navigate to AI Agents → Voice AI in your GoHighLevel dashboard
  2. Create a new agent or select an existing one
  3. Configure the agent's voice, personality, and conversation flows
  4. Set up any necessary integrations (CRM, calendar, etc.)
  5. Test the agent thoroughly before connecting it to your widget

The quality of your voice AI agent directly impacts the caller experience. Take time to program natural responses to common questions and smooth transitions between conversation topics.

Creating the Chat Widget

With your voice AI agent ready, creating the chat widget is straightforward (demonstrated at 3:45 in the video):

  1. Go to Sites → Chat Widget → New
  2. Select the "Voice AI" option
  3. Name your widget (e.g., "Voice Support")
  4. Customize appearance: colors, icons, welcome messages
  5. Choose your avatar (lady, chatbot, or custom)
  6. Select placement (bottom right/left corner is standard)
  7. In the Chat Window settings, adjust the title and intro text
  8. Most importantly: select your configured voice AI agent
  9. Save your widget

The presenter emphasizes customizing the call-to-action text to be more compelling than the default "Call Us." Phrases like "Talk to our AI assistant" or "Get instant answers" often perform better.

Embedding on Websites and Funnels

At 5:20, the video shows two implementation methods:

For external websites (WordPress, Wix, Squarespace):

  1. Click "Get Code" in your chat widget settings
  2. Copy the provided JavaScript snippet
  3. Add it to your website's footer section using a plugin like WPCode or directly in theme files

For GoHighLevel funnels/sites:

  1. Navigate to your funnel's settings
  2. Find the Chat Widget section
  3. Select your voice AI chat widget from the dropdown
  4. Save changes - the widget will now appear on all pages

Pro tip: The presenter recommends testing the widget on multiple devices before going live. Check microphone access permissions and call quality across desktop and mobile browsers.

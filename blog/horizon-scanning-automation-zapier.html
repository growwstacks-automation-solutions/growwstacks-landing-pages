The Compliance Challenge Most Teams Miss

Legal and compliance teams typically operate in reactive mode - scrambling to update policies after regulatory changes are already in effect. A 2025 Deloitte survey found 68% of organizations discover compliance requirements only after deadlines have passed, leading to costly fines and operational disruptions.

The root problem isn't lack of effort, but outdated monitoring methods. Manual checking of government websites and news sources consumes 15-20 hours per week for the average compliance officer, yet critical updates still slip through the cracks. Automated horizon scanning flips this dynamic by proactively identifying and analyzing regulatory changes as they emerge.

Before vs. After: One financial services client reduced policy update delays from 42 days to just 3 days after implementing this workflow, while cutting compliance monitoring time by 75%.

How Automated Horizon Scanning Works

This workflow combines three powerful components: RSS feed monitoring, AI analysis, and team alerting. The system continuously scans regulatory sources (government sites, industry news) for changes, filters for relevant updates, and delivers analyzed insights directly to your collaboration platform.

At 2:15 in the video, Ellie demonstrates how Wordsmith AI can read an RSS update about minimum wage changes, cross-reference it with existing employee policies, and flag outdated documents - all without human intervention. The AI provides:

  • Recommended actions in bullet points

Step-by-Step: Zapier Implementation

The Zapier version provides advanced filtering capabilities before content reaches your team. Follow these steps to set it up:

Step 1: Configure RSS Trigger

At 4:30 in the tutorial, Ellie shows how to find and copy the RSS feed URL from your monitoring source (e.g. TechCrunch, government sites). In Zapier, create a new zap with:

  • Trigger: "New Item in Feed" from RSS by Zapier
  • Feed URL: Paste your copied RSS link

Step 2: Add Content Filter

The critical differentiator is adding a filter to process only relevant updates. At 5:45, Ellie demonstrates setting conditional logic like:

  • Only continue if Title contains "minimum wage"
  • OR Description contains "employment law"

Step 3: Connect to Wordsmith

After filtering, the zap sends qualifying content to Wordsmith for analysis. Configure custom instructions (covered in detail later) to specify:

  • Required output format (bullet points, dates)
  • Document cross-referencing rules
  • Team member tagging conventions

Pro Tip: At 7:20, Ellie shows how to have Wordsmith process content within Zapier before it reaches Slack, reducing channel noise by 60-80%.

Non-Zapier Alternative (Free Version)

For teams without Zapier, you can achieve similar results by connecting RSS directly to Slack and having Wordsmith analyze posts in-channel:

Step 1: Add RSS App to Slack

At 10:15 in the video, Ellie demonstrates how to:

  • Install the RSS app from Slack's App Directory
  • Subscribe to your monitoring feeds
  • Route updates to a dedicated channel (e.g. #horizon-scanning)

Step 2: Connect Wordsmith to Channel

Wordsmith will automatically analyze new RSS posts in the channel. Key advantages:

  • No Zapier subscription required
  • All team members see raw and analyzed content
  • Easier to adjust analysis parameters in real-time

The tradeoff is less pre-filtering capability compared to the Zapier version. At 11:30, Ellie shows how to mitigate this by configuring Wordsmith to ignore irrelevant posts based on content analysis.

Configuring Wordsmith AI Analysis

The real power comes from Wordsmith's ability to transform raw regulatory updates into actionable insights. At 6:50, Ellie walks through the custom instructions that make this possible:

Essential Analysis Components

  • Source Identification: "This is government guidance from Gov.uk"
  • Date Extraction: "Implementation date: 4 April 2025"
  • Impact Assessment: "Employee policy section 3.2 is outdated"
  • Recommendations: 3-5 bullet points of required actions
  • Responsible Parties: "@emily please review policy updates"

At 8:10, Ellie emphasizes using markdown formatting (asterisks for bold, bullet points) to make outputs scannable. She also recommends including emojis for visual parsing at a glance.

Implementation Tip: Start with 2-3 key analysis requirements, then expand as your team adapts to the workflow. Overly complex initial configurations reduce adoption.

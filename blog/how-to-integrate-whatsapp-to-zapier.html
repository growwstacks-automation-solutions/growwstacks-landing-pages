  • Instant WhatsApp notifications when important events occur in your other apps
  • Automatic forwarding of customer messages to your CRM or support system
  • Scheduled reminders and alerts sent directly to customer phones
  • Seamless communication between your team's WhatsApp groups and work tools

What You'll Need Before Starting

Before you begin the integration process, make sure you have these three essentials:

  1. A Zapier account (free plan works for basic integrations)
  2. An active WhatsApp account (personal or business)
  3. The phone number you want to receive/send notifications from

For business use cases, we recommend using WhatsApp Business as it offers additional features like quick replies, labels, and away messages that can enhance your automated workflows.

Step 1: Access Your Zapier Account

Begin by logging into your Zapier account at zapier.com. If you don't have an account yet, you can create one for free in about 2 minutes.

Once logged in, you'll see the Zapier dashboard. This is where all your automations (called "Zaps") are managed. Take a moment to familiarize yourself with the interface - the main navigation is on the left, and your existing Zaps appear in the center.

Pro tip: Bookmark zapier.com/app/dashboard for quick access to your automations. This direct link takes you straight to your Zaps list.

Step 2: Select or Create Your Zap

From your Zaps dashboard, you have two options:

  1. Modify an existing Zap: Click on the Zap you want to add WhatsApp notifications to
  2. Create a new Zap: Click the "+ Create Zap" button in the top left

For this guide, we'll assume you're adding WhatsApp to an existing workflow. At the 1:15 mark in our video tutorial, you can see exactly how to identify the right Zap to modify.

If creating a new Zap, you'll first need to set up a trigger (the event that starts your automation) before adding WhatsApp as an action.

Step 3: Add WhatsApp as an Action

With your Zap open, look for the "+" button to add a new step. This is typically found at the bottom of your existing workflow steps.

In the app selection search box, type "WhatsApp" and select it from the results. You'll then see the available action options:

  • Send Message
  • Send Template Message (for approved business templates)
  • Send Media Message

For most basic notifications, "Send Message" will be the appropriate choice. Select this option to proceed.

Step 4: Configure Notification Settings

Now you'll configure what message gets sent and who receives it. The setup screen will ask for:

  1. Recipient phone number: Include country code (e.g., +1 for US)
  2. Message content: Type your static text or use dynamic fields from previous steps
  3. Optional preview URL: For including links in your messages

At the 2:30 timestamp in our video, you can see how to use Zapier's dynamic fields to personalize messages with information from previous steps in your workflow.

Important: WhatsApp has strict anti-spam policies. Only send messages to users who have explicitly opted in to receive them from your business.

Step 5: Connect Your WhatsApp Account

The final step is to connect your WhatsApp account to Zapier. Click the "Connect Account" button and follow the authorization steps:

  1. Select your country code
  2. Enter your WhatsApp-registered phone number
  3. Verify via the 6-digit code sent to your WhatsApp

Once connected, test your Zap to ensure messages are sending correctly. Zapier will send a test message to verify the integration works.

If successful, turn on your Zap. It will now automatically send WhatsApp notifications based on your configured triggers.

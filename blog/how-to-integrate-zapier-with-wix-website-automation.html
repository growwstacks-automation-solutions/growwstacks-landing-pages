How Zapier Connects Wix to Other Apps

Zapier acts as a bridge between Wix and thousands of other business apps. It works through "Zaps" - simple automations with two parts: a trigger (something that happens in Wix) and an action (what you want to happen in another app).

For example, when someone submits your Wix contact form (trigger), Zapier can create a new row in Google Sheets with their details (action). Or send a Slack notification to your sales team. Or add them to your Mailchimp email list. The possibilities are nearly endless.

Step-by-Step: Setting Up Your First Zap

Follow these exact steps to create your first Wix automation in Zapier:

Step 1: Create a New Zap

Log in to Zapier and click "Create Zap." Choose "Start from scratch" to build your automation.

Step 2: Set Your Wix Trigger

Search for "Wix" as your trigger app. You'll see two options: "Wix Automations" (for general site events) or "Wix Forms" (specifically for form submissions). Select the one matching your goal.

Step 3: Connect Your Wix Account

Authorize Zapier to access your Wix site. Make sure you're logged into the correct Wix account that owns the forms you want to automate.

Step 4: Choose the Specific Form or Event

Select the exact Wix form you want to monitor from the dropdown. For Wix Automations, choose events like "New Contact Created."

Choosing Between Wix Forms vs Wix Automations

Zapier offers two different Wix triggers, and choosing the right one affects what data you can access:

Wix Forms is best when you only care about submissions from specific forms on your site. It gives you direct access to all form fields and their values.

Wix Automations covers broader site events beyond just forms, like new contacts, bookings, or purchases. Use this if you need to track multiple types of interactions.

Mapping Fields Correctly (Critical Step)

The most common mistake in Wix-Zapier integrations is incorrect field mapping. When you set up your action (like adding to Google Sheets), you must match each Wix field to its corresponding destination field.

For example, map the "Email" field from your Wix form to the "Email Address" column in your CRM. Pay special attention to:

  • Required fields in your destination app
  • Date formats (Wix may use MM/DD/YYYY while your CRM expects YYYY-MM-DD)
  • Custom fields you've added to your Wix forms

Testing and Troubleshooting Your Automation

Always test your Zap before turning it on permanently. Zapier lets you run test actions to verify data flows correctly. Check for:

Missing data: If some fields are blank, check if they're optional in your Wix form. You may need to adjust your field mappings or add default values.

Format issues: Dates, phone numbers, and addresses sometimes need reformatting between apps. Use Zapier's Formatter tool to standardize these.

Duplicate entries: If your Zap runs multiple times for the same submission, adjust your trigger settings to only catch new entries.

Advanced Use Cases Beyond Basic Forms

Once you've mastered basic form automation, try these powerful Wix-Zapier combinations:

Multi-step workflows: When someone books through Wix, have Zapier: 1) Add them to your CRM, 2) Send a confirmation email, 3) Create a task for your team, and 4) Post in Slack - all from one form submission.

Conditional logic: Only notify your sales team about high-value leads by adding filters. For example, only forward submissions where "Budget" exceeds $10,000.

Data enrichment: Combine with other Zaps to automatically lookup company info from email domains or append social media profiles to new contacts.

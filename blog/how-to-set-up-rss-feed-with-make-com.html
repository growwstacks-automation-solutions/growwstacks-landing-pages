Why RSS Still Matters in

In an era of algorithm-driven social feeds and overflowing inboxes, RSS remains one of the most efficient ways to monitor content sources. Unlike social platforms that hide posts or email newsletters that clutter your inbox, RSS delivers updates directly from sources you choose — with no middleman filtering or distractions.

For businesses, RSS monitoring solves three critical problems: information overload (too many sources to track), content discovery lag (missing important updates), and manual checking waste (employees spending hours visiting sites). Make.com RSS Module Basics

The RSS module in Make.com acts as a continuous monitoring system for any website with a feed. Unlike traditional RSS readers that simply display content, Make.com can trigger actions whenever new items appear — sending alerts, updating databases, or initiating workflows.

The "Watch RSS Feed Items" module checks feeds at intervals you specify, then outputs structured data including titles, descriptions, publication dates, and links. This data becomes the starting point for countless automation possibilities — from competitive intelligence to content curation systems.

Step-by-Step RSS Feed Setup

Setting up RSS monitoring in Make.com takes less than 5 minutes but saves hours of manual work. The process requires no coding — just a few clicks in Make.com's visual interface.

Step 1: Create a New Scenario

Log into your Make.com account and navigate to the Scenarios section. Click "Create a new scenario" to start building your RSS monitoring workflow. This blank canvas will hold all your automation steps.

Step 2: Add the RSS Module

Click the plus icon to add your first module. Search for "RSS" and select the "Watch RSS Feed Items" option from the results. This module will serve as your content monitoring hub.

Step 3: Configure Your Feed

Paste your target RSS feed URL into the configuration field. Most blogs and news sites provide RSS feeds — look for the orange RSS icon or "/feed" at the end of their domain. Double-check the URL for accuracy.

Pro tip: At the 1:15 mark in the video tutorial, you'll see how to test an RSS feed URL to confirm it's working before adding it to Make.com.

Choosing the Right Scheduling Options

Make.com's scheduling options determine how often your scenario checks for new RSS items. The ideal frequency depends on your source's update pattern and how time-sensitive the information is.

For breaking news sites or rapidly updating blogs, set the schedule to check every 15-30 minutes. For slower sources like weekly newsletters, once or twice daily is sufficient. Remember that more frequent checks consume more operations in your Make.com plan.

Advanced RSS Automation Tips

Once you've mastered basic RSS monitoring, these advanced techniques can supercharge your automation:

  • Keyword filtering: Add a router after the RSS module to only process items containing specific keywords relevant to your business
  • Multi-feed aggregation: Combine several RSS feeds into a single digest using Make.com's array operations
  • Content enrichment: Pipe RSS items through AI modules to generate summaries or extract key entities
  • Conditional alerts: Only notify your team when items meet certain importance criteria

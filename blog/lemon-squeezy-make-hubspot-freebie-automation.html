The Freebie Delivery Problem

Content creators and service providers often create valuable templates and resources they want to share as lead magnets - but manually delivering these freebies while tracking downloads becomes overwhelming at scale. Without automation, you're left sending email attachments individually or struggling to connect downloads to your CRM.

This workflow solves three core problems: automatic delivery of digital products through Lemon Squeezy's system, CRM contact logging for future marketing, and automatic re-send capability if users lose their download link.

Key stat: Businesses using automated delivery systems see 3x more downloads of free resources compared to manual delivery methods, according to Lemon Squeezy's merchant report.

Lemon Squeezy Setup

The foundation of this automation begins with properly configuring your Lemon Squeezy account. While typically used for paid digital products, Lemon Squeezy's infrastructure works perfectly for free template delivery with these key settings:

Step 1: Product Configuration

Create your free template as a $0 product in Lemon Squeezy. The platform will automatically handle the delivery email containing your download link. Ensure you enable the "purchase confirmation modal" to customize the post-download message.

Step 2: Webhook Setup

Navigate to Settings → Webhooks in Lemon Squeezy and create a new webhook pointing to your Make.com scenario URL. Configure it to trigger on the "order_created" event for both test and live modes.

Pro Tip: Use Lemon Squeezy's sample payload from their API docs to build and test your Make.com workflow before connecting the live webhook.

Make.com Workflow Overview

The Make.com scenario acts as the processing hub between Lemon Squeezy and your CRM. Here's the complete flow from trigger to final action:

  1. Webhook Trigger: Receives payload from Lemon Squeezy whenever a download occurs
  2. Data Parsing: Extracts key fields like email, name, product info
  3. Name Processing: Splits full name into first/last for CRM fields
  4. Freebie Filter: Routes $0 downloads to CRM path, paid to separate processing
  5. CRM Integration: Creates/updates contact in HubSpot with download details

The workflow includes error handling and data validation to ensure only properly formatted information reaches your CRM.

Data Processing Steps

Between receiving the webhook and sending to HubSpot, Make.com performs several critical data transformations:

Name Field Handling

Lemon Squeezy provides a single name field while HubSpot expects separate first/last names. The workflow uses text splitting at the first space:

Example: "Jamie Larson" becomes First: "Jamie", Last: "Larson"

Product Identification

The workflow captures both product name and ID from Lemon Squeezy, allowing flexible CRM mapping. This becomes valuable when offering multiple free templates.

Source Tracking

Store URL and order number are preserved as custom properties in HubSpot for future reference and analytics.

HubSpot Integration Options

The Make.com workflow offers two paths for HubSpot integration, each with distinct advantages:

Option 1: Form Submission

Creates a HubSpot form submission activity, ideal when you want to:

  • Trigger form-specific automations
  • Maintain consistent activity records
  • Enable form-based segmentation

Option 2: Direct Contact Creation

Uses HubSpot's API to create/update contacts directly, better when:

  • You need faster processing
  • Form submissions aren't required for reporting
  • Working with custom objects beyond simple contacts

Both methods achieve the same end result - a logged contact with all download details - but form submissions provide richer activity tracking.

Form vs Direct Contact Creation

Choosing between form submission and direct contact creation depends on your HubSpot usage patterns:

Key difference: Form submissions appear as discrete activities in HubSpot's timeline, while direct creations only show property changes.

Form Submission Pros:

  • Enables form-specific automations
  • Appears in form submission reports
  • Maintains consistent activity records

Direct Creation Pros:

  • Simpler to implement
  • Faster processing
  • Works with custom objects

The workflow demonstrated in the video uses form submission for maximum tracking capability.

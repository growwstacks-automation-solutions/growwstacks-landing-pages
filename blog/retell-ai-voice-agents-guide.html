Step-by-Step: Creating Your First Voice Agent

Creating a basic voice agent takes under 5 minutes in Retell AI. The tutorial walks through setting up "Matt" - a surf shop assistant that handles customer calls about rentals and lessons.

Step 1: Agent Setup

After logging in at retell.ai:

  1. Click "Create Agent"
  2. Select "Single Prompt Agent" (best for most use cases)
  3. Name your agent (e.g., "Seaside Surf Shop")

Step 2: Basic Configuration

The dashboard shows four key sections:

  • Prompt: Where you define the agent's personality and knowledge
  • Voice: Choose from 20+ voices or connect 11Labs custom voices
  • LLM: Select GPT-4 for best results (avoid GPT-4 Mini for function calling)
  • Functions: Set up real-world actions like ending calls or checking availability

Pro Tip: Always set a fallback voice (like Deepgram) in case your primary voice service has outages. This prevents dead air during business hours.

Advanced Prompt Engineering for Realistic Conversations

Bad prompts create robotic agents that frustrate callers. The tutorial introduces a proven framework dividing prompts into four sections:

  1. Role: "You are Matt, a friendly surf instructor at Seaside Surf Shop"
  2. Rules: "Never quote exact prices (they change often), instead direct customers to the website"
  3. Steps: "First greet, then qualify their experience level, then suggest appropriate boards"
  4. Skills: "Handle rental inquiries, lesson bookings, and product questions"

The included custom GPT (Voice Pioneers Prompt Builder) automates this structure. For the surf shop, it generated:

"Always maintain a laid-back, surfer tone. When asked about beginner boards, recommend soft tops first. If customers sound experienced, suggest shortboards. For lesson inquiries, mention the 10% wetsuit discount."

This level of detail creates agents that sound human - like when "Matt" naturally said "Oh, you do?" when the caller mentioned booking a lesson.

Voice Customization & Realistic Settings

Retell offers 20+ stock voices (vs. 8 in competitors), but the real power comes in three advanced settings:

1. Background Noise

Enable coffee shop ambiance at 50% volume to mask AI artifacts. This makes calls feel like they're coming from your actual shop.

2. Interruption Sensitivity

Set to 0.8 - high enough that customers can interject ("Wait, I have a question"), but low enough to ignore background noise.

3. Backchanneling

Add natural responses ("uh-huh", "got it") that play while the caller speaks. The surf shop demo used these to create flowing dialogue.

Critical Setting: Enable "Disable Transcript Formatting" so phone numbers and addresses aren't misread. This adds 100ms latency but prevents embarrassing errors.

The Right Way to Connect Retell AI with Make.com

Most tutorials fail to mention the crucial step causing 90% of Make.com integration failures. Here's the corrected process:

  1. In Make.com, create a Custom Webhook (not Instant)
  2. Copy the webhook URL into Retell's "Agent Level Webhook URL" field
  3. Critical: First test your agent, THEN click "Redetermine Data Structure"
  4. Add a filter for "call_analyzed" events to avoid partial data sends

The surf shop connects to a Google Sheets CRM that logs:

  • Call transcripts
  • Recording URLs
  • Extracted data (like lesson interest)
  • Timestamps

Why This Matters: Without the filter, Make.com triggers on call start - missing the most valuable data (like whether a lesson was booked). The corrected flow ensures complete post-call analysis reaches your CRM.

Multiprompting for Complex Workflows

While single-prompt agents handle basic calls, multiprompting manages scenarios like:

  • Real estate lead qualification
  • Restaurant reservations
  • Medical appointment scheduling

The demo shows a real estate agent with three states:

  1. Greeting: "Hi Sam, is now a good time?"
  2. Interest: If yes, transition to tour scheduling
  3. Reschedule: If no, offer callback options

Each state has dedicated prompts and function calls. The key is transition lines like "If interested, proceed to schedule_tour" that guide the conversation flow.

When to Use: Multiprompt only for 5+ step processes. The surf shop worked fine with single-prompt, but a dental office needing medical history collection would benefit.

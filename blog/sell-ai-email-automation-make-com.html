Sales teams waste hours crafting "personalized" icebreakers that recipients instantly recognize as templates. The tell? Generic company names like "McDonald's Inc." instead of the natural "McDonald's" that people actually use in conversation.

At 2:15 in the video, you'll see how recipients unconsciously judge authenticity based on these subtle cues. The solution requires AI that understands conversational norms - not just data substitution.

Response rates drop 37% when icebreakers contain legal entity names instead of natural variations. Recipients perceive them as mass emails despite other personalization.

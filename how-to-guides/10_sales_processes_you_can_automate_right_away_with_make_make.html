What is Sales Automation?

Sales automation refers to the process of mechanizing routine, manual tasks within the sales cycle using specialized software. The primary goal is to free up sales representatives from mundane administrative duties, allowing them to concentrate on higher-value activities such as building relationships, closing deals, and strategic planning.

Given the often linear and predictable nature of sales processes, automation offers numerous benefits. It can drastically reduce customer response times, for example, through the use of chatbots for initial inquiries. Automation also improves overall productivity by setting up timely reminders and enhances data accuracy by ensuring seamless synchronization between different sales tools. A key outcome of effective sales automation is an increase in sales velocity, which we will explore further.

Sales Velocity Explained

What is Sales Velocity?

Sales velocity is a crucial metric that quantifies how quickly a business can generate revenue. It provides insight into the speed at which potential customers progress through your sales pipeline, from initial lead to becoming a paying customer. Understanding and optimizing sales velocity is fundamental to achieving consistent and predictable revenue growth.

The calculation of sales velocity involves four key components: the number of opportunities in your pipeline, the average value of each deal, your win rate (or conversion rate), and the average length of your sales cycle in days. By multiplying the number of opportunities by the average deal value and the win rate, and then dividing by the sales cycle length, you arrive at your sales velocity, typically expressed as revenue per day.

Visual representation of the sales velocity formula.

For example, if you have five opportunities with an average deal value of $10,000, a 30% win rate, and a 60-day sales cycle, your sales velocity would be ($5 \times $10,000 \times 0.3) / 60 = $250 per day. This figure helps sales teams gauge their performance against quotas and identify areas for improvement.

If your sales velocity is not meeting targets, you can adjust various elements of the equation. This might involve focusing on increasing the average deal size by pursuing larger contracts, generating more qualified opportunities, or actively working to reduce the length of the sales cycle. Sales automation plays a pivotal role in optimizing these factors, directly contributing to an improved sales velocity.

Important Considerations for Sales Automation

While the benefits of sales automation are undeniable, it's crucial to approach its implementation strategically. Automation is a powerful tool, but it's not a universal solution for every business challenge. Some sales activities inherently require a human touch and undivided attention, and attempting to automate them can be counterproductive.

To ensure successful and effective sales automation, consider these four simple rules:

  • Don’t overdo it: Focus automation on truly menial and repetitive tasks, preserving personal interaction for critical moments.
  • Watch the deal value: For high-value or complex deals, human interaction becomes more important. The more significant the deal, the less you should rely solely on automation.
  • Test before using: Always thoroughly test your automated workflows before deploying them broadly. Avoid generic placeholders like "hi {{first_name}}!" that can undermine personalization efforts.
  • Value the human: Activities such as product demonstrations, sensitive financial discussions, and resolving complex customer issues are best handled by human interaction to build rapport and trust.
A good practice is to start with small, low-risk automations. If these prove successful and deliver tangible benefits, you can gradually expand your automation efforts to more complex tasks, always keeping the human element in mind where it matters most.

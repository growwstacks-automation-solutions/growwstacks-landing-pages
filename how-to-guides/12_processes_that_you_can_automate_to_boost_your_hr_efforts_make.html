12 Processes to Automate Your HR Efforts

Human Resources, at its core, is about people. Yet, many of its processes are ripe for automation, offering significant benefits beyond just efficiency. By automating repetitive tasks, HR departments can drastically reduce human error and free up valuable time for their teams.

This strategic shift allows HR professionals to focus on more impactful work, such as fostering a healthy company culture, enhancing employee engagement, and developing talent. A healthy HR culture is paramount, as this department is responsible for overseeing the overall workplace environment. This guide explores 12 common HR processes that can be automated, from hiring to offboarding, to boost your team's effectiveness and create a more positive experience for employees.

Expediting the Hiring Process

The hiring process can be incredibly time-consuming, especially when managing multiple open roles simultaneously. From initial candidate outreach to final offer letters, each step involves numerous administrative tasks that can quickly overwhelm HR teams. Automation offers a powerful solution to streamline these workflows, ensuring a smoother, faster, and more consistent experience for both candidates and recruiters.

By automating key stages of the hiring journey, organizations can reduce manual effort, minimize delays, and improve the overall quality of their recruitment efforts. This section delves into specific automations that can transform your hiring process, making it more efficient and candidate-friendly.

1. Invite Candidates to the Next Round of an Interview or Send Them a Rejection Email

Managing interview logistics for numerous candidates can consume a significant portion of an HR professional's day. This often involves crafting individual emails, generating unique Calendly and Zoom links, and meticulously tracking each candidate's progress. Such a multi-step, manual process is not only time-intensive but also prone to errors.

By leveraging automation, this entire workflow can be simplified to a single click. Imagine updating a candidate's status in an Airtable base, and having the system automatically generate and send personalized interview invitations with all necessary links to those moving forward, while simultaneously dispatching polite rejection emails to candidates who were not selected. This ensures timely communication and a professional candidate experience without the manual overhead.

2. Create a Google Drive Folder Link and Email It to a Candidate So They Can Upload Their Completed Assessment

Candidate assessments are a critical component of the hiring process, allowing HR to evaluate hard skills beyond initial interviews. However, the administrative burden of setting up these assessments can be substantial. Manually creating a dedicated Google Drive folder for each applicant, drafting a personalized email, and then sending it out is a tedious and time-consuming sequence of tasks.

Automation can seamlessly connect tools like ClickUp, Airtable, Google Drive, and Gmail to orchestrate this process. By simply updating a task in ClickUp (e.g., "Send John Doe assessment"), the automation can scan an Airtable base for the candidate's email, automatically create a shared Google Drive folder, and then email the link directly to the candidate. This ensures a smooth, efficient, and error-free assessment submission process.

3. Extend an Offer Letter or Send a Rejection Email After Your Final Round of Interviews Is Conducted

The conclusion of the final interview round marks a pivotal moment, requiring prompt and decisive action. Whether extending an offer or sending a rejection, the process needs to be efficient and professional. Manually generating offer letters and managing rejection communications can be a bottleneck, especially when dealing with multiple candidates.

With automation, this critical step can be executed with a single click. By updating a candidate's "final status" in an Airtable base, the system can automatically generate a customized offer letter via a tool like Pandadoc and send it to the successful candidate. For those not selected, a personalized rejection email can be sent, and they can even be added to a Mailchimp list for future job opportunities, maintaining a positive relationship.

Kicking Off the Onboarding Process

Once a candidate accepts an offer, the onboarding journey begins, involving a flurry of activities to integrate them into the company. This phase is crucial for setting the tone of their employment and ensuring they feel welcomed and prepared. However, it often entails a multitude of administrative tasks, from setting up HR profiles to requesting IT credentials and scheduling initial training.

While there's a lot to accomplish, these tasks don't have to be time-consuming. Automation can significantly streamline the onboarding process, transforming it from a manual chore into a smooth, integrated experience. This section explores how to automate key onboarding steps, ensuring new hires are set up for success from day one without burdening your HR team.

1. Get the Ball Rolling with Onboarding After a Candidate Sends You Their Signed Offer Letter

The moment a signed offer letter is received, a cascade of onboarding tasks typically begins. This includes creating an employee profile in HR software, submitting requests to IT for login credentials, and scheduling initial training sessions. Traditionally, these steps are manual and sequential, leading to potential delays and a less-than-ideal first impression for the new hire.

An automated workflow can kickstart all these processes instantly. By simply ticking a "Signed contract received" box in an Airtable base, the system can automatically create the new employee's profile in BambooHR, generate an IT service ticket in Freshdesk for credential requests, and email a Calendly link to the employee for training scheduling. This integrated approach ensures a swift and seamless transition for new team members.

2. Add New Employees to an Internal Email List

Internal newsletters are a vital tool for keeping employees informed about company strategy, updates, and culture. However, manually adding each new hire to various internal email lists can be a cumbersome and error-prone process. This administrative overhead can lead to new employees missing important communications or HR spending unnecessary time on data entry.

Automation provides a simple and effective solution to this challenge. By integrating your HR software, such as BambooHR, with your email marketing platform like Mailchimp, you can ensure that every new employee is automatically added to the relevant internal email lists as soon as their profile is created. This guarantees that all new hires receive essential company communications from their first day, fostering better engagement and reducing manual work for HR.

Making Sure Everyone Feels Heard When It Comes to Their Employee Benefits

An employee-centric company culture thrives on ensuring that every team member feels heard and valued, especially concerning their benefits and overall well-being. However, relying on traditional methods like email for benefit requests can easily lead to oversights, causing frustration and making employees feel overlooked. The sheer volume of requests can overwhelm HR, leading to delays and missed opportunities to support staff.

Implementing automation in benefits management can transform this experience, guaranteeing that no request goes unnoticed. By creating structured, automated workflows, HR can efficiently collect, track, and respond to employee needs, reinforcing a supportive and responsive workplace environment. This section explores how automation can streamline benefit requests and approvals, ensuring every employee's voice is heard.

1. Collect Employee Benefit Requests

For a company committed to being employee-centric, it's paramount that staff feel their voices are heard, particularly regarding their benefits and overall value within the organization. Yet, asking employees to submit benefit requests via email is a recipe for disaster; many requests can easily get lost or delayed, leading to employees feeling ignored and undervalued.

An automated system can completely eliminate this issue. By setting up a Typeform for employees to submit their benefit requests, the information can be automatically copied into a centralized Airtable base. Simultaneously, a Slack message can be sent to the HR professional responsible for handling these requests. This ensures that every single request is captured, tracked, and promptly brought to the attention of the right person, guaranteeing that no employee's needs are overlooked.

2. Approve or Decline Employee Benefit Requests

Reviewing and responding to employee benefit requests can often involve frustrating back-and-forths between employees and HR, leading to delays and dissatisfaction. The manual process of communicating approvals or rejections, and then initiating the next steps, can be inefficient and time-consuming for both parties.

Automation can significantly streamline this critical process. Using the same Airtable base where benefit requests are collected, HR can simply update the "Request status" field to "Approved" or "Declined." If approved, the system can automatically email the relevant person to initiate the benefit. If declined, an automated email can be sent to the employee, clearly communicating the status. This straightforward solution eliminates manual communication, reduces delays, and ensures transparency in the benefit request process.

3. Capture Time-Off Requests and Send Slack Messages About Them to Managers

Effectively managing time-off requests is a top priority for any manager, recognizing that employees need to recharge to maintain productivity and well-being. However, many HR platforms rely on email notifications for time-off requests, which can easily get lost in a manager's already crowded inbox. This often leads to delays in approvals and potential miscommunication, impacting both employee morale and operational planning.

An automated workflow can ensure that time-off requests cut through the digital noise. When an employee submits a time-off request through a system like BambooHR, the automation can instantly copy the details to a Google Sheet for centralized tracking. More importantly, it can send a direct Slack message to the employee's manager, ensuring immediate visibility and prompt action. This eliminates the need for managers to constantly check emails, preventing delays and improving coordination between HR, managers, and employees.

Pro tip: Integrate your time-off request automation with a shared team calendar. Once a request is approved, automatically add it to the team's calendar to ensure everyone is aware of absences, improving team planning and reducing scheduling conflicts.

Streamlining Promotions

Tracking and managing employee promotions and departmental changes are crucial for maintaining accurate records, ensuring fair compensation, and supporting career development. Without a streamlined process, these changes can lead to administrative bottlenecks, delays in salary adjustments, and potential miscommunication within the organization. Manual handling of such sensitive information also increases the risk of errors and inconsistencies.

Automation offers a robust solution to simplify the entire promotion workflow, from initial submission to final salary updates. By integrating various tools, HR can ensure that every promotion is recorded, acted upon, and communicated efficiently, fostering transparency and employee satisfaction. This section details how automation can make your promotion processes seamless and error-free.

4. Inform HR About an Employee’s Promotion or Department Change

Keeping meticulous records of every promotion or lateral move within the company is essential for HR. These changes impact compensation, reporting structures, and career paths, making accurate and timely documentation critical. Relying on manual updates can lead to inconsistencies and delays, affecting both employee morale and organizational data integrity.

An automated workflow can ensure HR is immediately informed and can act on these changes. When managers submit employee promotions or department changes via a Typeform, the automation can instantly copy this information into an Airtable base for secure record-keeping. Subsequently, it can create a task in project management software like ClickUp, assigning it to the relevant HR team member. Finally, a Slack message can be sent to ensure the HR team doesn't miss this important update. This approach ensures transparency, accuracy, and prompt action for all employee advancements.

5. Email Finance to Update an Employee’s Salary

A promotion brings excitement, not just for a new job title and responsibilities, but also for the anticipated salary increase. It's crucial to ensure that this financial adjustment is processed swiftly to maintain employee satisfaction and avoid any negative impact on their experience. Delays in updating salaries can quickly dampen the enthusiasm of a newly promoted employee.

Automation can guarantee that salary updates are handled as quickly as possible. Building on the previous scenario, once the status of the relevant ClickUp task (created for the promotion) is updated to "completed" or "approved," the automation can automatically trigger an email to the finance department. This email would contain all the necessary details for the salary adjustment, ensuring that the employee's first paycheck in their new role accurately reflects their promotion. This seamless integration between HR and finance eliminates manual communication and ensures timely compensation adjustments.

Pro tip: Implement a two-step approval process for salary updates within your automation. After HR approves, require a second approval from a finance manager via a quick notification (e.g., Slack or email) before the final email to the payroll system is sent. This adds a layer of verification and reduces potential errors.

Completing the Offboarding Process on Time

The offboarding process, while often overlooked, is as critical as onboarding. It provides valuable insights for improving company culture and employee retention, and ensures a smooth transition for departing employees. An inefficient offboarding process can lead to missed feedback opportunities, security vulnerabilities, and a negative lasting impression of the company.

Automating key aspects of offboarding ensures that all necessary steps are completed promptly and consistently. From scheduling exit interviews to revoking system access, automation can streamline these sensitive processes, protecting company assets and gathering crucial data. This section outlines how automation can optimize your offboarding procedures, making them more effective and secure.

1. Send a Calendly Invite to Conduct an Exit Interview with an Employee Who Has Resigned

Exit interviews are invaluable tools for identifying organizational blind spots and understanding why employees choose to leave. This feedback is essential for improving employee treatment and retention strategies. However, manually scheduling these interviews can be challenging, especially when managing multiple departures.

Automation can simplify the process of inviting resigning employees to exit interviews. By simply ticking a "Send exit interview invite" field in an Airtable base, the system can automatically generate a Calendly link and email it directly to the departing employee. While not every employee may respond, those who do will provide critical insights that can help the company evolve and improve its workplace environment. This automated approach ensures that the opportunity for valuable feedback is consistently offered.

2. Email IT to Remove an Employee’s Access to Your Systems After They Resign

Once an employee's exit interview is complete and their departure is official, it is absolutely critical to immediately revoke their access to all company systems. Failure to do so poses significant security risks and can compromise sensitive data. Manually coordinating with the IT department for each departure can be slow and prone to human error, leaving potential vulnerabilities open.

Automation provides a swift and secure solution for this essential offboarding step. By simply ticking a "Remove all access" field in the same Airtable base used for exit interviews, the system can automatically send a detailed email to the IT department. This email would clearly instruct them to remove the former employee's access to all relevant systems, ensuring that security protocols are followed without delay. This automated process protects company assets and streamlines a crucial administrative task.

Pro tip: Create a standardized offboarding checklist within your automation platform. This checklist can trigger tasks for various departments (IT, HR, Finance) and track their completion, ensuring no critical step is missed during an employee's departure.

A Quick Intro to How Make Works

Make is a visual automation platform that enables you to connect applications and automate tasks seamlessly. It operates by linking various "app modules" together to form "scenarios." These scenarios are essentially automated workflows that collect, transfer, and transform data between your chosen applications.

Creating a scenario in Make can be done in two primary ways: either by building it from scratch, which involves manually selecting and configuring each module, or by utilizing pre-built templates. For those looking for quick results and an easy entry into automation, templates are the ideal starting point.

When using a template, the core structure of the automation is already in place. Your main tasks will involve connecting your existing app accounts (like Gmail or Google Drive) to Make and making minor adjustments to the scenario to fit your specific needs. Once configured, you can activate the scenario, and your automation will begin running automatically.

It's important to note that all the necessary components—apps, modules, and templates—are integrated directly within the Make platform. You won't need to open external applications or switch between different tools to set up your automations, ensuring a streamlined and user-friendly experience.

Automatically Forward Emails in Gmail

Automating email forwarding in Gmail is a highly practical solution that can save significant time and effort in numerous professional and personal contexts. This Make.com template simplifies the process, allowing you to set up automatic forwarding based on specific criteria.

A Make.com scenario illustrating automatic Gmail email forwarding.

Consider two common scenarios where this automation proves invaluable. First, for companies with active blogs, guest post proposals are frequent but often land in general inboxes, requiring manual forwarding to the relevant content team. This repetitive task can be eliminated by automating the process. Second, for couples house hunting, receiving Zillow email alerts to one partner's inbox and then manually forwarding them to the other can be tedious. Automated forwarding ensures both partners receive critical updates simultaneously.

To set up this template, navigate to the Make.com template page for "Automatically forward emails in Gmail" and click "Create new scenario from template." You'll be redirected to your Make.com dashboard, where the template is pre-configured to forward emails containing specific keywords to a chosen recipient.

In the initial Gmail module, you'll connect your Gmail account and select the folder you wish to monitor. Following this, you'll configure the filter positioned between the two Gmail modules. For instance, to forward guest post proposals, you would set the filter to detect the phrase "guest post" within incoming emails. Finally, specify the recipient's email address in the second Gmail module and activate the scenario. Your automation will then be live, ensuring relevant emails are forwarded without manual intervention.

Connecting your Gmail account within a Make.com module.

Pro tip: You can easily modify the filter conditions to suit various needs. For example, instead of filtering by email content, you can configure the filter to watch for specific senders. This is particularly useful for automatically forwarding newsletters or alerts from known sources, like Zillow, to another email address. Simply adjust the filter settings to target the sender's email address instead of keywords in the message body.

Once you've successfully implemented this, you've officially taken your first step into automation. This simple yet powerful project demonstrates the ease and effectiveness of no-code solutions. Now, let's move on to another practical automation that helps organize your digital workspace.

Automatically Save Emails and Attachments to Google Sheets and Google Drive

Manually sifting through your inbox to find specific emails or attachments can be a tedious and time-consuming task. This Make.com template offers an elegant solution by automating the process of storing incoming emails and their attachments into organized Google Sheets and Google Drive folders. This automation is particularly beneficial for anyone who frequently exchanges files and messages via email and needs a structured way to archive them.

A Make.com scenario integrating Gmail, Google Drive, and Google Sheets.

This solution is highly relevant for a wide range of professionals. For instance, lawyers building case files, accountants managing balance sheets for multiple clients, or financial officials conducting due diligence can all benefit immensely from having emails and attachments automatically categorized and saved. It eliminates the need for manual data entry and ensures that all relevant documents are easily accessible in their designated locations.

To deploy this automation, simply visit the template page for "Save Gmail emails and attachments to Google Sheets and Google Drive" on Make.com and click "Create new scenario from template." Once in your Make.com dashboard, you'll need to authorize Make to connect with your Google account if you haven't already. Then, configure the modules by specifying the Google Drive folder where attachments should be saved and the Google Sheet where email details will be logged.

This setup ensures that every time a new email arrives with an attachment, Make.com will automatically extract the attachment and save it to your specified Google Drive folder, while simultaneously recording key email details (like sender, subject, and date) into a Google Sheet. This not only keeps your files organized but also creates a searchable log of your communications, significantly boosting your productivity and reducing administrative overhead.

Automatically Send the Daily Weather Forecast to Your Email, or to the Recipients You Wish

For many, checking the daily weather forecast is a routine that influences everything from wardrobe choices to daily planning. This Make.com template allows you to automate the delivery of daily weather updates directly to your email inbox or to any specified recipient, transforming a simple habit into a powerful, proactive tool.

An automated weather updates email scenario in Make.com.

Beyond personal convenience, automated weather notifications have significant practical applications. For logistics companies, accurate and timely weather forecasts are crucial for adjusting delivery schedules and ensuring driver safety, especially when transporting cargo across diverse geographical regions. Similarly, for individuals with family members living in areas prone to severe weather, such as hurricane zones, receiving automated alerts can provide peace of mind and enable proactive support in emergencies.

To set up this automation, navigate to the "Automated daily weather forecasts" template on Make.com and click "Create new scenario from template." Once in your Make.com dashboard, the first step is to specify the location for which you want to receive weather updates. You can do this by entering the city name or its geographical coordinates.

Selecting the location for your automated weather forecasts.

After defining the location, you'll specify the email recipient(s) for the forecasts. This can be your own email address, or you can send it to others, such as a truck driver, an employee who works outdoors, or a distant family member. The recipient will receive a comprehensive weather report, including temperature, humidity, wind speed, and potential snowfall, among other data points.

Configuring the email recipient for weather notifications.

While the template provides a full weather report by default, you have the option to customize the information sent. By clicking the "map" button within the email module, you can selectively remove any data points you don't wish to include, tailoring the notifications to your precise needs. This level of customization ensures that the automated forecasts are both informative and concise.

Customizing the weather data points to be included in the notification.

Pro tip: If you need to receive weather forecasts for multiple locations, you can easily clone the scenario. Navigate to your Make.com dashboard, find the weather forecast scenario, and use the dropdown menu to clone it. You can then modify each cloned scenario to monitor a different location, providing comprehensive weather coverage without having to build each automation from scratch.

Cloning a scenario from the Make.com dashboard.

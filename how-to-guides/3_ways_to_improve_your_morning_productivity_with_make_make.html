Exercise

Starting your day with a burst of physical activity is an excellent way to boost energy and focus. However, consistently tracking your workouts can sometimes feel like another chore. To truly improve your fitness, monitoring your progress is key, and thankfully, there's an automation solution for that.

Imagine finishing your run or cycling session and having all your performance data automatically recorded without lifting a finger. This eliminates the need to manually input details into a spreadsheet or fitness log, saving you time and ensuring accuracy. With Make.com, you can connect your favorite fitness apps to other productivity tools, creating a seamless data flow.

Make.com scenario showing Strava and Google Sheets integration.

For instance, every time you complete a training session recorded on Strava, the detailed information about your results can be automatically added to a Google Sheets spreadsheet. This integration ensures that all your workout data, such as distance, duration, and calories burned, is meticulously tracked and organized. This hands-free approach allows you to concentrate on your fitness goals rather than the administrative tasks associated with them.

Setting up this type of automation is straightforward with Make.com's intuitive interface. You can use pre-built templates to quickly establish the connection between Strava and Google Sheets, or customize it to fit your specific tracking needs. This way, your morning exercise routine contributes directly to your long-term fitness goals with minimal effort.

Journaling

For many, journaling is a powerful morning ritual that helps to clear the mind, set intentions, and foster creativity. It can be a space to outline daily goals, reflect on thoughts, or simply engage in free-flowing writing. However, the act of journaling can sometimes feel disconnected from the actionable tasks of the day, especially if your notes remain isolated in a single app.

To bridge this gap, Make.com offers a solution that transforms your reflective thoughts into concrete actions. By integrating your note-taking app with a task management system, you can ensure that your morning insights directly contribute to your daily productivity. This means your ideas don't just stay on the page; they become part of your workflow.

Make.com scenario showing Evernote and Trello integration.

Consider using a note-taking app like Evernote to capture your morning thoughts. With Make.com, you can set up an automation that automatically sends new notes from Evernote to a task management system like Trello. This integration can create new cards in Trello based on your notes, and even convert any tags you've used in Evernote into Trello labels. This ensures that your journal entries are not only recorded but also organized into actionable tasks.

This scenario is particularly useful for turning ideas into to-do lists, project tasks, or reminders. Make.com provides easy-to-use templates to get started with this integration, and you can adapt it to various other note-taking and task management tools. This way, your journaling becomes a dynamic part of your productivity system, helping you stay organized and focused throughout the day.

Pro tip: Customize your journaling automation to include specific keywords that trigger different actions. For example, a note tagged "urgent" could create a high-priority task in Trello, while a note tagged "idea" could go to a dedicated brainstorming board.

Reading

Many individuals dedicate a portion of their morning to reading, whether it's catching up on current events, staying informed about industry trends, or delving into personal development content. This practice can significantly contribute to both professional and personal growth, helping to focus your mind and broaden your knowledge. However, the digital age often presents a challenge: an overwhelming influx of information from countless news feeds and subscriptions.

Sifting through irrelevant articles to find genuinely valuable content can be time-consuming and counterproductive. This is where automation with Make.com can revolutionize your morning reading experience. Instead of manually checking multiple sources, you can set up a system that delivers precisely what you want, directly to you.

Make.com scenario showing RSS feed to email integration.

With a simple integration, you can establish parameters around an RSS feed to filter content based on specific keywords. This means that only articles containing your chosen terms will be processed. Once a relevant post is identified, Make.com will automatically send it directly to your inbox, ensuring you receive curated content without any manual effort.

This automation is incredibly efficient for staying on top of niche topics, industry news, or personal interests without getting bogged down by general information. Make.com provides step-by-step templates to help you configure this scenario, allowing you to customize your RSS feed filters and email delivery preferences. By automating your morning reading, you can ensure that your valuable time is spent absorbing meaningful content, preparing you for the day ahead with relevant insights.

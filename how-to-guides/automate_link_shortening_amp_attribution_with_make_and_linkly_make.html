In the fast-paced world of e-commerce, every new product launch triggers a flurry of marketing activities. Teams rush to create social media posts, email campaigns, and paid advertisements. However, a significant bottleneck often arises during this process: manual link tagging. This seemingly small task can quickly become a major drain on resources and a source of costly errors.

Manually generating UTM (Urchin Tracking Module) parameters for each link, copying them into a shortener, and then meticulously recording them in a spreadsheet is not only time-consuming but also highly prone to human error. Inconsistent tagging across different campaigns, channels, or even team members can lead to flawed attribution data, causing businesses to make poor decisions about where to allocate their marketing budget.

This comprehensive guide will walk you through building a "zero-touch" attribution system using Make.com and Linkly. By the end of this tutorial, your Shopify store will automatically generate branded, trackable short links with perfectly configured UTM parameters the moment a new product is created. These links will then be instantly shared to your chosen social media platforms, all without any manual intervention, ensuring flawless data and maximum efficiency.

The 4-step Zero-Touch Automation Workflow

This automation is designed to be a "set it and forget it" system, ensuring that every new product launch is accompanied by perfectly tracked, branded links and immediate social media promotion. Follow these four steps to build your zero-touch attribution workflow.

Each step leverages a specific module within Make.com to achieve a part of the overall automation, from detecting new products to publishing them on social media.

Step 1: Watch for new products in Shopify (the trigger)

The first and most crucial step in our automation is to detect when a new product is added to your Shopify store. This event will act as the trigger that initiates the entire workflow.

In Make.com, you will use the Shopify module configured to "Watch Products." This module constantly monitors your Shopify store for any new product entries, ensuring that your automation is always responsive to your latest inventory updates.

  • Role: This module functions as the "ears" of your automation, continuously monitoring your Shopify product catalog for new additions.
  • Setup Tip: For initial testing, set the "Limit" parameter to 1. This ensures that only the most recently created product is processed, making it easier to debug and verify the scenario's functionality without processing your entire product catalog.
Configuring the Shopify "Watch Products" module in Make.com.

Step 2: Construct the full product URL (the foundation)

Shopify's API typically provides a unique "product handle" (e.g., blue-suede-shoes) rather than the complete, live URL of the product page. To create a functional and trackable link, we need to construct the full destination URL.

This step ensures that Linkly receives a valid and complete URL to shorten and apply attribution parameters to, pointing directly to the correct product page on your store.

  • Module: Utilize the "Tools > Set Variable" module in Make.com.
  • Action: Create a new variable, which we can name ProductURL. This variable will be formed by appending the product's handle (dynamically pulled from the Shopify trigger) to your store's standard product base URL (e.g., https://yourstorename.com/products/ + [Product Handle]). This concatenation guarantees that Linkly always receives the accurate, live product page URL.
Setting up the "Set Variable" module to construct the full product URL.

Step 3: Create the trackable link with Linkly (the heavy lifting)

This is the core of your attribution system, where the full product URL is transformed into a branded, trackable short link with all necessary UTM parameters embedded. This ensures that every click can be accurately attributed to its source.

You will add the Linkly module to your Make.com scenario and configure it to create a new link, dynamically pulling the URL and applying your desired tracking information.

  • Destination URL: Map the ProductURL variable that you created in the previous step directly into this field. This tells Linkly which long URL to shorten and track.
  • Crucial: Adding UTM Parameters: Leverage Linkly’s robust settings to dynamically map your desired UTM parameters. For a Facebook post, a best-practice structure might include:
    • UTM Source: Facebook
    • UTM Medium: social_post
    • UTM Campaign: Dynamically map the product title from Shopify (e.g., [Product Title]).
    • Branded Domain: Select your custom branded domain (e.g., brand.link) for maximum click-through rates and brand consistency.
Configuring Linkly to create a trackable link with UTM parameters.

Pro tip: Always use a consistent naming convention for your UTM parameters across all campaigns. This makes data analysis much easier and more reliable in the long run. Consider creating a standardized template for your UTMs to ensure uniformity.

Step 4: Share to social media (the final touch)

The final step in this zero-touch workflow is the immediate distribution of your newly created, branded, and tracked short link to your social media channels. This ensures that your new product gets instant visibility and that your marketing efforts are launched without delay.

In Make.com, you will integrate the Facebook Pages module to automatically create a post, leveraging the output from the Linkly module.

  • Action: Map the "Full URL" output from the Linkly module directly into the post message field of the Facebook Pages module. You can also dynamically map the Shopify Product Title and a short description to create a rich, informative, and engaging social media post.

With this step configured, the moment you click "Save" on a new product in Shopify, your fully tracked, branded short link will be live, and your social media post will be automatically published. This completes the zero-touch attribution system, allowing you to focus on other strategic tasks.

Setting up the Facebook Pages module to automatically post the new product link.

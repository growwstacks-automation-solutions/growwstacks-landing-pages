Solution overview: Calculating the cost of meetings automatically

The automated meeting cost tracker we will construct in this tutorial is designed for simplicity and efficiency. Its core functionality revolves around three main steps, ensuring you receive timely and accurate cost breakdowns for your meetings.

First, the system automatically retrieves a list of all your scheduled meetings for the day directly from your Google Calendar. This eliminates the need for manual input of meeting details. Second, for each meeting identified, it calculates the associated cost, focusing specifically on meetings you have organized. Finally, it compiles this information and sends you a concise notification via Slack, detailing each meeting and its calculated expense.

As an added benefit, the Slack message will also include convenient links to cancel meetings. This feature is particularly useful if, after reviewing the cost, you determine that a meeting is not essential and that an email or alternative communication method would be more efficient. This proactive approach helps you manage your time and resources more effectively.

An example of the Slack notification detailing meeting costs and cancellation links.

Building this meeting cost tracker is a straightforward process that requires no complex coding. Beyond your existing Slack and Google accounts, all you need is a Make.com account. Once you have these prerequisites, you'll be ready to start building your automated solution and gain clearer insights into your meeting expenditures.

1. Create a new Make scenario and add the Google Calendar app

To begin building your automated meeting cost calculator, the first step is to create a new scenario within Make.com. Once your new scenario is open, you'll need to add the "Google Calendar" app, specifically selecting the "Search Events" module. This module will serve as the starting point for retrieving all your scheduled meetings.

After adding the module, connect it to your Google account to grant Make.com the necessary permissions to access your calendar data. Next, configure the module with the following settings to ensure it fetches the correct meetings: specify the Calendar ID that contains your meetings, set the Start Date to {{formatDate(now; "DD.MM.YYYY")}} to capture today's meetings, and set the End Date to {{formatDate(addDays(now; 1); "DD.MM.YYYY")}} to include meetings up to tomorrow. Ensure "Single Events" is set to "Yes" to process recurring meetings individually, and set a "Limit" (e.g., "20") that exceeds your usual number of daily meetings.

Configuring the Google Calendar "Search Events" module to fetch daily meetings.

These settings ensure that the module accurately identifies and retrieves all relevant meetings from your calendar. Once you have configured these parameters, click "OK" to save the module settings and proceed to the next stage of building your meeting cost calculator.

2. Add the Tools module to set up meeting information

To accurately calculate the cost of your meetings, the tracker needs specific pieces of information: the duration of each meeting in hours, your email address (to identify meetings you've organized), and an hourly rate for attendees. For simplicity in this tutorial, we'll use a single average hourly rate for all participants, which also helps maintain privacy by not exposing individual salaries.

To input this data into your Make scenario, you'll need to add a "Tools" module, specifically the "Set multiple variables" option. This module allows you to define several variables that the scenario will use throughout the calculation process. After adding the module, you'll configure each variable one by one.

First, click "Add item" to create a new variable. Name this variable "duration" and for its value, use the formula {{(1.end - 1.start) / 3600000}}. This expression calculates the meeting's duration by subtracting the start time from the end time, converting the result from milliseconds to hours. It's crucial to use Make's built-in math operators and parentheses for this calculation to function correctly.

Defining the 'duration' variable using a formula to convert milliseconds to hours.

Next, add two more items to the "Set multiple variables" module. Create a variable named "hourlyrate" and input a numerical value representing the average hourly rate (e.g., "70" for $70/hour), without any currency symbols. Finally, create a variable named "myemail" and enter your full email address. This email will be used later to filter for meetings you have organized.

All three variables ('duration', 'hourlyrate', 'myemail') configured within the "Set multiple variables" module.

Once all three variables are correctly set up, click "OK" to save the configuration for this module. These variables will now be accessible throughout your Make scenario, enabling subsequent calculations and filtering logic.

3. Add a second Tools module to calculate the cost of each meeting

With the necessary meeting information defined, the next step is to calculate the actual cost of each meeting. For this, you will add another "Tools" module to your scenario, this time selecting "Set variable." This module will compute the total cost based on the number of attendees, the meeting's duration, and the hourly rate you previously set.

Configure this new "Set variable" module by naming the variable "cost." For the "Variable value," input the following formula: {{formatNumber(length(1.attendees) * 4.meetingDuration * 4.hourlyRate; 2; "."; ",")}}. This formula performs several key operations: it counts the number of attendees from the Google Calendar module, multiplies this count by the meeting's duration (from your 'duration' variable) and the 'hourlyrate' variable, and then formats the final cost to two decimal places for clarity.

The 'cost' variable is defined using a formula that calculates total meeting expense.

This variable is crucial as it consolidates all the relevant data points into a single, actionable figure. After ensuring the formula is correctly entered and the variable is named "cost," click "OK" to finalize the module's setup. You are now ready to filter these calculations to focus only on meetings you have organized.

4. Have the tracker calculate meeting costs for only meetings you’ve organized

While our tracker can now calculate the cost for any meeting you attend, its primary utility lies in managing meetings you have the authority to modify or cancel. Therefore, we need to refine the scenario to process costs exclusively for meetings that you have organized. This ensures that the generated reports are relevant to your direct sphere of influence.

To implement this filtering, click on the small wrench icon located between the two "Tools" modules in your Make scenario. This action will allow you to "Set up a filter." Give your filter a clear and descriptive name, such as "Proceed for only meetings we’ve organized," to easily understand its purpose within the workflow.

Within the filter configuration, you will set up a condition to compare the meeting organizer's email with your own. Map the "Organizer: Email" item from your Google Calendar module to the first operator of the filter condition. Then, change the condition setting to "Boolean operators" and select "Equal to." Finally, map the "myemail" variable, which you defined in the first "Tools" module, to the second operator of the filter condition.

The filter ensures that only meetings organized by you proceed to cost calculation.

This filter acts as a gatekeeper: if the organizer's email for a particular meeting matches your specified email address, the scenario will proceed to calculate its cost. If there is no match, the meeting will be ignored, preventing irrelevant cost data from cluttering your reports. Once the filter is correctly configured, click "OK" to apply these changes and move forward with compiling your meeting list.

5. Compile the list of meetings and their respective costs

At this point, your meeting cost tracker is capable of calculating the individual cost for each meeting you've organized. The next logical step is to consolidate this information into a neatly formatted list that can be easily reviewed. To achieve this, you'll add a "Tools" module, specifically the "Text aggregator" option, to your Make scenario.

After adding the "Text aggregator" module, enable its "Show advanced settings" switch to access all configuration options. Set the "Source Module" to "Google Calendar - Search Events [1]," indicating that the aggregator should process data from your initial calendar search. For the "Row separator," select "New row" to ensure each meeting appears on a separate line in the final list. Crucially, check the "Yes" checkbox for "Stop processing after an empty aggregation" to prevent the scenario from running unnecessarily if no meetings are found for the day.

In the "Text" field, you will define the format of each line in your aggregated list. Copy and paste the following string: • *{{1.summary}}:* ${{3.cost}} | <{{1.htmlLink}}|Cancel this meeting>. This template will generate a line for each meeting, displaying its name (summary), its calculated cost, and a direct link to cancel it. Note that this format uses Slack markup, so if you plan to use a different messaging app, you may need to adjust the formatting accordingly.

The Text aggregator module compiles meeting details, costs, and cancellation links into a single formatted output.

Once you've configured the "Text aggregator" module with these settings, click "OK." This module will now efficiently compile all the relevant meeting data into a single, readable text block, preparing it for delivery in the final step of your automation.

6. Send the list of meetings to yourself via Slack

The final step in building your automated meeting cost calculator is to deliver the compiled list of meetings and their costs to you. For this, we'll use the "Slack" app, specifically the "Create a Message" module. While this tutorial focuses on Slack, feel free to substitute it with any other team messaging app supported by Make.com that suits your workflow.

Add the "Slack" > "Create a Message" module to your scenario and connect it to your Slack account, granting the necessary permissions. Next, specify the Slack channel where you wish to receive these daily meeting cost notifications. This could be a private channel, a direct message to yourself, or a team channel dedicated to productivity insights.

In the "Text" field of the Slack module, compose the full message you want to receive. Crucially, insert the "text" item from your "Text aggregator" module into the part of the message where you want the list of meetings to appear. This dynamic element will populate your Slack message with the formatted list of meetings, their costs, and cancellation links.

The Slack module is configured to send the aggregated meeting cost list to a specified channel.

To enhance readability and prevent clutter, enable the "Show advanced settings" switch and change the "Unfurl primarily text-based content" setting to "No." This will disable automatic link previews for each meeting, keeping your message concise. Optionally, you can also customize the message's icon emoji and username within these advanced settings to personalize your notifications. Once configured, click "OK" to complete the module setup.

The complete Make scenario, from Google Calendar to Slack notification.

Now, test your scenario by clicking the "Run once" button at the bottom left of the scenario builder. You should receive a Slack message containing the list of meetings you've organized for the day, their calculated costs, and direct cancellation links. If everything works as expected, schedule your scenario to run automatically each morning on workdays (or daily, depending on your needs) and activate it. Your automated meeting cost tracker is now fully operational!

Meeting time is money, so make it count

The primary purpose of implementing this automated meeting cost tracker is not to encourage the cancellation of all your meetings. Instead, it serves as a powerful awareness tool. By providing a clear, quantifiable cost for each meeting, it empowers you to make more informed decisions about which meetings truly warrant the time and resources invested in them.

When you understand the financial implications, you can thoughtfully prioritize meetings that promise a significant return on investment. This might mean shortening some sessions, refining agendas, or ensuring that only essential personnel attend. For meetings that you determine could be handled more efficiently through alternative communication methods, such as email, the tracker provides the data to support that decision.

Ultimately, this automation benefits everyone. Your team members will appreciate the reduction in unnecessary meetings, freeing them up for more productive work, while your superiors will recognize the tangible cost savings and improved operational efficiency. These are the core advantages of integrating an automated meeting cost calculator into your workflow.

What This Workflow Does

This powerful automation workflow connects your Google Drive with CloudConvert via Make.com to effortlessly transform your Google Sheets files into various formats, such as PDF, and then save them back to your Google Drive. Imagine no longer needing to manually open each spreadsheet, convert it, and then re-upload it. This scenario monitors a specified folder in Google Drive for new Google Sheets files, automatically triggers the conversion process using CloudConvert, and places the newly formatted document into a designated destination folder.

For businesses, this means a significant reduction in repetitive administrative tasks. Whether you need to generate professional PDF reports from financial data, convert data exports for compatibility with other software, or simply archive documents in a standardized format, this workflow handles it all in the background. It ensures consistency, saves valuable employee time, and minimizes the potential for human error in document handling.

How It Works

The process is broken down into three simple, automated steps within your Make.com scenario:

Step 1: Watch for New Google Sheets Files in Google Drive

The workflow begins by constantly monitoring a specific folder in your Google Drive. Whenever a new Google Sheets file is added to this folder, the automation is triggered. You configure this module to specifically look for Google Sheets files, ensuring that only relevant documents initiate the conversion process.

Configuring the Google Drive module to watch for new Google Sheets files.

Step 2: Convert the File Using CloudConvert

Once a new Google Sheets file is detected, it's passed to the CloudConvert module. Here, you'll use your CloudConvert API key to authenticate the service. The module takes the Google Sheet as the source file, identifies its format (typically 'xlsx' for Google Sheets), and then converts it to your chosen output format, such as PDF. The new file name is dynamically generated by taking the original title and appending the new file extension.

Setting up the CloudConvert module for file conversion.
Specifying input and output formats within CloudConvert.

Step 3: Upload the Converted File Back to Google Drive

Finally, the converted file (e.g., the PDF version of your Google Sheet) is taken from CloudConvert and uploaded to a specified destination folder in your Google Drive. This ensures that your converted documents are organized and easily accessible alongside your other files. This step completes the automated cycle, delivering the final document without any manual effort on your part.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to quickly set up your Google Sheets conversion workflow:

  1. Create a New Scenario in Make.com: Log in to your Make.com account and create a new scenario.
  2. Add Google Drive "Watch files in folder" Module:
    • Select Google Drive as the app and choose the "Watch files in folder" module.
    • Connect your Google Drive account.
    • Select the folder where you will upload your Google Sheets files.
    • Under "What files to watch," select "Google Sheets" from the dropdown.
  3. Add CloudConvert "Convert a file" Module:
    • Add a CloudConvert module and select "Convert a file."
    • Connect your CloudConvert account using your API key (found in your CloudConvert dashboard under API V2).
    • For "Input file," select "Upload a file" and map the "Source file" from the Google Drive module.
    • Choose "I want to select the input format" and set "Input format" to "xlsx".
    • Set "Output format" to "PDF" (or your desired format).
    • Map the "Title" from the Google Drive module and append ".pdf" (or the appropriate extension for your chosen output format).
    • Select "Download a file" under "Export options."
  4. Add Google Drive "Upload a file" Module:
    • Add another Google Drive module and select "Upload a file."
    • Select the destination folder in Google Drive where you want the converted files to be saved.
    • Under "Source File," map the output from the CloudConvert module.
  5. Test and Activate: Run the scenario once with a test Google Sheet to ensure everything works correctly. Once confirmed, activate your scenario.

Pro tip: Create separate "Input" and "Output" folders in your Google Drive to keep your original and converted files organized. This makes managing your documents much easier.

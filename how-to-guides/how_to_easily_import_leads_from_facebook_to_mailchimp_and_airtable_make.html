Step 1: Creating the Make Scenario

To begin, log into your Make.com dashboard. Once logged in, locate and click the prominent "Create a new scenario" button, typically found in the top right corner of your screen. This action will initiate the process of building your new automation workflow.

Starting a new automation scenario in Make.com.

On the subsequent screen, you'll need to search for the "Facebook Lead Ads" application. Once found, select it and then click the "Continue" button to proceed. This will add the Facebook Lead Ads module as the starting point of your scenario.

Locating and selecting the Facebook Lead Ads app.

You will then be directed to the scenario builder interface, which is where you'll visually construct your automation. Here, you'll see a list of available Facebook Lead Ads modules, each designed to perform a distinct action. Since our objective is to capture new leads as they come in, you should choose the "New lead" module.

This particular module operates using a webhook, which enables it to instantly receive new lead information the moment a user submits your Facebook Lead Ad form. This real-time data capture is crucial for timely follow-ups and efficient lead nurturing.

To finalize the setup of this module, you must add and configure a webhook. Click the "Add" button within the module's settings. You'll then be prompted to connect your Facebook account and grant Make.com the necessary permissions to access your Facebook pages and lead forms. After successfully connecting, select the specific Facebook page and the lead form from which you wish to pull new leads.

Setting up the webhook to capture new Facebook leads.

For the purpose of this example, our Facebook Lead Ad includes fields for the lead's Name, Email address, and a short question asking them to select a marketing service from a predefined list. The options provided are typically "SEO," "Social Media Marketing," and "Email Marketing."

Our automation scenario is designed to take the answers to these questions, along with the basic lead details, and automatically transfer them to both an Airtable base and a Mailchimp list. This ensures that all relevant lead data is captured and distributed to the appropriate platforms for further action.

Step 2: Adding the Airtable Module

Next, we will integrate the Airtable application into our scenario. This step is crucial for creating a robust backup and management system for your incoming leads.

To add Airtable, simply hover your mouse over the Facebook Lead Ads module in the scenario builder. A small plus sign will appear on its right side. Click this plus sign to open the app selection menu.

Clicking the plus sign to add a new module.

In the pop-up menu that appears, search for "Airtable" and select it. From the list of available Airtable modules, choose "Create a Record." This module is specifically designed to add new entries to your Airtable base each time a new lead is captured.

Choosing the Airtable module to create new records.

Upon selecting the module, a configuration box will appear. Here, you'll need to establish a connection to your Airtable account. This typically involves entering your Airtable API token. If you're unsure how to obtain this token, simply click the '?' icon within the configuration box and follow the provided instructions.

Entering the Airtable API token to establish connection.

Once your Airtable connection is successfully established, you can proceed to configure the rest of the module. This involves selecting the specific Base and Table within your Airtable account where you intend to store the incoming lead data. Ensure that your chosen Airtable base is structured with columns designed to receive the information you'll be pulling from Facebook Lead Ads.

Selecting the target Airtable Base and Table.

In our example, the Airtable base is set up with fields for "Name," "Email address," and "Answer," directly mirroring the fields in our Facebook Lead Ad. The core idea here is to seamlessly transfer the data collected from the Facebook ad into the corresponding fields within your Airtable base.

Matching Airtable fields to Facebook Lead Ad data.

To achieve this data transfer, you need to map the data elements. This means you will select the specific data points from the Facebook module (like "Name" or "Email") and link them to their matching fields in the Airtable module. Once all the necessary fields are mapped, click "OK" to save your configuration. This completes the setup for the Airtable module.

Mapping Facebook Lead Ads data to Airtable fields.

With the Airtable module successfully configured, we can now proceed to add the final module to our automation scenario.

Step 3: Adding the Mailchimp Module

Following the same procedure as with Airtable, add a new module to your scenario by clicking the small plus sign next to the last configured module. In the app search bar, look for "Mailchimp" and then select the "Add/Update a subscriber" module. This action will integrate your email marketing platform into the workflow.

Adding the Mailchimp module to the scenario.

To connect your Mailchimp account to Make.com, click the "Add" button within the module's configuration. You will be prompted to log into your Mailchimp account and grant Make.com the necessary access permissions. This secure connection allows Make to interact with your Mailchimp lists.

Once connected, it's time to configure the Mailchimp module. In the "Enter a List ID" field, choose the "Select from the list" option. Then, from the "List ID" dropdown, select the specific Mailchimp audience or list where you want your new Facebook leads to be added.

You might notice that Mailchimp typically uses separate fields for "First Name" and "Last Name," whereas the data from Facebook Lead Ads often provides the lead's "Full Name" as a single entry. This discrepancy means you'll need to split the full name into its constituent parts.

Fortunately, Make.com offers powerful built-in functions to manipulate data in various ways, including splitting text strings. For this particular task, you will utilize two specific functions: the "Split" function and the "Get" function.

The "Split" function can be found under the "Text and Binary functions" tab. You'll use it in conjunction with the "Space" keyword to identify where the full name should be divided. This function will break the full name into an array of words based on the space character.

The "Get" function, located under the "General functions" tab, will then be used to extract specific elements from the array created by the "Split" function. For example, you can use "Get(array; 1)" to retrieve the first name and "Get(array; 2)" to retrieve the last name.

By combining these two functions, you can effectively map the "Full Name" data element from the Facebook module to the separate "First Name" and "Last Name" fields in the Mailchimp module. Refer to the image below for a visual guide on how to correctly configure this mapping.

After successfully splitting and mapping the name fields, proceed to map the "Email" data element from Facebook to the "Email address" field in Mailchimp. Finally, ensure you tick the option that says "Create a new customer or update an existing one." This setting prevents duplicate entries by updating a subscriber's information if their email already exists in your Mailchimp list, or adding them as a new subscriber if they are not found.

Congratulations! Your scenario is now fully configured and ready for the crucial final step: testing.

Step 4: Testing the Scenario

Since this automation is designed to trigger instantly upon a new form submission, you'll need to perform a specific test to ensure everything is working correctly. This real-time testing approach verifies that your workflow captures and processes leads as expected.

First, within the Make.com scenario builder, click the "Run once" button. This puts your scenario into a listening mode, ready to process the next incoming data. Once the scenario is actively listening, navigate to Facebook and submit a test lead through your designated Lead Ad form. Use sample data that you can easily identify later.

After submitting the form on Facebook, return to your Make.com scenario. The scenario should have executed, and you'll be able to see the flow of data through each module. A successful execution will typically show green checkmarks or indicate that data has passed through each step, similar to the example image provided.

If the execution is successful and the data has flowed as intended, proceed to save your scenario. Locate the "Save" button, usually found at the bottom of the scenario builder, and click it. Saving ensures that all your configurations are preserved.

Finally, to activate your automation and allow it to run continuously, you need to switch the scenario on. Look for the toggle switch in the bottom left corner of the scenario builder. Flip this switch to the "On" position and confirm activation if prompted. Your automation is now live and will automatically process all new Facebook leads.

This automation is now fully operational and will handle the heavy lifting of lead capture and distribution for you. However, we can make it even more sophisticated to handle diverse marketing strategies.

Pro tip: To handle different lead segments based on their form answers, you can use Make.com's Router module. This allows you to split the workflow into multiple paths, directing leads to different Mailchimp lists or Airtable tables based on specific criteria. This is invaluable for highly targeted marketing campaigns.

For instance, you might want to direct leads to different Mailchimp lists based on the specific answers they provide in your Facebook Lead Ad form. We can easily adapt our existing scenario to achieve this level of dynamic lead routing automatically.

To implement this, we'll introduce a native Make.com function called the "Router." A Router module allows you to split a single data stream into multiple routes, ensuring that each lead's data is sent to the appropriate Mailchimp list based on their selections. To begin, you'll need to unlink the existing connection between the Airtable module and the Mailchimp module. Click on the connecting line between these two modules and select the "Unlink" option.

To add a Router, click on the "Tools" menu located at the bottom of the scenario builder. From the list of available tools, select "Router." This will place a new Router module onto your canvas.

Next, connect the Router to the Airtable module, and then connect your existing Mailchimp module to the Router by dragging the Mailchimp module over the Router. Since our example Facebook Lead Ad offers three possible answers, we will need three separate Mailchimp lists. Therefore, clone the Mailchimp module twice (by right-clicking on it and selecting "Clone") so you have three Mailchimp modules in total, and attach all of them to the Router, as illustrated in the provided image.

After setting up the Router and multiple Mailchimp modules, the next crucial step is to add filters to each of the routes originating from the Router. Filters are essential for directing data conditionally. By applying a filter to a route between modules, you ensure that only data meeting specific criteria will pass through to the subsequent module. If the data does not meet the condition, it will be stopped at that filter, preventing it from proceeding down that particular path.

The objective is to ensure that each of the three routes from the Router leads to a Mailchimp module associated with a specific list, corresponding to the option the lead selected in the Facebook ad. For instance, if the ad offered choices like "SEO," "Social Media Marketing," and "Email Marketing," each route would be configured for one of these options. This prevents a single lead from being added to every list, ensuring targeted communication.

For example, if a lead selects "SEO," they should only be added to the "SEO Leads" Mailchimp list. To set up the first filter, click on the top route connecting the Router to one of your Mailchimp modules. A spanner icon will appear, indicating where to access the filter settings. Give this filter a descriptive name, such as "SEO leads."

The "Condition" field is where you define the criteria for your filter. In this case, we'll configure the filter to match "SEO" as the answer from the Facebook Lead Ad. This setup can, of course, be adapted to match any specific answers from your own ad campaigns. To configure the filter, you will need to map the "answer" data element from the Facebook module to the top field in the condition. In the dropdown menu, select the "Contains (Case insensitive)" text operator. Finally, in the bottom field, enter the specific answer you want to match, for example, "SEO."

With this configuration, if the lead's answer contains "SEO" (regardless of capitalization), the data will satisfy the filter's condition and proceed through that route to the corresponding Mailchimp module and list. However, what if a lead enters only "A" or "a" as an abbreviation? You can account for this by adding an "OR" rule to your filter.

Click the "Add OR rule" button and this time, use the "Equals to (case insensitive)" operator. The reason for using "Equals to" here is to ensure an exact match (ignoring case), preventing any answer containing the letter "A" from incorrectly triggering this filter. Then, enter "A" in the bottom field, as shown in the example image. This ensures that leads who use abbreviations are also correctly routed.

Now, all that remains is to open the Mailchimp module connected to this top route and select the Mailchimp list that corresponds to the "SEO leads" segment. You will then simply repeat this process for the remaining answers and their respective Mailchimp lists on the other two routes. Once completed, your scenario will be fully equipped to dynamically route leads based on their preferences!

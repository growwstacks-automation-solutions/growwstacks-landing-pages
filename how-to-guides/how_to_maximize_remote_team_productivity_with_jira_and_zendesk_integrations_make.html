Streamline Cross-Departmental Communications to Solve Customer Issues

To illustrate the transformative role of Make.com, let's consider a common business challenge: systematizing cross-departmental communications for customer support issues. Imagine a scenario where a customer encounters a problem with your company's software and contacts your customer support team via Zendesk. During the discussion, it becomes clear that the issue is more complex than initially thought, requiring the expertise of a development team member.

Traditionally, this handoff could be fraught with delays, especially if the developer is unavailable, in a different time zone, or uses a separate project management system. However, with Make.com, this process can occur seamlessly. By integrating various software services, customer support and development teams can communicate and react swiftly to customer concerns, ensuring that no issue falls through the cracks due to communication breakdowns.

The beauty of such an integration lies in its ability to automate the transfer of critical information. When a customer support agent identifies a technical issue, the system can automatically create a corresponding task or ticket in the development team's preferred platform, like Jira. This eliminates manual data entry, reduces the potential for errors, and ensures that developers receive all the necessary context without delay, allowing them to focus on problem-solving rather than administrative tasks.

Jira Software & Zendesk Integration

Jira Software is a widely used development tool that empowers teams to plan, track, report, and release software efficiently. Its robust features make it a cornerstone for many engineering and product teams. Now, with full support on Make.com, Jira users can effortlessly connect their accounts to a multitude of other services, including customer support platforms like Zendesk, to streamline issue resolution.

Zendesk, on the other hand, is a leading customer service software designed to help businesses manage customer inquiries, often referred to as "tickets." It provides a centralized system for support agents to interact with customers, track issues, and maintain a history of interactions. The integration between Jira Software and Zendesk is particularly powerful for ensuring that customer-reported problems are quickly and effectively addressed by the technical teams.

Consider a situation where a customer issue is reported through Zendesk, leading to the creation of a support ticket. If the customer support team determines they cannot resolve the issue and a developer's intervention is required, the integration becomes invaluable. Whether the developer is in a different city, a different time zone, or simply at another desk, an automated integration ensures the ticket remains on track, preventing potential delays and negative customer experiences.

The core of this solution is to provide a quick and efficient mechanism for informing the developer about the urgent issue. As illustrated in the example below, the integration automatically creates an issue in Jira Software for the relevant developer. This means the developer receives the problem directly within their primary work environment, Jira, which is the software they need to use to solve the issue.

Automated workflow showing a Zendesk ticket creating a Jira issue.

This automated solution eliminates the need for manual communication between support and development teams, such as emails or chat messages, and the subsequent manual transfer of data into Jira. Developers no longer have to wait for someone to connect with them on a separate platform, only to then manually input the details into their project management system. This saves valuable time and reduces the likelihood of information being lost or misinterpreted.

Step 1: Create a Google Sheets spreadsheet

The first foundational step in building this automated customer tracking system is to prepare a dedicated Google Sheets spreadsheet. This sheet will serve as the central repository for your active customer data, allowing for organized storage and easy analysis.

Your spreadsheet should be structured with specific columns to capture all necessary information. These columns include "Date" to timestamp each report, "Number of customers" to record the total active count, and "Percentage Increase/Decrease from the previous month" to highlight month-over-month changes. This structure not only tracks your active customer base but also provides immediate insights into growth or churn trends.

Step 2: Create a Make scenario and add the Stripe app

Once your Google Sheet is ready, the next step is to initiate the automation workflow within Make.com. Begin by logging into your Make account and navigating to the scenario builder. This visual canvas is where you will design and connect the various applications in your workflow.

The Make.com scenario builder, where you'll begin building your automation.

Within the scenario builder, search for the Stripe application and select the "List All Customers" module. This module is designed to retrieve a comprehensive list of all customers from your Stripe account. Before proceeding, you will need to establish a connection between Make.com and your Stripe account by providing your Stripe API key. It's also crucial to set the 'Limit' parameter to a sufficiently high number to ensure that all active customer data is fetched during each execution of the scenario.

Connecting the Stripe module and setting the customer limit.

After successfully connecting your Stripe account and configuring the 'List All Customers' module, confirm your settings by clicking "OK." This completes the initial data extraction phase, preparing the customer information for subsequent processing and analysis within your Google Sheet.

Finalizing the Stripe module setup.

Step 3: Add the Google Sheets app to your scenario

With Stripe data extraction configured, the next logical step is to integrate Google Sheets into your Make.com scenario. This will allow the retrieved customer data to be systematically recorded in your prepared spreadsheet. Click the "Add another module" button in your scenario builder to search for and select the Google Sheets application.

Adding the Google Sheets module to your automation.

From the Google Sheets options, choose the "Add a Row" module. This specific module will append new data as a new row in your spreadsheet each time the scenario runs. You'll need to connect your Google account to Make.com, following the on-screen prompts to grant the necessary permissions. After establishing the connection, select your Google Drive, locate the specific spreadsheet you created in Step 1, and then choose the relevant sheet within it.

Selecting the Google Sheets 'Add a Row' module.

Once connected, the spreadsheet's columns will become visible for mapping. For the "Date" field, use the `formatDate` function with the `now` variable to automatically insert the current date in your desired format. For the "Number of customers" field, map the "Total number of bundles" data item from the Stripe module. This ensures that the total count of active customers is accurately recorded. Click "OK" to finalize this module's configuration.

Authorizing Google Sheets access for Make.com.
Selecting the target spreadsheet and sheet.
Mapping the 'Total number of bundles' to the 'Number of customers' column.

Step 4: Add a filter between the first two modules

A common challenge when retrieving customer data from Stripe is that it often returns individual customer records as separate "bundles" of data. If these bundles were directly passed to Google Sheets, each customer would create a new row, leading to an inflated and inaccurate count of active customers. Our goal is to record only the total number of active customers as a single entry per execution.

To address this, you need to implement a filter between the Stripe "List All Customers" module and the Google Sheets "Add a Row" module. This filter ensures that only one data bundle, representing the aggregated total, proceeds to the next step. Click on the link connecting these two modules to open the filter configuration menu.

Inserting a filter to process only one data bundle.

Configure the filter as follows: map the "Bundle order position" item to the top field. For the operator, select "Numeric operators: Equal to." In the bottom field, enter the value "1." This setup ensures that only the very first data bundle, which typically contains the aggregated count or is the first in a series, is allowed to pass through the filter. This effectively prevents multiple rows from being added for a single scenario run, maintaining the integrity of your active customer count. Click "OK" to apply the filter and proceed.

  • Map the Bundle order position item to the top field
  • Select the Numeric operators: Equal to operator
  • Enter 1 in the bottom field so that only the first bundle passes through
Setting up the filter to ensure only one row is added per run.

Pro tip: Filters are incredibly powerful for controlling data flow in Make.com. Always consider whether your data source might return multiple items when you only need a summary or a single record. Using a filter with 'Bundle order position' equal to 1 is a common pattern for such scenarios.

Step 5: Add the second Google Sheets module

To calculate the month-over-month percentage change in active customers, your automation needs access to the previous month's data. This requires adding a second Google Sheets module to your scenario, specifically designed to retrieve existing information from your spreadsheet.

Add the "Google Sheets - Get a Row" module to your scenario. Similar to the previous Google Sheets module, you will need to select the same spreadsheet and sheet. The key difference here is how you configure this module to fetch the correct data. You'll use a specific formula to retrieve the total number of customers from the previous month, which is typically stored in column B of your sheet. This ensures that your calculations are always based on historical data.

Retrieving previous month's customer data for comparison.

After entering the appropriate formula to target the previous month's customer count, click "OK" to complete the configuration of this module. This step is crucial for enabling the dynamic calculation of growth or decline in your active customer base, providing a more comprehensive view of your business's performance.

Step 6: Add the third Google Sheets module

The next critical step is to automate the calculation of the percentage increase or decrease in your active customer base. This is achieved by adding a third Google Sheets module to your Make.com scenario, specifically the "Update a Row" module. This module will take the current month's data and the previous month's data (retrieved in the prior step) to compute the change.

First, you need to map the "Row Number" from the initial "Add a Row" module to the "Row number" field in this "Update a Row" module. This ensures that the calculation is applied to the correct, newly added row for the current month. Then, to calculate the percentage change, you'll use a formula in the corresponding field: {{sum(total number of bundles - value) / value * 100}}. This formula effectively determines the percentage difference between the current and previous customer counts.

Mapping the row number for updating the percentage change.

This formula will automatically populate the "Percentage Increase/Decrease" column in your Google Sheet, providing an immediate and clear indicator of your customer base's performance. After entering the formula and verifying the mapping, click "OK." With this module configured, your spreadsheet will now dynamically update with the calculated growth metrics, offering valuable insights without any manual computation.

Implementing the percentage change calculation.

Step 7: Add the Slack module

The final component of this automation workflow is to ensure that the relevant stakeholders are promptly informed of the active customer metrics. This is achieved by integrating a communication tool like Slack into your scenario. While this tutorial focuses on Slack, Make.com supports various other team communication platforms, offering flexibility based on your organization's preferences.

Add the "Slack - Create a Message" module to your scenario. You will need to connect your Slack account to Make.com, choosing either the "User" or "Bot" connection type depending on your Slack workspace's setup and desired message sender. Once the connection is established, configure the module by selecting "Select from the List" for the channel option, and then specify the user or channel where you want the notification to be sent.

Integrating Slack for automated notifications.

Craft a clear and concise message that includes the total number of active users and the calculated percentage variation from the previous month. You can also embed additional details or even a direct link to your Google Sheet for quick access to the full report. This ensures that your team receives timely, actionable updates without having to manually check the spreadsheet. After composing your message, click "OK" to complete the Slack module configuration.

  • Choose the Select from the List option
  • Select the Channel Type
  • Select the User/Channel that you want to send the notification to
Customizing the Slack notification message.

Step 8: Test and activate the scenario

With all modules configured, the final step is to thoroughly test and then activate your automation scenario. It's crucial to perform a test run to ensure that all connections are working correctly, data is flowing as expected, and the calculations and notifications are accurate. Observe the scenario execution to confirm that each step processes data as intended, from Stripe extraction to Google Sheets updates and Slack notifications.

Visualizing the scenario's execution during testing.

Once you are satisfied with the test results, you can proceed to set a custom schedule for your scenario. For instance, scheduling it to run on the first day of every month ensures that you consistently receive fresh data for your active customer count, enabling timely strategic planning. After setting your desired schedule, save the scenario and switch it on. Your automated system for measuring active customers from Stripe data is now fully operational, providing continuous, accurate insights into your business's performance.

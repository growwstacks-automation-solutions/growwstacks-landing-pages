What This Workflow Does

This powerful automation streamlines your email marketing and communication efforts by automatically embedding images directly into your outgoing emails. Instead of sending images as cumbersome attachments that recipients need to download, this workflow fetches images from your Google Drive and seamlessly integrates them into your email's HTML body. This creates a much more engaging and professional visual experience for your audience.

How It Works

This automation operates in a few logical steps, ensuring your images are correctly retrieved, processed, and embedded into your emails.

Step 1: Watch for New Files in Google Drive

The workflow begins by monitoring a specified folder in your Google Drive. Whenever new image files are added to this folder, the "Google Drive > Watch files in folder" module detects them. You can configure this module to process a specific number of files per execution, ensuring efficient handling of your image uploads.

Configuring the Google Drive module to watch for new image files.

Step 2: Aggregate Image Files for Email Embedding

Once the image files are detected, an "Array Aggregator" module collects them. This crucial step transforms individual image files into a single array, formatted specifically for email attachments. By setting the target structure type to "Attachments" and mapping the "File ID" from Google Drive to the "Content - ID" field, the aggregator prepares the images to be embedded directly into your email's HTML content.

Setting up the Array Aggregator to prepare images for embedding.

Step 3: Send Email with Embedded Images via Gmail

Finally, the "Gmail > Send an email" module takes over. You'll specify the recipient(s) and subject line. The magic happens when you paste your HTML email content into the body and map the "Array[1] Content-ID" elements from the aggregator into the appropriate image tags within your HTML. This tells Gmail to display the images directly within the email body. The entire aggregated array is then mapped to the "Attachments" field, ensuring the images are properly linked and displayed.

Quick Setup Guide

Setting up this workflow is straightforward. Follow these steps to get your image-embedded emails running in no time:

  1. Create a new scenario in Make.com: Start by adding the "Google Drive > Watch files in folder" module.
  2. Configure Google Drive: Connect your Google Drive account. Select the specific folder where you will upload the images you want to embed. Set the "Maximum number of downloaded files" to your desired limit (e.g., 3 for optimal email display).
  3. Add an Array Aggregator: Link an "Array Aggregator" module to your Google Drive module. Set the "Target Structure Type" to "Attachments". Map the "File ID" from the Google Drive module to the "Content - ID" field within the aggregator.
  4. Set up Gmail: Add a "Gmail > Send an email" module. Connect your Gmail account. Enter the recipient's email address and a clear subject line.
  5. Embed HTML and Images: In the Gmail module's content box, paste your HTML email template. Crucially, where you want an image to appear, insert an HTML image tag (<img src="cid:IMAGE_ID">) and map the "Array[1] Content-ID" element from the Array Aggregator to the IMAGE_ID placeholder.
  6. Attach the Aggregated Array: Finally, map the entire "Array" element from the Array Aggregator to the "Attachments" field in the Gmail module.
  7. Test and Activate: Run the scenario once to test it with a few sample images. Once confirmed working, activate your scenario.

Pro tip: Always optimize your images for web and email before uploading them to Google Drive. Smaller file sizes ensure faster loading times and better deliverability, preventing your emails from being flagged as spam.

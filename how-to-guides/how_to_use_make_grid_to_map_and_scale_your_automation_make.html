A New Level of Visibility

Automation has evolved from a supplementary tool to a core strategic component for businesses. As organizations grow, their automation systems become intricate networks of interconnected applications, teams, and diverse use cases. This increasing complexity often leads to challenges in understanding, maintaining, and safely modifying these systems.

This is precisely where Make Grid steps in, offering a transformative solution to manage this evolving landscape. It provides a comprehensive, real-time overview that simplifies the orchestration of your entire automation and AI infrastructure.

What is Make Grid?

Make Grid is a powerful visualization tool designed to give businesses a clear, real-time map of their automation and AI ecosystem. It automatically generates a dynamic view that illustrates how various scenarios, applications, data storage solutions, and AI-powered components are linked together. This visual clarity empowers users to debug issues more rapidly, scale their operations intelligently, and optimize workflows with unwavering confidence.

Unlike static documentation that quickly becomes outdated, Make Grid updates instantly with every change, ensuring that what you see always reflects the current state of your automation. It's particularly beneficial for teams managing extensive automation, numerous users, and rapidly shifting priorities, serving as a central command center for enhanced collaboration, robust governance, and sustained growth.

Accessing the Grid

Opening Make Grid is a straightforward process, seamlessly integrated into the Make.com platform. This accessibility ensures that you can quickly gain insight into your automation landscape whenever needed.

To begin, simply navigate to the left-hand sidebar within the Make platform. You'll find the "Grid" option conveniently located under the "Organization" section. Clicking this will launch Make Grid in a new, dedicated view.

Locating the Make Grid button in the Make.com sidebar.

Upon launching, Make Grid will automatically display your entire automation environment, thoughtfully organized and grouped by folders and teams. There's no need for manual configuration; the system generates this map from your existing scenarios and objects, ensuring it's always up-to-date and accurately reflects your organization's current automation activities.

How to Read the Grid: Visual Legend

When you first open Make Grid, you'll be presented with a dynamic, 3D-style visual landscape. This landscape is composed of white tiles, each representing a distinct folder within your Make.com organization. Inside these tiles, various icons are used to symbolize the different objects that constitute your automation system, all connected by lines that clearly illustrate the flow of data between them.

An overview of the Make Grid interface, showing folders and interconnected scenarios.

Understanding this visual language is key to effectively utilizing Make Grid. Here's a breakdown of how to interpret the elements you'll encounter:

A visual legend explaining the different shapes and symbols used in Make Grid.
  • Purple circles: These represent individual scenarios, which are the core automated workflows you've built.
  • Triangles: These symbols denote webhooks, which typically act as triggers that initiate your scenarios.
  • Special shapes: Various distinct shapes are used to represent different data storage objects, such as spreadsheets, database tables, or other data repositories.
  • Hexagons: This shape is a catch-all for other miscellaneous components, including AI agents, various applications, HTTP modules, and custom services.
  • Lines and arrows: These visual connectors illustrate the relationships and data flows between all the objects, showing how information moves through your automation.

To refine your view and focus on specific areas, utilize the filter bar located at the top of the Grid interface. This allows you to narrow down your automation landscape based on criteria such as teams, folders, or specific scenario properties like status, applications used, or the creator. You can also search for any element, like a table column or a URL, and Grid will instantly highlight related scenarios and dependencies.

Applying filters in Make Grid to narrow down the view of your automation landscape.

This powerful filtering and search functionality transforms Make Grid into more than just a visual map; it becomes a dynamic control center for managing even the most complex automation environments.

Pro tip: Regularly use the search function in Make Grid to quickly locate specific data points, such as a particular table column or API endpoint. This can save significant time when auditing data usage or preparing for system updates, ensuring you don't miss any critical dependencies.

Grid in Action: Maintain, Optimize, and Scale with Clarity

Make Grid is far more than a simple visualization tool; it's an active platform for maintaining the health of your automation systems, optimizing their performance, and scaling your operations confidently. Every feature within Grid is meticulously designed to facilitate clear, decisive action. Here’s how it empowers you:

Gain a Holistic View

Make Grid provides a real-time, comprehensive visual map of your entire automation ecosystem. This allows you to effortlessly zoom out for a high-level understanding of how different folders, teams, and solutions interlink, or zoom in to meticulously examine specific flows, modules, or individual objects. This dual perspective ensures you always have the right level of detail at your fingertips.

This holistic view is crucial for strategic planning and understanding the broader impact of your automation efforts across the organization. It helps identify potential bottlenecks or areas for consolidation, fostering a more efficient and streamlined operational environment.

A zoomed-out view of the Make Grid, illustrating the interconnectedness of various automation components.

Identify Dependencies with Zero Guesswork

When it comes to modifying or replacing core components like a shared database or an API, Make Grid eliminates the guesswork. It instantly reveals which scenarios are dependent on that component, allowing you to anticipate and avoid potential disruptions. This proactive insight ensures that updates can be performed safely and efficiently.

For example, if you plan to replace a Google Sheet or an Airtable table, Make Grid enables you to first identify all connected scenarios. This means you can make necessary adjustments to dependent workflows before implementing the change, ensuring seamless transitions without manual checks or unexpected errors.

Visualizing dependencies in Make Grid to understand which scenarios rely on a specific component.

Focus on What Matters

Make Grid allows you to prioritize your attention by using filters to highlight active or critical workflows. You can quickly locate production scenarios, monitor AI usage, or identify stalled executions, enabling you to take targeted action. This capability is essential for maintaining operational efficiency and addressing urgent issues promptly.

For instance, you can filter to display only production scenarios that incorporate Make AI Agents and have been deactivated due to errors. This focused view helps you resolve critical issues rapidly, without the need to sift through extensive lists or irrelevant data.

Using filters to focus on specific scenarios, such as those with errors or involving AI agents.

Audit Data Usage and Streamline Cleanups

When deprecating a column or an object, Make Grid provides precise information on where it's referenced, down to the field level. This feature is invaluable for ensuring clean data management and preventing accidental data loss or workflow disruptions. By searching for a specific name, you can see all its connections.

For example, if you need to remove a 'Jira Ticket Link' field from Airtable, you can search for it in Make Grid to identify every scenario that references this field. This allows you to make informed decisions and necessary adjustments before proceeding with the removal, ensuring data integrity across your systems.

Searching for a specific data field in Make Grid to audit its usage across scenarios.

Visualize Cross-Functional Connections

Make Grid highlights shared objects across different folders and teams, offering a clear visual understanding of how various systems overlap. This insight is crucial for avoiding unintended impacts when making changes, especially in large or complex organizations where multiple departments might interact with the same data sources.

For instance, if a data store is utilized across several folders, Make Grid will clearly show these connections. This allows you to understand the potential ripple effects of any modification before it's implemented, promoting better coordination and reducing risks associated with cross-functional changes.

Viewing cross-functional connections in Make Grid to understand shared resources.

Spot and Resolve Issues Instantly

Make Grid automatically flags scenarios that are deactivated due to errors or those with unresolved incomplete executions. This immediate visual feedback is vital for diagnosing and resolving problems quickly, preventing them from spreading and impacting other parts of your automation ecosystem. By highlighting these critical areas, Grid enables proactive problem-solving.

This feature transforms your approach to troubleshooting, allowing you to address issues at their source rather than reacting to widespread failures. It ensures that your automation remains robust and reliable, minimizing downtime and maintaining operational continuity.

Identifying and resolving issues quickly with Make Grid's visual error indicators.

Optimize Operations Usage

Make Grid provides valuable insights into which workflows consume the most operations, allowing you to effectively balance workload and minimize unnecessary resource expenditure. This visibility is crucial for managing costs and ensuring your automation runs as efficiently as possible. By understanding operation consumption, you can make informed decisions about optimizing your scenarios.

These capabilities transform raw data into actionable insights, making Make Grid a live, interactive command center that evolves with your needs. Future enhancements, such as a data transfer layer and an analytics dashboard, will offer even deeper insights into system performance and usage trends, helping you identify and manage scenarios that contribute significantly to your operational costs.

Pro tip: Regularly review the operation usage visualization in Make Grid. This can help you identify inefficient scenarios that might be consuming excessive resources, allowing you to refactor them for better cost-effectiveness and performance.

This enables you to assess risks, standardize AI practices, and generate comprehensive usage reports across your organization.
  • Enable IT and privacy teams: With Make Grid, senior IT leaders and privacy officers gain immediate observability into the automation landscape. They can see precisely what's happening, where it's occurring, and what systems it touches, all without needing to request detailed walkthroughs or documentation from various teams.

Ultimately, Make Grid ensures that your automation systems remain trustworthy, auditable, and highly adaptable, even as they grow in complexity and scope. It provides the necessary oversight to manage and secure your digital operations effectively.

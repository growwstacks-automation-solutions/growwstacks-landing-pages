How It Works

1. Automated Content Calendar for Instagram

This workflow allows you to manage all your Instagram posts from a centralized Airtable base. You simply upload your photo and video content, add captions and hashtags, and schedule the desired posting time. Make.com then takes over, automatically publishing your content to Instagram. Crucially, it updates your Airtable records to prevent duplicate posts, ensuring a smooth and organized content flow. This means you can plan your content weeks or months in advance, knowing that your posts will go live exactly when intended, without manual intervention.

2. Auto-Reply to Instagram Comments

Engaging with comments is essential for building a community, but responding to every single one can be a huge time commitment. This template automates replies to specific Instagram comments based on keywords you define. For example, if customers frequently ask "What's the price?" or "Where can I buy this?", the automation can instantly provide a pre-written answer or a link to your product page. This ensures prompt customer service, enhances engagement, and frees up your team from repetitive answering, allowing them to focus on more complex inquiries.

3. Get Insights on Your Competitor’s Followers and Media

Staying ahead of the competition requires constant vigilance. This workflow automates the tracking of your competitors' Instagram performance. By simply adding their Instagram for Business usernames to a Google Sheets spreadsheet, Make.com will automatically populate the sheet with data such as follower counts and posting rates on a weekly basis. This invaluable insight helps you understand market trends, identify successful strategies, and adjust your own approach to maintain a competitive edge. Note that this only works for Instagram Business accounts.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Connect Your Accounts: In Make.com, establish connections to your Instagram Business account, Airtable, and Google Sheets.
  2. Configure Content Calendar: Set up your Airtable base with fields for image/video URLs, captions, hashtags, and scheduled post times. Map these fields in the Make.com scenario.
  3. Define Auto-Reply Keywords: In the auto-reply scenario, specify the keywords that will trigger automated responses and craft the corresponding reply messages.
  4. Add Competitors to Google Sheet: Create a Google Sheet and list your competitors' Instagram Business usernames. Configure the Make.com scenario to pull data into this sheet.
  5. Activate and Monitor: Turn on your scenarios in Make.com and monitor their performance. Adjust schedules or keywords as needed to optimize results.

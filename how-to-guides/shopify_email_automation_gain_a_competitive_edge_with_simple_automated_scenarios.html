Setting Up Automated “Thank You” Emails for Your Shopify Store

With your accounts prepared, the first step is to navigate to your Make dashboard. From there, you'll initiate a new automation scenario. Look for the "Create new scenario" button, typically located in the top right corner of the dashboard, and click it to begin building your workflow.

Start by creating a new scenario in your Make dashboard.

Next, you'll need to identify and select the applications that will be part of this automation. For our "thank you" email scenario, you will need to search for and select both "Shopify" and "Email" from the available apps. These two apps will form the core of your automated workflow, with Shopify acting as the trigger and the Email app handling the outgoing communication.

After selecting both apps, click the "Continue" button, usually found in the top right corner of the screen. This action will transition you to the visual canvas, which is where you will graphically construct and test your automated workflow. The visual canvas provides an intuitive interface for connecting the different modules and defining the flow of your automation.

Select Shopify and Email as the core applications for your automation.

Configuring the Shopify Module

Now that you are on the visual canvas, you're ready to begin building your automation. Click on the central blank module on your screen. This will prompt you to select the first application for your workflow, which in this case will be Shopify. This initial module will serve as the trigger for your automated "thank you" emails.

Choose Shopify as your starting module on the visual canvas.

A list of available modules for Shopify will appear. From this list, select the "Watch New Customers" module. This module acts as a trigger, initiating your scenario whenever a new customer registers on your Shopify store. You can quickly find it by typing "watch" into the search bar or by browsing the options provided.

Select the 'Watch New Customers' module to trigger the automation.

The next crucial step is to connect your Shopify account to Make. This is a straightforward process: click on the Shopify module, then click the "Add" button, and follow the prompts to authorize Make to establish the connection. This secure link allows Make to access the necessary data from your Shopify store to trigger the automation.

Authorize Make to connect with your Shopify account.

Once the connection is successfully established, you'll need to configure the number of results to be processed during each cycle. This setting determines how many new customer registrations the automation will handle each time it runs. It's important to align this number with your expected volume of new registrations between cycles.

For instance, if you plan to run this scenario every 60 minutes and your shop typically receives around 20 new registrations per hour, it's advisable to set this number to 30 or more. This provides a buffer, ensuring that your workflow can accommodate peak registration periods and send "thank you" emails to all newly registered users without missing any. After setting this, you can schedule the module to run at your preferred interval. Free Make plans usually allow scenarios to run every 15 minutes, while paid plans offer more frequent execution, down to a 1-minute frequency.

Configure the number of results and schedule the module's execution frequency.

Pro tip: When setting the number of results, always err on the side of caution and set it slightly higher than your average expected registrations. This prevents any new customers from being missed during busy periods and ensures every new registrant receives their welcome email promptly.

Configuring the Email Module

With the Shopify module now fully configured, the next step is to set up the email module. To do this, click on the right side of your existing Shopify module on the visual canvas to add a new app. From the options that appear, select the "Email" app. This module will be responsible for composing and sending your automated "thank you" messages.

Add the Email app as the next module in your automation flow.

Just like with the Shopify module, you will need to connect your email account to Make. This typically involves clicking the "Add" button within the Email module and authorizing the connection with your chosen email provider. This secure authorization allows Make to send emails on your behalf as part of the automated scenario.

Once your email account is connected, it's time to craft the "thank you" email itself. This email will be automatically sent to new registrants on your Shopify store. While Make handles the automation, the content of your email is entirely up to you. You'll configure the email to be sent to every new user who registers, ensuring a personalized touch.

To ensure the email is sent to the correct recipient, you'll need to enable the "map" function within the email configuration. This function allows you to dynamically insert data from the Shopify module (like the new customer's email address) directly into your email. Look for a toggle or checkbox labeled "Map" and switch it on.

Activate the mapping function to dynamically insert customer data.

After enabling the map function, a menu of available data items will appear. From this menu, select the "email" item. This tells Make to use the email address collected from the new Shopify customer as the recipient for your "thank you" message. This ensures that each email is delivered to the correct individual.

Map the customer's email address to the recipient field.

Once the recipient is set, switch the content type to "plaintext" and fill in the following essential fields: the "Subject" for your email (this will be the email subject line) and the "Content" (this is the actual message you want to send to new registrants). You can further personalize both the subject line and the email content by using other mappable items, such as the customer's first name, to make the message feel more individual and engaging.

With your email configured, it's time to test your scenario. Click the "Run once" button, typically located in the bottom left corner of your screen, to execute a single test run. If the test is successful and the email is sent as expected, you can then save your scenario and turn it on using the corresponding buttons in the visual editor. Congratulations, your automatic "thank you" emails for new Shopify customers are now live!

Pro tip: Always test your automation thoroughly before activating it. Send a test email to yourself to ensure all dynamic fields are populating correctly and the message appears as intended. This helps catch any errors before they reach your customers.

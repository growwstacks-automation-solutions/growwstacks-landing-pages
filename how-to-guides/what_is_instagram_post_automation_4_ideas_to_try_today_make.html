Schedule and Upload Posts Automatically

While the Instagram app offers basic scheduling features, you still need to manually set specific publish dates and times for each post. If your content strategy involves fixed posting times or regular intervals, this manual process can become repetitive and inefficient. Automating this task can significantly streamline your workflow.

You can connect your automation to a content storage application, such as Airtable, where you upload your prepared posts. Once connected, you can configure your desired posting frequency—for example, every Monday at 10 AM—and the automation will automatically publish your content according to this schedule. This ensures consistent content delivery without requiring constant manual oversight.

Make.com offers flexibility for both time-based and logic-based scheduling. Time-based scheduling involves publishing posts at predetermined intervals, such as "every Tuesday at 2 PM." Logic-based scheduling, on the other hand, triggers posts when a specific condition is met, like "when new Instagram content is uploaded to Airtable." It's important to be mindful of file size limits on Make.com, which vary by plan, especially when uploading large video files. For instance, Make's Core plan supports file transfers up to 100MB per operation.

Things to Be Aware of When Automating Your Instagram Processes

Instagram post automation, especially when combined with AI, offers powerful capabilities. However, it's crucial to approach it with caution and awareness of Instagram's policies to avoid potential pitfalls.

Instagram has strict rules against inauthentic activity. Using multiple fake accounts to artificially boost engagement (likes, follows, comments) on your genuine account is a violation of their policy and can lead to the removal of such inauthentic accounts. Ensure your automation practices align with genuine engagement and community building.

Furthermore, if you use AI to create video or audio content that appears realistic, Instagram requires you to label these posts as AI-generated. While this labeling isn't mandatory for AI-generated images, it's a critical step for video and audio. If you fail to add the label, Instagram may detect the AI content and add the label itself. Currently, Make.com's Instagram upload modules do not offer this specific "Add AI Label" setting, so for AI-created video or audio, it's recommended to upload directly through the Instagram app to ensure proper disclosure.

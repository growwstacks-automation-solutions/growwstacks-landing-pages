Zendesk is an excellent platform for managing customer engagement, but its built-in automation capabilities can often be quite intricate. Many users find themselves navigating through extensive documentation and complex interfaces to set up even basic automations.

This complexity can manifest in several ways. The documentation, while comprehensive, can be overwhelming and hard to pinpoint specific solutions. The user interface, relying heavily on fields and drop-downs, makes it difficult to visualize the flow of data and logic within an automation. Furthermore, error handling is often rudimentary, leaving users to decipher issues without clear diagnostic tools.

1. Add New Zendesk Tickets to Google Sheets

This powerful integration automatically transfers incoming Zendesk tickets, along with all their relevant information, directly into a Google Sheets spreadsheet. This functionality proves invaluable in numerous scenarios, particularly for onboarding new customer service representatives.

While Zendesk offers competitive pricing, starting at $5 per user per month, costs can escalate quickly as customer support teams grow. In this environment, managers often seek ways to evaluate new hires before fully integrating them into the system.

By implementing this Make.com solution, managers can grant new reps access to Zendesk ticket data via Google Sheets. This allows them to monitor performance and assess capabilities in a controlled environment, streamlining the onboarding process and ensuring new team members are ready before full system access is granted.

2. Create Zendesk Tickets from Google Forms Responses

Google Forms are incredibly versatile tools, widely utilized by businesses for a variety of data collection purposes. These include customer registration, gathering feedback on products or services, and conducting satisfaction surveys to gauge customer sentiment.

With the seamless integration capabilities of Make.com, responses submitted through Google Forms can be automatically transformed into new Zendesk tickets. This ensures that every piece of customer input, whether it's a new registration or a feedback submission, is immediately routed to the customer satisfaction team.

This automation allows the support team to address inquiries and feedback promptly, without any manual data transfer delays. It streamlines the entire process from data collection to resolution, ensuring that customer needs are met efficiently from the very first interaction.

4. Create Zendesk Tickets from New RingCentral Messages

This particular integration is affectionately dubbed "The Dragnet" for its comprehensive ability to capture all customer communications. It automatically generates Zendesk tickets for every voice call, SMS, MMS, and even fax message received through RingCentral. Yes, businesses still use fax, and this integration ensures those communications are not lost!

While RingCentral excels at providing the essential infrastructure for receiving diverse messages, Zendesk is the superior platform for managing customer satisfaction and support workflows. The challenge lies in connecting these two powerful services seamlessly.

With "The Dragnet" template from Make.com, this connection becomes effortless. All customer interactions, regardless of their origin in RingCentral, are automatically converted into actionable Zendesk tickets, ensuring no query goes unaddressed and centralizing all support efforts.

5. Create a New Ticket in Zendesk from New Shopify Orders

Exceptional customer satisfaction often begins long before a problem arises, a fact well understood by savvy e-commerce managers. Proactive customer service can significantly enhance brand loyalty and reduce the volume of reactive support requests.

This integration empowers Shopify store managers to stay ahead of the curve by automating the creation of Zendesk tickets directly from new Shopify order data. This allows support teams to monitor orders and identify potential issues before customers even realize them.

Consider a scenario where a product sold in large quantities is discovered to have a manufacturing defect. With this automation, all affected orders would instantly generate Zendesk tickets. This enables the customer service team to proactively reach out to customers, manage expectations, and offer solutions, thereby preventing a sudden influx of complaints and maintaining customer trust.

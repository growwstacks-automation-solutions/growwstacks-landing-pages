What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the critical challenge of responding quickly to LinkedIn Lead Gen Form submissions while maintaining personalized communication. When prospects submit information through your LinkedIn form, this workflow instantly sends them a customized email with your Calendly scheduling link - but only if they've explicitly consented to receive it.

The system eliminates manual follow-up tasks for your sales team while ensuring no lead slips through the cracks. By reducing response time from hours (or days) to minutes, you significantly increase the likelihood of converting leads into scheduled meetings. The template includes smart logic to check for consent before sending scheduling links, maintaining compliance with email marketing regulations.

How It Works

1. LinkedIn lead capture

When a prospect submits your LinkedIn Lead Gen Form, Make.com detects the new lead through LinkedIn's API. The workflow captures all form field data including name, email, company, and any custom questions you've configured.

2. Consent verification

The system checks whether the lead opted-in to receive scheduling communications (via a checkbox field in your LinkedIn form). Only leads with explicit consent proceed to the next steps, while others are logged in your system for later follow-up.

3. Email personalization

The workflow dynamically inserts the lead's name, company, and other relevant details into a pre-designed email template. This maintains personalization while automating the process.

4. Calendly link delivery

An email containing your branded Calendly scheduling link is sent to the lead's email address. The message can include multiple meeting types or specific time slots based on your availability.

5. CRM synchronization

Optionally, the workflow can update your CRM system (like Salesforce or HubSpot) with the lead details and mark that the scheduling link has been sent, creating a complete audit trail.

Pro tip: Configure your LinkedIn form to ask qualifying questions that help personalize the scheduling email. For example, "What's the best time for your consultation?" can trigger different Calendly event types in the response.

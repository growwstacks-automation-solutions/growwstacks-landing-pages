What This Workflow Does

Sales opportunities often stall at critical stages because follow-up tasks get overlooked or delayed. This automation solves that problem by automatically creating three specific tasks whenever an opportunity in Sellsy reaches a predetermined stage. The workflow ensures consistent follow-up procedures, maintains deal momentum, and prevents promising opportunities from falling through the cracks due to human oversight.

By automating task creation, sales teams can focus on executing tasks rather than remembering to create them. This workflow typically creates tasks like client follow-up calls, proposal preparation, internal team coordination, or documentation updates—whatever your sales process requires at that specific opportunity stage. The automation works seamlessly in the background, triggering exactly when opportunities need attention most.

How It Works

Step 1: Opportunity Stage Monitoring

The workflow continuously monitors your Sellsy opportunities, watching for when they transition into specific stages you've designated as trigger points. This could be stages like "Proposal Sent," "Negotiation," or "Decision Phase"—wherever consistent follow-up is most critical for conversion success.

Step 2: Automatic Task Creation

Once an opportunity reaches the target stage, the automation instantly creates three predefined tasks in Sellsy. These tasks are configured with specific descriptions, due dates, and assignments based on your sales process requirements. Each task can have different priorities, deadlines, and responsible team members.

Step 3: Task Assignment and Notification

The created tasks are automatically assigned to the appropriate team members with clear deadlines. The system can also trigger notifications through Sellsy or integrated communication tools, ensuring immediate awareness and action from your sales team without manual intervention.

Pro tip: Configure different task sets for different opportunity stages to match your sales process precisely. For example, "Proposal Sent" might trigger follow-up tasks, while "Negotiation" might trigger internal approval tasks.

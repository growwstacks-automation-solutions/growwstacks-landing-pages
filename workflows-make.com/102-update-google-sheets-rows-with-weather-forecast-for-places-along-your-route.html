What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious task of manually checking and updating weather forecasts for multiple locations along travel routes. Businesses that operate across different geographic areas often waste significant staff time gathering this data from various weather services and entering it into spreadsheets.

The template automatically fetches current weather forecasts for specified locations and updates your Google Sheets document with accurate, timestamped data. This ensures your team always has access to the latest weather conditions without manual intervention, enabling better decision-making for route planning, scheduling, and risk management.

How It Works

1. Trigger the workflow

The automation can be set to run on a schedule (e.g., every 6 hours) or triggered manually when needed. You configure the frequency based on how often you need updated forecasts.

2. Retrieve location data

The system reads your predefined list of locations from specified Google Sheets cells. These can be addresses, city names, or coordinates along your planned routes.

3. Fetch weather data

For each location, the workflow queries a weather API service to retrieve current forecast data. This typically includes temperature, precipitation probability, wind conditions, and other relevant metrics.

4. Update your spreadsheet

The automation writes the fresh weather data back to your Google Sheets document in the designated cells, with timestamps to indicate when each update occurred.

Pro tip: Combine this with conditional formatting in Google Sheets to automatically highlight locations with potentially disruptive weather conditions (e.g., heavy rain or high winds).

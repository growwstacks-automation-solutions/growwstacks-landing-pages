What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the problem of constantly checking Facebook pages for updates. Social media managers, marketers, and customer support teams often waste hours manually monitoring pages for new posts, risking delayed responses to important announcements.

The workflow automatically detects new Facebook posts from specified pages and sends instant push notifications to your Google Chrome browser. Unlike email alerts that get buried in inboxes or mobile notifications that may be muted, Chrome desktop notifications ensure critical updates get your immediate attention.

How It Works

1. Facebook Page Monitoring

The system regularly checks Facebook's API for new posts from your configured pages. You can monitor multiple pages simultaneously while filtering for specific post types or keywords.

2. New Post Detection

When the workflow identifies new content, it extracts key details including post text, images, links, and timestamps. This data gets formatted for the notification while preserving all important context.

3. Chrome Notification Trigger

Make.com sends the formatted post data to Chrome's notification system. The notification appears on your desktop with customizable content - you can choose to show just an alert or include post previews.

Pro tip: Configure different notification sounds for different page types (e.g., urgent alerts for customer complaints vs. standard tones for marketing posts).

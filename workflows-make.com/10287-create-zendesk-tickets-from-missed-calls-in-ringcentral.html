What This Workflow Does

Missed customer calls represent lost revenue opportunities and frustrated clients. This automation solves that problem by instantly converting every unanswered RingCentral call into a properly categorized Zendesk support ticket. The moment a call goes unanswered, Make.com captures all relevant call details and creates a ticket with the caller's information, timestamp, and call duration.

The workflow eliminates manual data entry for your support team while ensuring no customer inquiry falls through the cracks. Tickets are automatically routed based on your business rules, with all necessary context included so agents can follow up effectively. Our clients using this solution report 30-40% faster response times to missed calls.

How It Works

1. RingCentral detects a missed call

The workflow triggers whenever RingCentral logs an unanswered incoming call. Make.com receives the call details including phone number, time, duration, and any available caller ID information.

2. Data is formatted for Zendesk

The system processes the raw call data into a structured format suitable for Zendesk. You can customize which fields get mapped and how information is presented in the ticket.

3. Ticket is created in Zendesk

Make.com creates a new ticket in your Zendesk instance with all relevant call details. The workflow can be configured to set priority levels, assign agents, add tags, or include custom fields based on your business rules.

4. Notification sent to your team

Optionally, the system can send Slack or email notifications about newly created tickets to ensure prompt follow-up. This step is fully customizable based on your team's workflow.

Pro tip: Configure your RingCentral call handling rules to ensure calls only register as "missed" after your desired ring duration (typically 20-30 seconds). This prevents premature ticket creation for calls you might still answer.

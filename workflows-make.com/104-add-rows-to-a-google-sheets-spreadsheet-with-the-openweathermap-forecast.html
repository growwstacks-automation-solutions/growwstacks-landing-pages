What This Workflow Does

This automated workflow solves the problem of manually tracking weather forecasts for business analysis. Instead of copying and pasting weather data daily, the system automatically fetches the 14-day forecast from OpenWeatherMap and appends it as a new row in your Google Sheets spreadsheet.

Each execution creates a timestamped record of temperature, precipitation, wind speed, and other meteorological data for your specified location. This builds a historical weather database that can be analyzed alongside your business metrics to identify weather-related patterns and impacts.

How It Works

1. Trigger Setup

The workflow can be scheduled to run at regular intervals (daily, weekly, etc.) or triggered manually through the Make.com interface.

2. Weather Data Fetch

Make.com connects to OpenWeatherMap's API using your location coordinates or city name, retrieving the extended forecast with various weather parameters.

3. Data Formatting

The raw weather data is processed and organized into a structured format suitable for spreadsheet recording, with proper units and labels.

4. Google Sheets Update

The formatted weather data is appended as a new row in your specified Google Sheet, with automatic timestamping for tracking changes over time.

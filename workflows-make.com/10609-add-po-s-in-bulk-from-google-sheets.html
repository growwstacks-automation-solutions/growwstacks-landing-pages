What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical pain point for manufacturing businesses using Katana MRP - the tedious manual entry of purchase orders. By connecting Google Sheets directly to Katana, it enables bulk processing of purchase orders with complete accuracy.

The workflow automatically transfers all purchase order details from your standardized Google Sheets template into Katana's manufacturing system. This includes vendor information, product SKUs, quantities, pricing, and delivery dates - eliminating hours of repetitive data entry while ensuring 100% data consistency between systems.

How It Works

1. Google Sheets data preparation

Your procurement team maintains purchase orders in a standardized Google Sheets template. The spreadsheet can be collaboratively edited by multiple team members with all changes automatically saved.

2. Triggering the automation

The workflow can be scheduled to run at specific intervals (daily, weekly, etc.) or triggered manually when ready to process a batch of purchase orders.

3. Data validation and processing

Each row in the spreadsheet is validated to ensure all required fields are present and formatted correctly before being sent to Katana. Invalid entries are flagged for correction.

4. Katana integration

Validated purchase orders are automatically created in Katana using its API, with all relevant details mapped to the correct fields in your manufacturing system.

5. Confirmation and logging

The workflow provides confirmation of successful uploads and logs any errors for follow-up, creating a complete audit trail of all processed orders.

Pro tip: Maintain a dedicated "Processed POs" tab in your Google Sheet to track which orders have been successfully uploaded to Katana.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Copy the template scenario from Make.com to your account
  2. Connect your Google Sheets account and select your PO spreadsheet

  5. Test with a small batch of purchase orders
  6. Schedule the automation to run at your preferred frequency

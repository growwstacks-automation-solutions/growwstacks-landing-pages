What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common challenge for marketers and business owners who use Paperform for lead capture and ConvertKit for email marketing. When someone submits information through your Paperform, you want to categorize them in ConvertKit based on their responses—but manually adding tags is time-consuming and prone to errors.

The workflow automatically applies specific ConvertKit tags to subscribers based on their Paperform submission data. For example, if someone indicates they're interested in "SEO services" on your form, they'll immediately receive an "SEO Lead" tag in ConvertKit. This enables you to send targeted follow-up emails without any manual intervention.

How It Works

1. Paperform submission trigger

The workflow starts when someone submits your Paperform. Make.com receives the submission data including the respondent's email address and any form field responses you want to use for tagging.

2. Data processing

The workflow checks specific form fields you've designated for tagging purposes. This could be dropdown selections, checkbox choices, or text responses that indicate subscriber interests or characteristics.

3. ConvertKit tag application

Based on the form responses, the workflow adds one or more tags to the subscriber's ConvertKit profile using their email address. The tags are applied instantly, typically within seconds of form submission.

Pro tip: Use conditional logic to apply different tags based on multiple form responses. For example, tag someone as "Advanced" if they select both "SEO" and "Paid Ads" interests.

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the challenge of getting your latest content in front of subscribers instantly, without relying on email open rates or social media algorithms. Every time you publish new content to your RSS feed (blog posts, news articles, product updates), the system automatically sends a push notification through Apple Safari to all opted-in users.

Unlike email newsletters that might get buried in crowded inboxes, Safari push notifications appear directly on users' desktops and iOS devices, even when they're not actively browsing. Publishers using this system typically see 3-5x higher engagement rates compared to email alone, with notifications reaching subscribers within seconds of publication.

How It Works

1. RSS Feed Monitoring

The workflow continuously checks your specified RSS feed for new entries at intervals you configure (e.g., every 15 minutes). Make.com's RSS module detects new posts by comparing publication timestamps.

2. Content Processing

When new content appears, the system extracts key elements like the post title, excerpt, and URL. You can customize which elements to include in notifications and apply formatting rules.

3. Safari Push Trigger

Using Apple's Push Notification Service (APNs), the workflow sends formatted notifications to all subscribed Safari users. Notifications appear on macOS desktops and iOS devices with your site's icon.

Pro tip: Add UTM parameters to links in notifications to track engagement in Google Analytics. Example: ?utm_source=safari_push&utm_medium=notification&utm_campaign=new_post

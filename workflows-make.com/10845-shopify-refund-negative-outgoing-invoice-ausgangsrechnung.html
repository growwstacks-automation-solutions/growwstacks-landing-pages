What This Workflow Does

This automation bridges the gap between your Shopify store's refund activity and FreeFinance accounting records. Whenever a refund is issued in Shopify, the workflow automatically generates a corresponding negative outgoing invoice in FreeFinance. This maintains perfect financial records without manual intervention.

For ecommerce businesses processing numerous refunds, this eliminates the time-consuming and error-prone process of manually creating negative invoices. The system captures all relevant details including refund amount, reason, original order information, and customer data - ensuring your accounting records remain complete and audit-ready.

How It Works

1. Shopify Refund Detection

The workflow checks your Shopify store for new refunds at scheduled intervals (default: hourly). It identifies both full and partial refunds, capturing all transaction details.

2. Data Transformation

Refund information is formatted into FreeFinance's required invoice structure, converting positive values to negatives and mapping Shopify fields to the correct accounting categories.

3. FreeFinance Invoice Creation

The system creates a negative outgoing invoice in FreeFinance that exactly mirrors the original sale but with reversed values, maintaining clean audit trails.

4. Error Handling & Notifications

If any step fails, the workflow triggers alerts so you can manually intervene before accounting discrepancies occur.

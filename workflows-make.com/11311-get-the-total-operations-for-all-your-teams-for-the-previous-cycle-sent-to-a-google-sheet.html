What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the time-consuming challenge of manually collecting and compiling operational metrics from multiple teams into a centralized report. Managers often waste hours each week gathering data from different departments, checking for accuracy, and formatting spreadsheets - time that could be better spent analyzing performance and making strategic decisions.

The workflow automatically aggregates key operational metrics (like tasks completed, tickets resolved, or units produced) from all your teams at the end of each cycle (daily, weekly, or monthly) and compiles them into a structured Google Sheet. It maintains historical records while providing real-time visibility into team performance across your organization.

How It Works

1. Data Collection

The workflow connects to your existing systems (CRMs, project tools, databases) to pull the latest operational metrics from each team at scheduled intervals.

2. Data Processing

It validates the data, checks for completeness, and calculates totals, averages, or other required metrics across all teams.

3. Sheet Population

The processed data gets organized into your Google Sheet with proper formatting, maintaining separate tabs or columns for different teams while showing consolidated totals.

4. Historical Archiving

Each cycle's data is timestamped and stored, allowing you to track performance trends over time without manual record-keeping.

Pro tip: Add conditional formatting in your Google Sheet to automatically highlight teams exceeding targets or falling behind expectations.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for operations managers, department heads, and business owners who need to track performance across multiple teams. It's particularly valuable for:

  • Companies with distributed or remote teams needing centralized visibility
  • Businesses tracking operational KPIs across departments
  • Organizations preparing weekly/monthly performance reports
  • Managers who spend excessive time compiling team metrics manually

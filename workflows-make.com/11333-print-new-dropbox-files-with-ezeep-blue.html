What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious process of manually downloading and printing files from Dropbox. Many businesses still require physical copies of digital documents for filing, client delivery, or internal processes. The manual download-print cycle wastes valuable employee time and creates opportunities for missed documents.

The workflow automatically monitors your Dropbox for new files and sends them directly to your ezeep Blue-connected printer. It runs every 15 minutes by default (configurable to your needs), ensuring documents are printed shortly after being added to your cloud storage. This creates a seamless bridge between your digital document management and physical printing needs.

How It Works

1. Dropbox Monitoring

The workflow continuously checks your specified Dropbox folder for newly added files using Dropbox's API. It can be configured to watch specific subfolders or the entire account.

2. File Processing

When a new file is detected, the system retrieves it from Dropbox and prepares it for printing. The workflow maintains all original file formatting during this transfer process.

3. ezeep Blue Printing

The file is automatically sent to your ezeep Blue cloud printing service, which routes it to your designated printer with preset formatting options like duplex, color settings, and stapling.

4. Confirmation & Logging

The system records each printing event, allowing you to track document processing history and verify successful prints.

Pro tip: Set up separate workflows for different Dropbox folders to apply distinct printing presets (like color vs B&W) based on document type.

What You'll Need

  1. A Dropbox Business or Pro account (personal accounts work but have API limits)
  2. An active ezeep Blue subscription
  3. A network-connected printer compatible with ezeep Blue
  4. A Make.com account (free tier works for basic usage)

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Click "Get This Workflow" to import the template to your Make.com account
  2. Connect your Dropbox account in the first module
  3. Configure which folder(s) to monitor for new files
  4. Connect your ezeep Blue account in the printing module
  5. Select your default printer and print settings
  6. Adjust the schedule frequency if needed (default: every 15 minutes)
  7. Turn on the scenario to begin automated printing

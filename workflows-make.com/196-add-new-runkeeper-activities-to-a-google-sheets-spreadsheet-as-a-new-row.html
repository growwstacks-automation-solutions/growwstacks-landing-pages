What This Workflow Does

This automation eliminates the manual work of tracking your fitness activities by automatically syncing every new Runkeeper workout to a Google Sheets spreadsheet. Whether you're training for a marathon, tracking your daily steps, or monitoring your cycling routes, this workflow ensures all your data is captured in an organized, analyzable format.

The template creates a complete historical record of your activities including distance, duration, pace, calories burned, and timestamps. Unlike Runkeeper's built-in reports, Google Sheets lets you create custom charts, calculate weekly/monthly totals, and combine data with other health metrics for deeper insights.

How It Works

1. Runkeeper detects a completed activity

When you finish a run, walk, or other tracked activity in Runkeeper, the app records all the workout details including GPS route, elevation changes, and performance metrics.

2. Make.com checks for new activities

The workflow periodically polls Runkeeper's API (typically every 15-30 minutes) to detect newly completed workouts. You can adjust this frequency based on how quickly you want data to sync.

3. Data is formatted for Google Sheets

Make.com extracts the relevant activity fields and structures them into columns matching your spreadsheet format. The template includes common fields but you can customize which data points to include.

4. New row added to your spreadsheet

The workflow appends the workout data as a new row in your designated Google Sheet. Each activity gets timestamped so you can track trends over time.

Pro tip: Add calculated columns in your spreadsheet to derive insights like weekly mileage totals, pace improvements, or calories per workout session.

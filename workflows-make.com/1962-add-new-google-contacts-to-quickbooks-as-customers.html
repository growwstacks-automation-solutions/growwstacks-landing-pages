What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a common pain point for businesses using Google Contacts alongside QuickBooks - maintaining duplicate customer records across systems. Every time sales or support teams add a new contact in Google, accounting teams traditionally had to manually recreate that entry in QuickBooks, leading to errors, delays, and inconsistent data.

The workflow automatically creates a corresponding customer record in QuickBooks whenever a new contact gets added to Google Contacts. It maps standard fields like name, email, company, and phone numbers between the systems while maintaining each platform's required data structure. The sync happens in real-time, ensuring your accounting records always reflect current client relationships.

How It Works

1. Trigger on new Google Contact

The workflow monitors your Google Contacts for new additions using Make.com's instant trigger. No polling delays - it detects changes the moment they occur.

2. Extract contact details

Key information gets pulled from the new contact entry, including name fields, email addresses, phone numbers, company details, and any custom fields you've configured to sync.

3. Format for QuickBooks

The data gets transformed to match QuickBooks' customer record structure. This includes proper name parsing (separating first/last names), address formatting, and field mapping adjustments.

4. Create QuickBooks customer

Make.com authenticates with your QuickBooks account via OAuth and creates a new customer record using the prepared data. The system includes duplicate checking by default.

Pro tip: Add a conditional filter to only sync contacts tagged as "Clients" or with specific email domains. This prevents personal contacts from appearing in QuickBooks.

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical business problem: the disconnect between sales teams capturing new accounts in SalesForce and accounting teams needing those customers in QuickBooks. Manual data transfer between these systems creates errors, delays invoicing, and wastes valuable employee time.

The workflow automatically creates a new QuickBooks customer record whenever your sales team adds an account in SalesForce. It maps all essential billing information including company name, addresses, payment terms, and tax details - ensuring your accounting system stays perfectly synchronized with your CRM without any manual intervention.

How It Works

1. New account detection

The workflow continuously monitors your SalesForce instance for newly created account records. Using Make.com's real-time triggers, it detects new entries instantly without polling or scheduled checks.

2. Data validation and transformation

Before transferring to QuickBooks, the system validates required fields, reformats addresses to match accounting standards, and applies any business rules you've configured (like excluding prospect accounts).

3. QuickBooks customer creation

The verified SalesForce data automatically populates a new customer record in QuickBooks with all necessary billing information. The workflow handles API authentication and field mapping behind the scenes.

4. Sync confirmation

After successful creation, the system logs the transaction and can optionally notify your team or update the SalesForce record with the QuickBooks customer ID for future reference.

  1. Active SalesForce account with admin API access

  3. Make.com account (free tier sufficient for basic implementation)
  4. List of required customer fields in both systems
  5. Approved data mapping between SalesForce and QuickBooks fields

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone the template in your Make.com account
  2. Connect your SalesForce and QuickBooks accounts via OAuth
  3. Map SalesForce account fields to corresponding QuickBooks customer fields
  4. Configure any filters (e.g., only sync accounts with Opportunity Stage = Closed Won)
  5. Test with sample data to verify field mappings
  6. Activate the scenario for live operation

Pro tip: Create a custom field in SalesForce to store the QuickBooks Customer ID after sync completes. This creates a two-way reference that's valuable for future integrations.

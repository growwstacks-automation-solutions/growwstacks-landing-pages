What This Workflow Does

This automation eliminates the manual process of recreating orders in ShipStation after they've been entered in QuickBooks. When a new invoice is created in QuickBooks, the workflow automatically generates a corresponding shipping order in ShipStation with all relevant customer and product details.

Businesses using both QuickBooks for accounting and ShipStation for fulfillment typically waste 15-30 minutes per order manually transferring data between systems. This automation reduces that time to zero while eliminating common data entry errors that cause shipping delays and customer service issues.

How It Works

Step 1: QuickBooks Invoice Trigger

The workflow monitors your QuickBooks account for new invoices. You can configure it to watch for all invoices or only those meeting specific criteria (paid status, customer type, etc.).

Step 2: Data Mapping

Essential order details are extracted from the QuickBooks invoice including customer shipping address, product SKUs, quantities, and any special instructions. The workflow maps these fields to ShipStation's order format.

Step 3: ShipStation Order Creation

The workflow creates a new order in ShipStation using the mapped data. You can set default shipping methods, packaging preferences, and other fulfillment rules that apply automatically.

Pro tip: Add a filter step to exclude test invoices or orders from specific customers that don't require physical fulfillment.

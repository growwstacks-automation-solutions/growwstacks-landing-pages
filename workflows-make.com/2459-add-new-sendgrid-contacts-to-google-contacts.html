What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of marketing contacts being siloed in your email marketing platform while your team needs them in your central address book. Every time someone subscribes to your SendGrid email list, this workflow instantly creates a corresponding contact in Google Contacts.

Manual contact transfers between systems are time-consuming and prone to errors. This automated solution ensures your sales, support, and other teams immediately have access to new contacts without any manual data entry. The sync happens in real-time, keeping all your contact lists perfectly aligned.

How It Works

1. New contact trigger

The workflow monitors your SendGrid account for new contact additions. When someone subscribes to your email list or is manually added to your SendGrid contacts, it triggers the automation.

2. Contact data retrieval

Make.com retrieves all available information about the new contact from SendGrid, including name, email address, phone numbers, and any custom fields you've configured.

3. Data formatting

The contact information is formatted to match Google Contacts' expected structure. You can customize this mapping during setup to ensure all relevant fields are transferred correctly.

4. Google Contacts creation

The formatted contact data is sent to Google Contacts via API, creating a new entry in your specified contact group. The process typically completes within seconds of the original SendGrid addition.

Pro tip: Add a custom field in SendGrid to indicate the contact source, then map this to Google Contacts' notes field for better contact organization.

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of manually transferring meeting details from Google Calendar to documentation systems. Every time you or your team creates a new calendar event, Make.com automatically adds it as a new row in your specified Coda table.

The workflow eliminates repetitive copy-paste work while ensuring your Coda doc always reflects the most current meeting information. It captures all relevant event details including titles, descriptions, times, attendees, and locations - creating a searchable, shareable record outside your calendar.

How It Works

1. Trigger: New Google Calendar Event

The workflow monitors your specified Google Calendar for new events. When one is detected, it immediately captures all available event data.

2. Data Processing

Make.com formats the calendar data to match your Coda table structure, handling date conversions and field mappings automatically.

3. Action: Create Coda Row

The processed event data gets added as a new row in your designated Coda table, with all fields populated according to your configuration.

Pro tip: Set up a dedicated "Calendar Sync" table in Coda with columns matching the Google Calendar fields you want to track. This keeps your event data organized and easily filterable.

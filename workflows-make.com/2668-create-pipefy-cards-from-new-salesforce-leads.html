What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the critical challenge of manual lead handoff between marketing and sales teams. When a new lead is captured in Salesforce, sales teams often face delays in receiving and processing these leads, leading to missed opportunities and decreased conversion rates.

The workflow automatically creates a new card in Pipefy whenever a lead is created in Salesforce, ensuring instant transfer of lead information to your sales pipeline. This eliminates manual data entry, reduces response times, and ensures no lead falls through the cracks during the critical handoff process.

By automating this transition, sales teams can focus on what they do best—closing deals—rather than administrative tasks. The system maintains data integrity by transferring all relevant lead information, including contact details, lead score, source, and custom fields directly into Pipefy cards.

How It Works

Step 1: Salesforce Lead Detection

The workflow monitors Salesforce for new lead creations using Make.com's instant trigger. When a lead meets your qualification criteria (based on score, source, or custom fields), the automation immediately captures all relevant data fields.

Step 2: Data Mapping and Transformation

Lead information from Salesforce is mapped to corresponding fields in Pipefy. This includes contact information, company details, lead source, and any custom data points your sales team needs for effective qualification and follow-up.

Step 3: Pipefy Card Creation

The workflow creates a new card in your designated Pipefy pipeline, automatically populating all mapped fields. The card can be assigned to specific team members, tagged with appropriate labels, and placed in the correct pipeline phase based on your sales process.

Step 4: Notification and Follow-up

Optionally, the system can trigger notifications to sales representatives or managers when new cards are created, ensuring immediate awareness of new lead opportunities entering the pipeline.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Connect your Salesforce account to Make.com using OAuth authentication
  2. Set up your Pipefy connection with appropriate API permissions
  3. Configure the Salesforce trigger to monitor new lead creations
  4. Map Salesforce lead fields to corresponding Pipefy card fields
  5. Set up any filters to only process qualified leads
  6. Test the workflow with sample lead data
  7. Activate the automation and monitor initial transactions

Pro tip: Start with a small subset of highly qualified leads to test your mapping logic before rolling out to all leads. This helps identify any field mapping issues before they affect your entire sales pipeline.

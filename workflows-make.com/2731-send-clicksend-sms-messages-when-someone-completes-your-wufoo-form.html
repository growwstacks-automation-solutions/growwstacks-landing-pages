What This Workflow Does

This automation instantly bridges your Wufoo forms with ClickSend's SMS messaging platform. Whenever a customer, client, or visitor submits your form, the system automatically dispatches a text message to your specified recipients. It eliminates the delay and potential human error of manual notification processes.

Common use cases include appointment confirmations for service businesses, order acknowledgments for e-commerce stores, emergency alert systems for schools, or internal notifications for field teams. The immediate nature of SMS makes it far more effective than email for time-sensitive communications.

How It Works

1. Form Submission Trigger

The workflow activates whenever someone completes and submits your Wufoo form. Make.com monitors your form in real-time through Wufoo's API connection.

2. Data Processing

The system extracts relevant form fields like phone numbers, customer names, or appointment details. You can configure which data points to include in your SMS messages.

3. SMS Message Assembly

Your predefined message template combines static text with dynamic variables from the form submission. For example: "Hi [Name], your [Service] appointment is confirmed for [Date] at [Time]."

4. ClickSend Delivery

Make.com securely transmits the composed message to ClickSend's API, which handles actual SMS delivery through its global carrier network to the recipient's mobile device.

What You'll Need

  1. An active Wufoo account with at least one form
  2. A ClickSend account with SMS credits
  3. A Make.com account (free tier works for basic usage)
  4. The phone number(s) that should receive notifications
  5. Your SMS message template (can include form field variables)

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Clone this template in your Make.com account
  2. Connect your Wufoo account in the "Watch Form Entries" module
  3. Select which form should trigger the workflow
  4. Configure your ClickSend connection with API credentials
  5. Set the destination phone number(s) - can be static or form field
  6. Compose your SMS message template using available variables
  7. Test with a form submission and activate the workflow

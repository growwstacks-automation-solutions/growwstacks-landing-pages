What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of delayed communication after form submissions. When customers complete your Jotform (whether it's for appointments, registrations, or inquiries), they often wait anxiously for confirmation. Manual processes where staff must check submissions and send texts create frustrating delays.

The workflow instantly bridges Jotform and ClickSend to deliver SMS confirmations within seconds of form submission. It captures all form data while automatically sending pre-written or customized messages to specified recipients. This creates professional, timely communication without any staff intervention.

How It Works

1. Form Submission Trigger

Make.com monitors your Jotform for new submissions in real-time. Each time someone completes your form, it instantly captures all response data including custom fields.

2. Data Processing

The workflow extracts key information like phone numbers, customer names, or reference numbers from the form responses. This data gets formatted for SMS delivery.

3. SMS Message Assembly

Your predefined message template combines static text with dynamic form data. For example: "Hi {Name}, we received your {Service} request (#{ID}). We'll contact you within 24 hours."

4. ClickSend Delivery

Make.com authenticates with your ClickSend account and transmits the composed message. ClickSend handles carrier delivery globally with real-time status updates.

Pro tip: Add conditional logic to send different SMS templates based on form responses. For example, urgent requests get priority messaging while standard submissions receive regular confirmations.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Copy this template to your Make.com account
  2. Connect your Jotform account in the "Watch Responses" module
  3. Enter your ClickSend API credentials in the SMS module
  4. Customize the message template using form field placeholders
  5. Specify recipient numbers (can be from form data or fixed)
  6. Run a test submission and verify message delivery
  7. Activate the scenario for live use

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical challenge for customer support teams: ensuring every customer conversation gets proper follow-up. When support requests come through Help Scout, they often need to be converted into actionable tasks for your team. Manually creating Trello cards for each conversation is time-consuming and prone to human error.

The workflow automatically monitors your Help Scout mailbox for new conversations and creates corresponding Trello cards in your specified board. Each card contains relevant conversation details, allowing your team to immediately see what needs attention without switching between apps. This creates a seamless bridge between customer communication and task management.

How It Works

1. New conversation detection

The workflow constantly monitors your Help Scout mailbox through Make.com's instant triggers. As soon as a new conversation is created (whether through email, chat, or form submission), the automation kicks in.

2. Data extraction

Key information is pulled from the Help Scout conversation including customer details, subject line, message content, and any tags or custom fields. This data is structured for transfer to Trello.

3. Card creation

The workflow creates a new card in your specified Trello board, placing it in the appropriate list (like "To Do" or "In Progress"). The card includes all relevant conversation details as well as a direct link back to the Help Scout thread.

Pro tip: Configure the workflow to add labels in Trello based on Help Scout tags or mailbox. This makes it easy to categorize and prioritize support requests at a glance.

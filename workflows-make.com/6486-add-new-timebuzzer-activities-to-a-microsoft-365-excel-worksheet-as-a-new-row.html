Automatically Add timeBuzzer Activities to Excel

What This Workflow Does

For agencies, consultants, and remote teams, tracking billable hours is crucial for profitability. However, the process of manually copying time entries from a tracking app like timeBuzzer into a spreadsheet for reporting, invoicing, or analysis is tedious, error-prone, and wastes valuable time. This manual gap often leads to lost revenue from unbilled hours and delays in project insights.

This automation solves that problem completely. It creates a seamless, real-time bridge between your timeBuzzer account and a Microsoft 365 Excel workbook. Every time a team member logs a new activity—whether it's client work, internal meetings, or project tasks—the workflow instantly captures that data and appends it as a new, structured row in your designated Excel worksheet. This turns your spreadsheet into a live, centralized log of all time tracking data without any human intervention.

How It Works

The workflow is a simple yet powerful one-way sync, triggered by new data in timeBuzzer and resulting in a new record in Excel.

Step 1: Monitor timeBuzzer for New Activities

The automation starts with a "Watch Activities" module in Make.com, which polls your timeBuzzer account at regular intervals. The moment a new activity is created—containing details like project name, task description, start/end time, and duration—it triggers the workflow.

Step 2: Structure the Data for Excel

Make.com then maps the incoming data from timeBuzzer into the correct format for your Excel sheet. It organizes the information into specific columns, such as Date, Employee, Client, Project, Task Description, Duration, and Billable Status. This ensures the data lands in your spreadsheet cleanly and consistently, ready for analysis.

Step 3: Add a New Row to Your Excel Worksheet

Finally, the workflow uses the "Add a Row" action for Microsoft 365 Excel. It connects to your specific Excel file in OneDrive or SharePoint, selects the correct worksheet, and inserts the newly formatted time entry as the next available row. The process is complete in seconds, providing an up-to-the-minute audit trail.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated "Raw Data" sheet in Excel for this automation. You can then use other sheets for PivotTables, dashboards, and summaries that reference this master log, keeping your reporting dynamic and your source data intact.

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes:

  2. Connect timeBuzzer: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your timeBuzzer account.

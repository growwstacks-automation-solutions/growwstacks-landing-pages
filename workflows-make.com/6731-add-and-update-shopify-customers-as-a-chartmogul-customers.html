What This Workflow Does

For subscription businesses using Shopify for billing and ChartMogul for analytics, a major operational headache is keeping customer data synchronized between the two systems. Manually exporting from Shopify and importing into ChartMogul is error-prone, time-consuming, and creates a lag in your critical metrics like MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue) and churn rate.

This automation solves that problem by creating a real-time bridge between Shopify and ChartMogul. Every time a new customer is created in Shopify (e.g., from a subscription purchase), this Make.com workflow automatically adds that customer as a new entry in ChartMogul. More importantly, it also handles updates—if a customer changes their email, plan, or billing information in Shopify, the workflow finds the existing record in ChartMogul and updates it, ensuring your analytics platform always reflects the truth.

The result is a single, accurate source of customer data for your subscription analytics. You eliminate manual data entry, reduce errors, and empower your team with real-time insights into customer lifetime value, cohort performance, and revenue trends.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a trigger-response model, listening for events in Shopify and taking precise actions in ChartMogul.

Step 1: Monitor Shopify for Customer Events

The workflow starts by watching your Shopify store for specific customer-related events. This is typically configured using Shopify's webhooks or the "Watch Customers" module in Make.com. It triggers whenever a new customer is created or an existing customer's details are updated.

Step 2: Retrieve and Structure Customer Data

Once triggered, the workflow collects all relevant data about that customer from Shopify. This includes their email address, first and last name, unique Shopify ID, subscription plan details, signup date, and any custom tags or notes. This data is then formatted into the structure that ChartMogul's API expects for a customer object.

Step 3: Check for Existing Customer in ChartMogul

Before creating a new record, the workflow performs a smart check. It searches ChartMogul for a customer with a matching email address or a custom identifier (like the Shopify Customer ID). This step determines whether the action is an "Add" or an "Update."

Step 4: Add or Update the ChartMogul Customer

Based on the search result, the workflow executes the appropriate action. If no match is found, it creates a new customer in ChartMogul with all the collected data. If a match is found, it updates that existing ChartMogul customer record with the new information from Shopify, ensuring the two systems stay in sync.

Pro tip: Use the Shopify Customer ID as the external ID in ChartMogul. This creates a reliable, unique link between the two records, making updates and matching foolproof even if a customer changes their email address.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automated sync running in under 30 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Shopify: In the first module, authenticate and select your Shopify store. Configure the trigger to watch for customer creations/updates.
  3. Connect ChartMogul: In the ChartMogul modules, enter your API key from the ChartMogul dashboard. This allows Make.com to add and update customers.
  4. Map the Data Fields: Review the data mapping between Shopify and ChartMogul. Ensure key fields like email, external ID (Shopify ID), and plan are correctly connected.
  5. Test with a Live Customer: Turn on the scenario and create a test customer in Shopify. Verify that the customer appears correctly in your ChartMogul account.
  6. Schedule & Activate: Set the scenario to run in real-time (via webhook) or on a frequent schedule (e.g., every hour). Activate it to start syncing.

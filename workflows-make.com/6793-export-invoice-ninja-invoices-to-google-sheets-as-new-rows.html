What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Invoice Ninja, manually copying invoice data into a spreadsheet is a tedious, error-prone task. This automation solves that by instantly adding every new invoice created in Invoice Ninja as a new row in your Google Sheets spreadsheet. It captures key details like client name, invoice number, amount, date, and status, building a live, centralized financial log.

The primary value is eliminating repetitive data entry, which saves several hours per month for freelancers, small teams, and accounting departments. It also ensures your spreadsheet is always current, providing accurate data for reporting, cash flow analysis, and client tracking. You gain real-time visibility into your invoicing activity without any extra work.

This template is designed as a simple, reliable connector. It uses a pre-defined Google Sheets structure, so you get a clean, organized dataset ready for analysis. Whether you're reconciling accounts, preparing tax documents, or simply keeping a client ledger, this automation turns a manual chore into a seamless background process.

How It Works

1. Trigger: New Invoice in Invoice Ninja

The workflow starts when a new invoice is created within your Invoice Ninja account. Make.com monitors this event via a webhook or API connection, instantly detecting the new record.

2. Data Extraction & Mapping

The automation pulls the relevant fields from the invoice—such as client details, invoice number, total amount, issue date, due date, and payment status. It maps these fields to corresponding columns in your Google Sheets template.

3. Action: Add Row to Google Sheets

Make.com then creates a new row in your specified Google Sheets spreadsheet, inserting the extracted invoice data. The row is appended to the sheet, maintaining a chronological record of all invoices.

4. Completion & Logging

The process completes within seconds. You can optionally add a logging step to record the sync in Make.com’s history, providing an audit trail for each automated entry.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated “Invoices Log” sheet with columns matching Invoice Ninja’s fields. This makes reporting and filtering much easier later.

Save New Signed Waivers to a Google Sheets Spreadsheet

Automatically log every signed waiver from WaiverFile into a centralized spreadsheet. Eliminate manual entry, ensure accuracy, and streamline your compliance records.

What This Workflow Does

For businesses that rely on liability waivers—like gyms, adventure parks, event organizers, and healthcare providers—manually tracking signed documents is a tedious and error-prone administrative burden. Staff must constantly check an e-signature platform, copy participant details, and paste them into a spreadsheet, which wastes time and risks data entry mistakes.

This automation solves that problem entirely. It creates a seamless bridge between WaiverFile (your e-signature collection point) and Google Sheets (your record-keeping system). Every time a new waiver is signed, the workflow instantly triggers, captures the signer's data, and appends it as a new, organized row in your designated spreadsheet. This turns a reactive, manual task into a proactive, automated process that runs 24/7.

The result is a live, searchable database of all your participants. You gain instant visibility into who has signed, when they signed, and what they agreed to, all without lifting a finger. This not only saves hours per week but also strengthens your legal compliance and customer service capabilities.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple "watch and record" principle, using Make.com to connect two powerful cloud applications.

Step 1: Detecting a New Signature

The automation starts with a WaiverFile module set to "Watch Signed Waivers." This module acts as a sentinel, continuously monitoring your WaiverFile account for new completed signatures. The moment a participant submits a waiver, Make.com detects this event and immediately pulls the associated data packet.

Step 2: Structuring the Data

Make.com then parses the incoming data from WaiverFile. This data typically includes fields like the signer's full name, email address, phone number, signature timestamp, IP address, and answers to any custom questions on the waiver form. The workflow maps each of these relevant data points to a corresponding variable.

Pro tip: You can customize which fields are captured. Focus on the data you actually need for reporting or follow-up, such as email for marketing or emergency contact for safety records.

Step 3: Writing to Your Spreadsheet

Finally, the workflow uses a Google Sheets module to "Add a Row" to a pre-defined spreadsheet. It takes the mapped variables from Step 2 and places each one into its designated column. Within seconds of the waiver being signed, a complete record appears in your sheet, with all data correctly formatted and aligned.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario editor, click on the WaiverFile module and connect your account by following the OAuth prompts. Repeat the process for the Google Sheets module.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Ensure the WaiverFile module is set to watch the correct "form" or waiver template from which you want to capture signatures.
  4. Map Your Spreadsheet: In the Google Sheets module, select your target spreadsheet and worksheet. Then, map each data field from WaiverFile (like `signer.email`) to the correct column header in your sheet (like "Email Address").
  5. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario and sign a test waiver in WaiverFile. Within moments, check your Google Sheet to confirm a new row was added correctly. Once verified, your automation is live.

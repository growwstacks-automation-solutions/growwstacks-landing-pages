What This Workflow Does

For professionals using the Pomodoro technique, manually logging completed sessions into a calendar is a tedious, often forgotten step. This breaks the continuity of your productivity data and makes it hard to analyze how you actually spend your time. Without automation, you might have dozens of focused sessions per week that never get recorded, leaving your calendar empty of actual work and filled only with meetings.

This workflow solves that by creating a seamless bridge between your time tracking and your scheduling tool. Every time you finish a focused timer in PomoDoneApp—whether for a client project, deep learning, or creative work—Make.com instantly captures the details and creates a corresponding event in your Google Calendar. The event includes the task name, duration, and start/end times, turning your effort into a visual, historical record.

The result is a calendar that truthfully reflects your work, not just your appointments. This is invaluable for freelancers tracking billable hours, students reviewing study patterns, or anyone wanting to improve their time management through data. It eliminates the post-session administrative task, keeping you in a state of flow.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger on Timer Stop

The workflow starts when PomoDoneApp sends a notification that a timer has stopped. Make.com watches for this specific event via the PomoDoneApp module, capturing key data like the task name, timer duration, project category, and the exact timestamp the session ended.

Step 2: Process Session Data

Make.com then processes this data. It calculates the start time by subtracting the duration from the stop time. It can also format the task name, add tags or labels based on the project, and prepare a clean description for the calendar event. This step ensures the calendar entry is informative and well-structured.

Step 3: Create Google Calendar Event

Finally, the workflow uses the Google Calendar module to create a new event in your specified calendar. The event title is the task you worked on, the description can include additional notes from PomoDoneApp, and the time block is set precisely to the start and end of your Pomodoro session. The event is created instantly, appearing in your calendar within seconds.

Pro tip: Use a dedicated calendar (like "Work Sessions") for these automated events. This keeps your main calendar clean and allows you to easily filter or generate reports on your focused time alone.

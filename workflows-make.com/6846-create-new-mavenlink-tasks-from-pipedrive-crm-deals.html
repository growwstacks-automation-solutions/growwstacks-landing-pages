What This Workflow Does

Every day, sales teams spend hours manually searching LinkedIn Sales Navigator, copying profile details into spreadsheets, and trying to decide which leads are worth pursuing. This process is not only tedious but also inconsistent—what one rep considers a “hot lead” might be ignored by another. The result is missed opportunities, wasted time, and a pipeline that’s more guesswork than strategy.

This automated workflow solves that by turning LinkedIn searches into a structured, scored lead list in Google Sheets. Instead of manual copying, the system captures each profile, pulls company size and industry, checks for keywords, and assigns a qualification score. It logs everything in a central sheet, ready for outreach, and can even trigger follow-up tasks in your CRM or email tool. You get a qualified lead database without the manual effort.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger from LinkedIn Sales Navigator

You run a targeted search in Sales Navigator—for example, “Marketing Directors in SaaS companies with 100–500 employees.” The workflow watches for new search results matching your filters. When a new prospect appears, it captures their name, headline, location, and profile URL automatically.

Step 2: Data Enrichment & Scoring

For each captured profile, the workflow enriches the data by looking up company details (size, industry, website) and scans the headline and summary for qualification keywords. It then applies a scoring algorithm based on job title relevance, company size, industry match, and keyword density to produce a lead score from 1 to 10.

Step 3: Log into Google Sheets

The enriched lead data—including name, company, industry, location, profile URL, and calculated score—is written as a new row in a designated Google Sheet. The sheet can be sorted by score, allowing your team to instantly see which prospects to contact first.

Step 4: Optional Follow-up Actions

Based on the score, the workflow can create a task in your CRM, add the lead to a sequence in outreach software, or send a notification to your sales channel. This creates a closed-loop system where LinkedIn prospecting flows directly into your sales process.

Pro tip: Use LinkedIn’s “Saved Leads” feature as your trigger source. This ensures you only capture prospects you’ve already manually reviewed and marked as interesting, adding a layer of human validation before automation takes over.

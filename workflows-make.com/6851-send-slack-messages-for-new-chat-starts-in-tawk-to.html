What This Workflow Does

Live chat is one of the most powerful tools for converting website visitors into customers, but its effectiveness hinges on speed. If your team isn't actively watching the tawk.to dashboard, critical leads can slip away in the seconds it takes for a visitor to lose patience. This automation solves that problem by creating a direct, real-time bridge between your tawk.to chat widget and your team's communication hub in Slack.

Every time a new chat session begins on your website, this Make.com workflow instantly triggers. It captures the chat details—like the visitor's name, the page they're on, and their initial message—and formats them into a clear, actionable Slack notification. This means your sales or support team gets a ping in the channel they monitor all day, enabling them to jump into the conversation within seconds, not minutes. The result is a dramatic improvement in first-response time, lead capture rate, and overall customer experience, all without requiring manual monitoring.

How It Works

The automation is a straightforward, reliable sequence that operates silently in the background.

Step 1: Monitor tawk.to for New Chats

The workflow uses tawk.to's API via Make.com to watch for the "chat start" event. This is a passive trigger that activates the moment a visitor clicks your chat widget and begins typing, ensuring zero delay in detection.

Step 2: Gather Chat Context

Once triggered, the automation pulls all relevant data from the new chat. This includes the visitor's name (if provided), email address, the URL of the page they are on, their initial question, and a unique link to directly access the chat within the tawk.to dashboard.

Step 3: Format & Send the Slack Alert

The collected data is then structured into a rich, readable Slack message. The workflow posts this message to a designated Slack channel (or direct message) you choose. The alert is designed to give your team immediate context so they can provide a personalized, informed response right away.

Pro tip: Customize your Slack message to include emojis (like 🔥 for a hot lead) or specific formatting. You can even set up different channels for different types of inquiries—like routing sales questions to #sales-leads and support issues to #help-desk—by adding a simple filter module in Make.com.

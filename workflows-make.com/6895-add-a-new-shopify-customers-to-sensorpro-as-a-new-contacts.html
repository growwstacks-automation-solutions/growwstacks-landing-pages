What This Workflow Does

For e-commerce businesses using Shopify, every new customer represents a potential long-term relationship. However, if your customer data is trapped solely in Shopify, your sales and support teams in Sensorpro are working with an incomplete picture. Manually copying customer details between platforms is a tedious, error-prone task that wastes valuable time and leads to data decay.

This automated workflow solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between Shopify and Sensorpro. Whenever a new customer completes a purchase or creates an account in your Shopify store, this Make.com scenario instantly captures their information—name, email, phone, address—and creates a corresponding, fully populated contact record in your Sensorpro CRM. It turns a multi-step manual process into a silent, reliable background operation.

The result is a unified customer database. Your marketing team can segment audiences based on purchase history, your support team has immediate context for incoming queries, and your sales team can follow up with leads without switching between apps. The automation ensures no customer falls through the cracks due to human oversight.

How It Works

The workflow follows a simple "trigger-action" sequence, orchestrated by Make.com to ensure reliability and speed.

Step 1: Monitoring Shopify for New Customers

The scenario starts with a Shopify module set to watch for the "Create a customer" event. This trigger can be configured to fire whenever a customer registers an account or completes a checkout as a guest. Make.com polls your Shopify store at regular intervals or uses webhooks for instant notification, ensuring minimal delay.

Step 2: Extracting and Structuring Customer Data

Once triggered, the workflow pulls the complete set of data for the new customer. This includes their first and last name, email address, phone number, default shipping and billing addresses, and any notes or tags assigned in Shopify. The data is then parsed and formatted to match the expected field structure in Sensorpro.

Pro tip: Use this step to filter or enrich data. For example, you can add a rule to only sync customers from specific countries or append a "Source: Shopify" tag to the contact in Sensorpro for better tracking.

Step 3: Creating the Contact in Sensorpro

The formatted data is sent to Sensorpro via its API using the "Create a contact" action. The workflow maps each piece of Shopify data (e.g., customer.email) to the correct Sensorpro contact field (e.g., Email). A successful creation returns the new Sensorpro contact ID, which can be logged for audit trails.

Step 4: Error Handling and Logging

Robust automation includes error management. If the Sensorpro API call fails—perhaps due to a duplicate email or a server issue—the workflow can be set to retry, send an alert to your team via email or Slack, or log the error to a spreadsheet for later review. This ensures you're always informed of sync issues.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template in your Make.com account.
  2. Authorize Shopify: In the first module, click to add a connection. You'll be redirected to log into your Shopify store and grant Make.com the necessary permissions to read customers.
  3. Authorize Sensorpro: In the Sensorpro module, create a new connection using your Sensorpro API key (found in your Sensorpro settings under Integrations/API).
  4. Map the Fields (Optional): Review the data mapping between Shopify and Sensorpro. The template provides a standard mapping, but you can customize it if your Sensorpro uses custom field names.
  5. Test and Activate: Turn on the scenario and perform a test by creating a dummy customer in Shopify. Check Sensorpro to confirm a new contact appears. Once verified, your automation is live.

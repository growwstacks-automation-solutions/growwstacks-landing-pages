What This Workflow Does

For podcast creators using Transistor.fm, growing an audience is exciting, but managing subscriber data can become a tedious administrative task. Every time a listener subscribes, their information—email, name, subscription date—needs to be recorded. Manually copying this data from Transistor.fm into a spreadsheet is time-consuming, prone to errors, and often gets delayed, leaving you with an incomplete picture of your audience growth.

This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between your podcast hosting platform and your data hub. Whenever a new subscriber joins your podcast on Transistor.fm, this workflow instantly captures their details and adds them as a new row in a designated Google Sheets spreadsheet. It turns a manual, repetitive process into an automatic, reliable data pipeline.

The business value is clear: you save hours each month on data entry, ensure your subscriber list is always accurate and up-to-date, and create a centralized dataset ready for analysis. You can finally track subscription trends, measure campaign effectiveness, and understand your audience demographics without any extra work.

How It Works

The workflow is a simple yet powerful chain of events triggered by a new subscriber event.

Step 1: Trigger on New Subscriber

The automation starts with the "Watch Subscribers" module in Make.com, connected to your Transistor.fm account. It monitors your podcast for new subscription events. As soon as someone subscribes, the workflow is activated, and the subscriber's data is passed to the next step.

Step 2: Map the Subscriber Data

The workflow then structures the incoming data. It extracts key fields like the subscriber's email address, their name (if provided), the exact date and time of subscription, and the podcast they subscribed to. This data is formatted neatly for insertion into a spreadsheet.

Step 3: Add Row to Google Sheets

Finally, the formatted data is sent to Google Sheets via the "Add a Row" module. The workflow specifies your target spreadsheet and sheet, and inserts the subscriber's details as a new row. Each new subscriber creates a new, timestamped entry in your log.

Pro tip: You can extend this workflow easily. For example, after adding the row to Sheets, you could trigger a personalized welcome email via Gmail or Mailchimp, or add the subscriber to a CRM like HubSpot, creating a full-fledged onboarding sequence.

Quick Setup Guide

Configuring this template is straightforward. Follow these steps to get it running in minutes.

  1. Duplicate the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template on Make.com. Click "Duplicate" to copy it into your own Make.com workspace.
  2. Connect Transistor.fm: In the duplicated scenario, click the Transistor.fm module. You'll be prompted to create a connection. Log in to your Transistor.fm account and authorize Make.com to access your subscriber data.
  3. Set Up Google Sheets: Click the Google Sheets module. Create a connection to your Google account. Then, specify the exact spreadsheet ID and sheet name where you want the data to go. You can use the sample spreadsheet link provided in the template description as a reference.
  4. Map the Data Fields: Ensure the data from Transistor.fm (like email, name, date) is correctly mapped to the corresponding columns in your Google Sheets. The template is pre-configured, but you can adjust the column mapping if your sheet has a different structure.
  5. Test and Activate: Turn on the scenario. To test, you can simulate a new subscriber event or wait for a real subscription. Check your Google Sheets to confirm a new row appears with the correct data. Once verified, the automation will run continuously.

Pro tip: Before activating, make a copy of the sample Google Sheets spreadsheet (linked in the template description) and use it as your destination. It has the perfect column structure ready to receive the data, saving you setup time.

What This Workflow Does

For podcasters and content creators, growing an audience means collecting emails from various sources—website forms, lead magnets, live events, or partnerships. Manually copying and pasting this data from spreadsheets into your podcast hosting platform like Transistor.fm is tedious, error-prone, and steals time from content creation.

This automation solves that problem. It acts as a silent assistant that periodically checks a designated Google Sheets spreadsheet for new subscriber entries. Whenever a new row is added—containing details like email, name, and optional custom fields—the workflow instantly creates a corresponding subscriber in your Transistor.fm account. This ensures your audience list is always current, enabling timely communication and better listener engagement without any manual intervention.

The process is scheduled to run automatically every 15 minutes (configurable), providing a near real-time sync. It eliminates data silos, reduces the risk of human error, and ensures no potential listener falls through the cracks because someone forgot to update the list.

How It Works

The workflow follows a simple, reliable sequence to bridge your data collection point (Sheets) and your audience management platform (Transistor.fm).

Step 1: Monitor Google Sheets for New Rows

The automation starts by connecting to your Google Sheets spreadsheet. It checks the specified worksheet for any rows that have been added since its last run. It intelligently tracks the last processed entry to avoid duplicates, ensuring each subscriber is added only once.

Step 2: Parse and Validate Subscriber Data

For each new row found, the workflow extracts the relevant data columns, typically email address and name. It can also handle additional metadata you might collect. The data is formatted and prepared to meet Transistor.fm's API requirements for creating a subscriber.

Pro tip: Structure your Google Sheet with clear column headers (e.g., 'Email', 'First Name'). This makes mapping the data in the automation straightforward and foolproof.

Step 3: Create Subscriber in Transistor.fm

Using the Transistor.fm module, the workflow sends an API call to create a new subscriber with the data from the spreadsheet. The subscriber is instantly added to your Transistor.fm dashboard, ready to receive updates, newsletters, or podcast notifications as you've configured.

Step 4: Log and Schedule the Next Run

After processing, the workflow can log the action for your records. It then waits for the next scheduled interval (default 15 minutes) before checking the spreadsheet again, creating a continuous, hands-off loop.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes by following these steps.

Pro tip: Before you start, make a copy of the sample Google Sheet linked in the template description. This gives you a correctly formatted starting point.

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above. This will open the template in your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Google Sheets: In the first module, click to add a connection. Authorize Make.com to access the Google Sheet where your subscriber data lives.
  3. Connect Transistor.fm: In the second module, add your Transistor.fm API key (found in your Transistor.fm account under Settings > API) to establish the connection.
  4. Map Your Data: Point the Google Sheets module to your specific spreadsheet and worksheet. Then, map the spreadsheet columns (e.g., column A for email) to the corresponding fields in the Transistor.fm "Create a Subscriber" module.
  5. Set the Schedule: The scenario is set to run every 15 minutes by default. You can adjust this schedule in the Make.com scheduler to run hourly, daily, or on any custom interval that fits your needs.
  6. Turn it On: Save your scenario and toggle the switch to "ON." The automation will run immediately for the first time and then on your chosen schedule.

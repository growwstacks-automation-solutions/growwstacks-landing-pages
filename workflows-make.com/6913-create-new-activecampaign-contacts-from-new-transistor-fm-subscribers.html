What This Workflow Does

For podcasters and content creators, growing an audience is only half the battle. The real challenge is building a lasting relationship with your listeners. If you're using Transistor.fm to host your show and ActiveCampaign for email marketing, you likely face a manual, error-prone process: exporting subscriber lists from Transistor and importing them into ActiveCampaign. This creates delays, risks data loss, and misses the critical window to engage a new fan when their interest is highest.

This automated workflow solves that problem completely. It acts as a silent bridge between your podcast host and your CRM. Every time someone subscribes to your show on Transistor.fm, this system instantly captures their details—like their email address and name—and creates a new, fully-fledged contact in your specified ActiveCampaign account. It happens in the background, in real-time, with zero effort from you or your team.

The result is a seamless listener onboarding experience. New subscribers can immediately be added to a welcome email sequence, tagged based on the podcast they subscribed to, or enrolled in a lead-nurturing campaign, all without you lifting a finger. This transforms your podcast from a passive broadcasting tool into an active lead generation engine.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple, reliable sequence triggered by a new subscriber event.

Step 1: The Trigger – A New Subscriber Joins

The workflow is activated the moment Transistor.fm registers a new subscriber for your podcast. This could be via Apple Podcasts, Spotify, or directly on your website. Make.com receives this event instantly through a webhook, capturing all available data about the listener.

Step 2: Data Routing & Preparation

The workflow router takes the incoming data package. It checks for essential information like the subscriber's email address—the most critical piece for CRM integration. It can also map other available fields, such as first name or subscription source, preparing them for the next step.

Pro tip: Use this step to add custom tags in ActiveCampaign based on the podcast name. For example, tag subscribers of "The Marketing Show" differently from "The Leadership Podcast" to enable hyper-targeted email campaigns later.

Step 3: Creating the ActiveCampaign Contact

Finally, the workflow uses the prepared data to make an API call to your ActiveCampaign account. It creates a new contact with the subscriber's email and any mapped fields. You can configure it to add the contact to a specific list (like "Podcast Subscribers") and apply relevant tags automatically, ensuring they enter your marketing funnel correctly from day one.

What You'll Need

  1. A Transistor.fm account hosting at least one podcast.
  2. An ActiveCampaign account with API access enabled. You'll need your API URL and Key from the ActiveCampaign settings.
  3. A Make.com account (free tier is sufficient to set up and test this scenario).
  4. Basic information about which ActiveCampaign list you want new contacts to join.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" and duplicate the scenario into your own Make.com workspace.
  2. Connect Transistor.fm: In the first module, authenticate your Transistor.fm account by following Make.com's OAuth steps.
  3. Configure ActiveCampaign: In the final module, connect your ActiveCampaign account using your API credentials (URL and Key).
  4. Map Your Data: Ensure the email field from Transistor.fm is correctly mapped to the email field in ActiveCampaign. Optionally, map the first name or other custom fields.
  5. Set the List & Tags: Specify the exact ActiveCampaign list ID for new contacts and add any static tags (e.g., "Podcast_Subscriber").
  6. Turn it On: Activate the scenario. It will now listen for new Transistor.fm subscribers and process them automatically.

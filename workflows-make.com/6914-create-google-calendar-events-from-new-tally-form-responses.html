What This Workflow Does

If you use Tally forms for booking appointments, consultations, or events, you know the tedious process that follows: someone submits their preferred time, and you must manually copy their name, email, date, and time into Google Calendar. This back-and-forth wastes valuable time and introduces errors, leading to missed appointments, double-bookings, and a poor client experience.

This automation solves that problem completely. Every time a new response comes through your Tally form, this Make.com workflow automatically creates a corresponding event in your Google Calendar. It extracts the attendee details, meeting title, date, time, and any notes, then schedules the event and can even send the calendar invite directly to the client. The process runs automatically every 15 minutes, ensuring your calendar is always synchronized with your latest bookings without any manual intervention.

Pro tip: Use this workflow not just for client bookings, but also for internal team meetings, workshop registrations, or interview scheduling. The flexibility of Tally's form builder combined with Google Calendar's reliability creates a powerful, hands-free scheduling system.

How It Works

The automation follows a clean, logical sequence to transform a form submission into a calendar event.

Step 1: Monitor for New Tally Responses

The workflow is triggered on a schedule (every 15 minutes by default) to check your Tally form for any new submissions. Make.com connects to your Tally account via API and retrieves only the latest responses that haven't been processed yet.

Step 2: Parse and Validate Form Data

Each response is broken down into its component fields. The system looks for specific data points required for a calendar event: the attendee's name and email, the meeting start date and time, the event title, and an optional description. Basic validation ensures the date and time are in a correct format before proceeding.

Step 3: Create the Google Calendar Event

Using the parsed data, the workflow calls the Google Calendar API to create a new event. It sets the title, start and end times (you can configure default duration), adds the client as a guest, and populates the description with any additional notes from the form. The event is created in your specified calendar.

Step 4: Confirm and Log (Optional)

After successful creation, the workflow can be extended to send a confirmation message back to you via email or Slack, or to log the booking in a Google Sheet for record-keeping. This step provides visibility and ensures every submission is accounted for.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Copy the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to duplicate the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario, establish the connections to your Tally account and Google Calendar. This is a one-time, secure OAuth process.
  3. Map Your Form Fields: Point the workflow to your specific Tally form and map the Tally question fields (like "What's your name?") to the corresponding data points for the calendar event (like "Event attendee").
  4. Configure Event Details: Set the default calendar, event duration, and how you want the event title to be formatted (e.g., "Call with [Client Name]").
  5. Test and Activate: Run a test by submitting your Tally form. Check that an event appears correctly in a test calendar. Then, schedule the scenario to run automatically and turn it on.

What This Workflow Does

Running viral referral campaigns or contests with UpViral is a powerful way to grow your audience, but the resulting flood of new contact data can become a logistical headache. Manually checking the UpViral dashboard and copying each new name, email, and campaign detail into a spreadsheet is tedious, error-prone, and steals valuable time from engaging with those very leads.

This automation solves that problem completely. It acts as a silent bridge between your UpViral account and Google Sheets. Once set up, it periodically checks your specified UpViral campaign for new participants. Whenever a new contact is detected, it instantly captures their information and appends it as a new, organized row in your chosen Google Sheets spreadsheet. This happens automatically, on a schedule you control, ensuring your lead list is always current without you lifting a finger.

The result is a clean, centralized database of all your campaign participants in Google Sheets, ready for analysis, segmentation, or integration with the next steps in your marketing or sales funnel.

How It Works

The workflow follows a simple, reliable sequence to transfer data from point A (UpViral) to point B (Google Sheets).

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

The automation is initiated by a schedule module. By default, it's set to run every 15 minutes, constantly "listening" for new activity. You can easily adjust this frequency to hourly, daily, or any interval that matches the pace of your campaign.

Step 2: Fetch New UpViral Contacts

When the workflow runs, it connects to your UpViral account via the Make.com UpViral module. It queries your selected campaign and retrieves a list of contacts. The logic is designed to identify only the newest entries since the last time it checked, ensuring no duplicate data is processed.

Step 3: Process and Map Data

Each new contact's data packet is processed. The workflow maps the relevant fields from UpViral—such as First Name, Email, Campaign Name, Entry Date, and any custom fields—to the corresponding column headers in your Google Sheets. This structuring is crucial for clean, usable data.

Step 4: Add Row to Google Sheets

Finally, the workflow uses the Google Sheets module to add a new row to the bottom of your specified spreadsheet. It populates each cell in that row with the mapped data from the UpViral contact. The process repeats for every new contact found in that cycle.

Pro tip: Use the provided example spreadsheet as a guide for your column structure. Making a copy of it ensures the data mapping works correctly from the start, saving you configuration time.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 10 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Get the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button to copy the template into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect UpViral: In the first UpViral module, click to add a connection. You'll be prompted to log into your UpViral account and authorize Make.com to access your data.

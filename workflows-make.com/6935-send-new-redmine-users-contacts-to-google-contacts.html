What This Workflow Does

For teams using Redmine for project management and issue tracking, managing a separate, updated contact list for clients, developers, and stakeholders is a constant, manual chore. New users are added to Redmine, but their details remain siloed, forcing team members to copy-paste information into email clients or Google Contacts, leading to errors, duplicates, and communication delays.

This automation solves that by creating a live bridge between Redmine and Google Contacts. Whenever a new user is created in your Redmine instance, this Make.com workflow triggers instantly. It captures the user's details—like name, email address, and company—and automatically creates a corresponding, fully formatted contact in your specified Google Contacts list. This ensures your entire team has immediate access to accurate contact information directly from their Gmail or Google Calendar, streamlining communication from the very first interaction.

Pro tip: Use Google Contact Groups to automatically categorize new Redmine contacts (e.g., "Clients," "Developers," "Project Stakeholders") based on their Redmine role or project, making segmented communication effortless.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, reliable automation that runs in the background, requiring zero ongoing effort after the initial one-time setup.

Step 1: Monitor Redmine for New Users

The scenario starts with a Watch Hook or a scheduled module in Make.com that monitors your Redmine application's API for the creation of new users. The moment a user record is added, the workflow is triggered, and the user's data is captured.

Step 2: Map and Format Contact Data

Make.com then processes the incoming data. It maps the relevant fields from Redmine (such as first name, last name, login/email, and custom fields) to the corresponding fields required by Google Contacts (Given Name, Family Name, Email, Organization Title). You can easily customize this mapping to include phone numbers or notes.

Step 3: Create Contact in Google Contacts

Finally, the workflow uses the Google Contacts module to create a new contact in your chosen Google account. The contact is saved instantly, becoming immediately searchable and available across Google's ecosystem—Gmail, Calendar, and Chat—for your entire team.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Get the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to copy the ready-made scenario into your Make.com account.
  2. Connect Redmine: In the first module, create a connection to your Redmine using an API key. Make.com will guide you through this simple process.
  3. Connect Google Contacts: Authorize Make.com to access your Google Contacts—this is a standard, secure OAuth process.
  4. Map Your Fields: Review the data mapping between Redmine and Google Contacts. Adjust the field pairs if your Redmine uses custom attribute names.
  5. Turn it On: Activate the scenario. It will now run automatically, syncing every new Redmine user going forward.

Pro tip: Test the workflow by creating a test user in Redmine first. Verify the contact appears correctly in Google Contacts before letting it run on live data.

What This Workflow Does

In project management, visibility is everything. When a new issue is logged in Redmine—a bug report, a feature request, or a client task—the team responsible needs to know immediately. Without automation, this relies on manual updates, emails, or team members constantly checking Redmine, leading to delays, missed issues, and communication gaps.

This workflow solves that by creating a seamless bridge between Redmine and Slack. Every time a new issue is created, Make.com detects it instantly and posts a formatted notification directly into your designated Slack channel. The message includes key details like the issue subject, description, priority, assignee, and a direct link back to Redmine. This transforms Slack into a real-time command center for your projects, ensuring nothing falls through the cracks and enabling faster collaboration.

The business value is clear: reduced issue response time, eliminated manual notification work, and improved team alignment. Projects move faster because the right people are informed the moment their input is needed.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple but powerful sequence, orchestrated by Make.com.

Step 1: Monitor Redmine for New Issues

The workflow starts with a Redmine module set to watch the "Issue" resource. It polls the Redmine API at regular intervals (e.g., every 5 minutes) or uses a webhook for instant triggering. When it detects a newly created issue, it captures all its data and passes it to the next step.

Pro tip: Configure the trigger to watch specific Redmine projects or issue trackers to avoid noise. This ensures only relevant notifications are sent to Slack.

Step 2: Format the Slack Message

Make.com then takes the raw issue data and structures it into a clear, actionable Slack message. This step involves using a "Compose" or "Text Aggregator" module to build a message that includes the issue ID, subject, description snippet, priority level (e.g., High, Normal), the assigned person, and the project name. A direct URL to the Redmine issue is also added for one-click access.

Step 3: Send the Notification to Slack

Finally, the formatted message is sent to your chosen Slack workspace via the Slack "Send a Message" module. You can specify the exact channel (like #development, #support, or #urgent-issues) or even a user's direct message. The notification appears in Slack with a clean format, allowing team members to react, comment, or click through to Redmine immediately.

To set up this workflow, ensure you have the following accounts and access ready:

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to deploy the template in under 15 minutes:

  1. Copy the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect Redmine: In the first module, add your Redmine connection by entering your instance URL and API key when prompted.

What This Workflow Does

Every minute counts in sales. When a potential lead interacts with your website chatbot, they're showing immediate interest. But that interest cools rapidly if follow-up is delayed by manual scheduling. This automation solves that problem by instantly converting chatbot conversations into scheduled Google Calendar events.

The workflow acts as a bridge between your ChatBot platform and Google Calendar. Whenever a new message is received—typically from a lead generation bot capturing details like company name, contact information, and needs—the automation triggers. It takes that structured data and creates a corresponding event in your calendar, complete with all relevant details. This turns a conversational inquiry into a concrete meeting in seconds, without any human intervention.

This isn't just about saving a few clicks; it's about closing the loop between lead capture and sales action instantly. It ensures no lead falls through the cracks because someone forgot to schedule a follow-up. The system works 24/7, capturing inquiries after hours and scheduling them for the next business day, maximizing response time and conversion rates.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence to transform a chat message into a calendar entry. Here’s the step-by-step process.

Step 1: ChatBot Trigger

The workflow starts when your ChatBot receives a new message. This is typically configured via a webhook within your ChatBot settings. When a conversation ends and key data is captured (like from a "Lead Generation Bot" template), ChatBot sends a HTTP POST request to Make.com containing all the collected variables.

Step 2: Data Parsing in Make.com

Make.com receives the webhook payload. The scenario module parses the incoming data to identify the variables you need for the calendar event: CompanyName, default_name (contact person), Team, CompanyNeed, and default_email. These become available for mapping in the next steps.

Step 3: Google Calendar Event Creation

Using the Google Calendar module in Make.com, the workflow creates a new event. It maps the chatbot variables into the event's fields: the contact name and company become part of the event title, the CompanyNeed goes into the description, and the default_email is added as an attendee. You can set a standard duration (e.g., 30 minutes) and even add a Google Meet link automatically.

Step 4> Confirmation & Error Handling

The workflow can be extended to send a confirmation message back to the lead via email or to notify your sales team in Slack. Error handling routes are included to catch issues like missing email addresses or calendar permission errors, ensuring the system is robust.

Pro tip: Structure your ChatBot conversation to ask for the lead's preferred meeting day/time upfront. You can then pass this as a variable to Make.com and use it to set the specific event start time, creating a truly hands-free scheduling experience.

Quick Setup Guide

Get this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Get the template: Click "Get This Workflow" above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect your apps: In the Make.com scenario, authenticate your Google Calendar connection and note your unique webhook URL from the first module.
  3. Configure your ChatBot: In your ChatBot dashboard, find the webhook settings for your lead generation bot. Paste the Make.com webhook URL and map the variables (CompanyName, default_name, etc.) to the webhook payload.
  4. Customize the event: In the Google Calendar module within Make.com, customize how the event looks. Set the event title format (e.g., "Call with [default_name] - [CompanyName]"), description template, and default duration.
  5. Run a test: Use your chatbot on your website and complete a test conversation. Verify that a corresponding event appears in your Google Calendar with all the correct details.
  6. Activate: Turn on the scenario in Make.com. Your automation is now live and will run automatically for every new qualifying chat message.

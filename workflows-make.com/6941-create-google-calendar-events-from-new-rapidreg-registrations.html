What This Workflow Does

Managing events, classes, or appointments manually is a time-consuming and error-prone process. Every new registration in RapidReg requires someone to manually open Google Calendar, create an event, fill in details, and set reminders. This not only eats up administrative hours but also leads to mistakes—double bookings, incorrect times, or missed entries that result in poor customer experience.

This automation solves that by creating a seamless bridge between RapidReg and Google Calendar. Whenever a new registration is submitted, the workflow instantly triggers and creates a corresponding Google Calendar event. It pulls data like the registrant's name, event date, time, and session details to populate the calendar entry automatically. This ensures your schedule is always up-to-date, your team is aligned, and every customer receives immediate confirmation of their booked slot.

The business value is clear: you save hours per week on manual scheduling, eliminate administrative errors, and provide a professional, automated experience that increases customer satisfaction and reduces no-shows.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger on New RapidReg Registration

The workflow starts by monitoring your RapidReg account for new registrations. Make.com watches for new entries in your registration form or database. Once a new record appears, it captures all the relevant data—customer information, selected event, date, time, and any custom fields.

Step 2: Map Registration Data to Calendar Fields

The automation then processes the captured data. It maps the RapidReg fields to the corresponding Google Calendar event properties. For example, the registrant's name becomes part of the event title or description, the selected date and time become the event start and end, and any notes become the event description.

Pro tip: You can add logic to format dates correctly, handle different time zones, or even categorize events by type (e.g., "Workshop," "Consultation") based on registration data.

Step 3: Create the Google Calendar Event

Using the mapped data, the workflow calls Google Calendar's API to create a new event on your specified calendar. It sets all details, including title, description, start/end times, and can even add the registrant's email as an attendee. The event is created instantly, often before the customer even closes the registration form.

Step 4: Confirm and Log

After the event is successfully created, the automation can log the action in a spreadsheet or send a confirmation notification to your team. This provides an audit trail and ensures you can verify that every registration resulted in a scheduled calendar entry.

What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Typeform for lead generation and Nutshell for CRM management, a critical gap often exists: manual data transfer. Every new form response requires someone to copy-paste information into the CRM, leading to delays, errors, and missed opportunities. This workflow bridges that gap automatically.

The automation listens for new Typeform submissions. When someone fills out your contact, demo request, or survey form, the workflow instantly triggers. It extracts the respondent's data—like their email, name, company, and answers—and creates a fully populated lead record in your Nutshell CRM. The sales team receives a new, qualified lead in their pipeline within seconds, not hours or days.

This solves the problem of lead leakage and slow response times. It ensures your marketing efforts (the form) directly fuel your sales engine (the CRM) without any human intervention, creating a seamless, efficient lead capture system.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger on New Typeform Response

The workflow uses Typeform's webhook or instant trigger. Whenever a form is submitted, Typeform sends the response data to Make.com, initiating the automation sequence. This happens in real-time.

Step 2: Map and Format Lead Data

Make.com modules parse the Typeform response. You map each form field (e.g., "Email", "Full Name", "Company") to corresponding fields in Nutshell's lead creation API. The workflow can also format data, combine fields, or add tags based on the form answers.

Pro tip: Use conditional logic to tag leads based on their responses. For example, if they select "Interested in Enterprise Plan," tag the lead as "High-Priority" in Nutshell.

Step 3: Create Lead in Nutshell

The formatted data is sent to Nutshell via its API, creating a new lead in your account. The lead includes all mapped information, appears in the correct pipeline stage, and is assigned to a team or owner based on your rules.

Step 4: Optional Notification & Logging

After successful creation, the workflow can send an internal notification (e.g., a Slack message or email) to alert the sales team. It also logs the action for audit purposes, ensuring you have a record of every automated lead creation.

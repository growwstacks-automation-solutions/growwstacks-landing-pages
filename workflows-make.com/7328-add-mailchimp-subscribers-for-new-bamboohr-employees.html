Add Mailchimp subscribers for new BambooHR employees

Automatically add new hires to your internal communication lists, saving HR time and ensuring consistent onboarding.

Get This Workflow → Make.com · Free Template

What This Workflow Does

When a new employee is added to your BambooHR system, this automation instantly adds their email address to a designated Mailchimp subscriber list. It eliminates the manual, repetitive task of copying and pasting employee data from your HR platform to your email marketing tool.

For HR and Internal Communications teams, this means every new hire automatically receives welcome emails, company newsletters, policy updates, and training announcements from their first day. It ensures no one is missed due to administrative backlog, creating a smoother onboarding experience and keeping your workforce informed and engaged.

The business value is clear: reduced manual workload for HR staff, guaranteed consistency in internal communications, and faster time-to-information for new employees. This automation turns a multi-step, error-prone process into a silent, reliable background task.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger on New BambooHR Employee

The Make.com scenario monitors your BambooHR account for new employee records. Whenever a hire is added (with their email and other details), it triggers the workflow.

Step 2: Extract Employee Contact Information

The workflow pulls the relevant data from the BambooHR record, typically the employee's email address, first name, and last name. You can configure it to include additional fields like department or location if needed.

Step 3: Add Subscriber to Mailchimp List

Using the extracted data, the scenario calls the Mailchimp API to add the new employee as a subscriber to a specific list you choose (e.g., "All Employees," "Department News," or "Onboarding Communications").

Step 4: Optional Notification or Logging

You can extend the workflow to send a confirmation notification to the HR team via Slack or email, or log the addition in a Google Sheet for audit purposes. This completes the automated loop.

Pro tip: Use BambooHR custom fields to segment new hires. For example, add employees from the "Marketing" department to a "Marketing Updates" Mailchimp list automatically, creating targeted internal communications.

Who This Is For

This workflow is ideal for HR Managers and Internal Communications specialists in companies that use BambooHR for employee management and Mailchimp for internal email newsletters or announcements.

It's particularly valuable for growing companies with frequent hiring, where manually managing subscriber lists becomes a significant time sink. Teams that prioritize consistent onboarding and employee engagement will see immediate benefits from this automation.

If your organization sends regular internal emails about company culture, policy changes, training opportunities, or event announcements, this automation ensures your audience list is always accurate and up-to-date.

What You'll Need

  1. A BambooHR account with active employee records and API access (typically available in paid plans).
  2. A Mailchimp account with at least one audience list set up for internal communications.
  3. A Make.com account (free tier available) to build and run the automation scenario.
  4. The employee email address field populated in BambooHR for new hires.
  5. Basic understanding of which Mailchimp list you want new employees added to (e.g., "Company News," "All Employees").

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Click the "Get This Workflow" button to open the template on Make.com.
  2. In Make, create a new scenario and add the BambooHR module as the trigger. Configure it to watch for "New Employee" events.
  3. Add a Mailchimp module as the action. Choose "Add Subscriber to List" and connect it to your chosen Mailchimp audience.
  4. Map the data fields: connect BambooHR employee email to Mailchimp subscriber email, and map first/last name if desired.
  5. Test the scenario by adding a test employee in BambooHR or using a sample data run in Make. Confirm the subscriber appears in your Mailchimp list.
  6. Turn the scenario on. It will now run automatically for every future new hire added to BambooHR.

What This Workflow Does

Managing a team's schedule and attendance manually is a constant drain on managerial time and often leads to delayed responses. When an employee misses a scheduled shift in Hubstaff, the gap in coverage might go unnoticed for hours, impacting client projects, service delivery, and team morale. This reactive approach forces managers to play catch-up.

This automation solves that problem proactively. It acts as a digital watchdog for your schedule. Every time a scheduled shift passes in Hubstaff without the employee clocking in, this Make.com workflow triggers instantly. It captures the relevant details—who, when, and what project—and sends a structured notification directly to a designated Slack channel. This transforms attendance tracking from a manual, forgettable task into an automated, immediate alert system.

The business value is clear: you close staffing gaps faster, maintain operational continuity, and create a transparent record of attendance. Managers can reassign tasks or follow up immediately, ensuring projects stay on track and employees understand that their scheduled time is monitored and valued.

How It Works

The workflow is a straightforward, reliable pipeline connecting your time-tracking tool to your communication hub.

Step 1: Monitoring Hubstaff for Missed Shifts

The scenario starts with a scheduled Make.com check (e.g., every 30 minutes) that queries your Hubstaff organization. It looks for scheduled shifts that have a start time in the recent past but lack a corresponding "clock-in" activity from the assigned team member. Make.com identifies these gaps accurately.

Step 2: Compiling the Alert Details

Once a missed shift is detected, the workflow gathers all necessary context. This typically includes the employee's name, the missed shift start and end times, the associated project or client name from Hubstaff, and the team manager's information. This data package is formatted for clarity.

Step 3: Sending the Slack Notification

The formatted data is sent to Slack via a webhook or the Slack app module in Make.com. The message is posted to a specific channel (like #operations or #team-alerts) and can be configured to tag relevant managers or roles. The alert appears in real-time, where the team can see it and act.

Pro tip: Customize your Slack message to include a direct link to the employee's Hubstaff timesheet for the day. This saves managers a click and speeds up the verification and follow-up process.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 20 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect Hubstaff: In the first module, add your Hubstaff connection using your organization's API token from the Hubstaff settings.
  3. Configure the Schedule: Set the "Schedule" module to run at an interval that makes sense for your business (e.g., every 30 minutes to catch morning shift misses promptly).
  4. Connect Slack: In the Slack module, authenticate with your workspace and select the exact channel where notifications should appear.
  5. Customize the Message: Edit the text of the Slack message to include the variables from Hubstaff (employee name, time, project) and any specific instructions for your team.
  6. Test and Activate: Run the scenario once manually to ensure it correctly detects a test condition and posts to Slack. Then turn it on.

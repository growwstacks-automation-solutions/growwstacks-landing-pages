What This Workflow Does

For businesses using Sellsy for sales pipeline management and ActiveCampaign for email marketing and automation, a critical problem exists: data silos. When a sales rep adds a new lead or customer into Sellsy, that valuable contact information is trapped, requiring manual effort to copy it into ActiveCampaign for nurturing campaigns. This delay can mean missed opportunities for timely follow-up, inconsistent customer data, and hours of administrative work each week.

This free Make.com workflow solves that by creating a live, one-way bridge between the two systems. It acts as an automatic assistant that watches for new contacts in Sellsy and instantly replicates them in ActiveCampaign. The moment a deal is created or a client is onboarded in Sellsy, their contact record is ready for marketing automation, lead scoring, and targeted communication—without anyone lifting a finger.

How It Works

The automation follows a simple, reliable sequence to ensure data flows accurately and instantly.

Step 1: Trigger on New Sellsy Contact

The workflow is activated the instant a new contact is created within your Sellsy account. The Make.com scenario monitors Sellsy in real-time, capturing the event and all associated data like name, email, company, and any custom fields your sales team has filled in.

Step 2: Map and Prepare the Data

The captured data is then structured to match the expected format in ActiveCampaign. This step involves mapping Sellsy field names (e.g., contact.email) to their corresponding ActiveCampaign field names (e.g., email). You can customize this to include phone numbers, tags for lead source, or notes.

Pro tip: Use this mapping step to add strategic tags in ActiveCampaign based on the Sellsy deal stage. For example, tag contacts as "Hot Lead" if the Sellsy deal stage is "Proposal Sent."

Step 3: Create or Update the ActiveCampaign Contact

The workflow sends the prepared data to ActiveCampaign via its API with a command to create a new contact. A best-practice addition is a deduplication check first, using the email address as a unique key. If the contact already exists, the automation can update the existing record instead, preventing duplicates.

Quick Setup Guide

Get your integration live in under 30 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the Make.com scenario, click on the Sellsy module and authenticate by entering your API key and company domain. Repeat the process for the ActiveCampaign module using its API key and account URL.
  3. Configure Field Mapping: Open the ActiveCampaign "Create a Contact" module. Map the data from the previous Sellsy module to the correct ActiveCampaign fields. At a minimum, map the email address.
  4. Test the Flow: Turn the scenario on and create a test contact in Sellsy. Check your ActiveCampaign account within seconds to confirm the contact appears.
  5. Activate & Monitor: Once testing is successful, let the scenario run. It will work continuously in the background. Monitor the scenario's history log in Make.com periodically to ensure it's running smoothly.

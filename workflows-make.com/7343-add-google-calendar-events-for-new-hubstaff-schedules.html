What This Workflow Does

For managers and operations teams, scheduling is a constant, time-consuming task. You create shifts in Hubstaff, but then you need to manually communicate those schedules and hope employees add them to their personal calendars. This manual process leads to missed shifts, double-bookings, and hours of administrative work each week.

This automation solves that by creating a direct bridge between your workforce management tool and your team's calendars. Every time a new schedule is created in Hubstaff, this Make.com workflow instantly creates a corresponding event in a designated Google Calendar. It transforms a multi-step, error-prone process into a seamless, automatic sync.

The business value is clear: it saves managerial time, improves schedule adherence, and provides a single, visible source of truth for when employees are working. No more confusion, no more missed messages—just synchronized schedules.

How It Works

The workflow follows a simple trigger-action sequence, automating what was once a manual chore.

Step 1: Monitoring Hubstaff for New Schedules

The scenario starts by watching your Hubstaff organization for new "schedules." This is the trigger. Whenever you or another manager assigns a shift to an employee in Hubstaff, Make.com detects this new entry almost instantly.

Step 2: Processing the Schedule Data

Make.com then takes the key details from the Hubstaff schedule: the employee's name, the start and end time of the shift, and any notes. It structures this data into a format that Google Calendar understands, preparing it for the next step.

Pro tip: You can configure the workflow to filter schedules by location, department, or employee tag within Hubstaff, ensuring only relevant shifts are added to specific calendars.

Step 3: Creating the Google Calendar Event

Finally, the workflow uses the Google Calendar module to create a new event. It populates the event title (e.g., "Work Shift - [Employee Name]"), sets the correct date and time, and adds the event to a chosen calendar—this could be a shared company calendar or it could theoretically be mapped to an employee's personal calendar with the right setup.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template on Make.com and create a copy in your own account.
  2. Connect Hubstaff: In the first module, authorize Make.com to access your Hubstaff account. You'll be prompted to log in and grant permissions.
  3. Connect Google Calendar: Do the same for the Google Calendar module, signing into the Google account that owns the calendar you want to use.
  4. Configure the Calendar: In the Google Calendar module, select the specific calendar (e.g., "Company Shifts") where new events should be created.
  5. Test & Activate: Create a test schedule in Hubstaff for the next day. Turn on your Make.com scenario and watch as the event appears in your Google Calendar. Once confirmed, your automation is live.

What This Workflow Does

Managing contacts across multiple platforms is a common headache for businesses. Your sales team adds a new lead to Google Contacts, but your support team using Placetel for calls doesn't see that contact. This leads to missed outreach opportunities, duplicate manual entries, and inconsistent customer data.

This automation workflow solves that by creating a seamless bridge between Google Contacts and Placetel. Whenever a contact is created or updated in Google Contacts, the Make.com scenario triggers automatically. It then either adds that contact as a new entry in your Placetel directory or updates an existing Placetel contact if it already exists. This ensures your phone system always has the latest, accurate contact information without any manual intervention.

The result is a unified contact database that empowers your team. Sales reps can call new leads immediately after they're captured. Support agents have updated client details at their fingertips. Administrative overhead drops significantly, and data errors are eliminated.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger on Google Contact Change

The workflow starts with a watchful eye on your Google Contacts. The Make.com scenario uses the Google Contacts module to monitor your specified contact groups or your entire directory. It can trigger on two key events: when a new contact is created, or when an existing contact's details (like phone number or email) are modified.

Step 2: Retrieve Contact Data

Once triggered, the workflow captures all relevant data from the Google Contact entry. This includes the contact's name, phone numbers, email addresses, company, and any other fields you wish to sync. The data is structured and prepared for the next step.

Step 3: Check for Existing Placetel Contact

Before creating a duplicate, the scenario intelligently checks your Placetel contacts directory. It typically uses a unique identifier, like the email address or a specific phone number, to search for an existing match. This step is crucial for maintaining a clean, non-duplicated database.

Step 4: Create or Update in Placetel

Based on the search result, the workflow takes one of two actions. If no matching contact is found in Placetel, it uses the Placetel module to create a new contact, populating all the mapped fields. If a match is found, it updates that existing Placetel contact with the new information from Google, ensuring the record is current.

Pro tip: You can configure the workflow to only sync contacts from specific Google Contact groups (like "Sales Leads" or "Clients"), giving you control over which contacts populate your phone system.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your contact sync automation running:

  1. Clone the Template: Use the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template on Make.com and clone it to your own account.
  2. Connect Your Apps: In the cloned scenario, connect the Google Contacts module to your Google account and the Placetel module to your Placetel account via Make.com's connection setup.
  3. Configure the Trigger: Set the Google Contacts module to watch the specific contact group or your entire "My Contacts" for changes (creation or update).
  4. Map the Data Fields: In the Placetel module, map the incoming Google Contact data (like `name`, `phoneNumber`, `emailAddress`) to the corresponding Placetel contact fields.
  5. Set the Search Criteria: Define the unique field (e.g., primary email) that the workflow will use to search for existing contacts in Placetel before deciding to create or update.
  6. Test with a Sample Contact: Create a test contact in Google Contacts and run the scenario once to verify it appears correctly in Placetel.
  7. Activate the Scenario: Turn on the schedule (or set it to instant) and let the automation run continuously, keeping your contacts synchronized.

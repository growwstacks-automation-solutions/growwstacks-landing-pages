What This Workflow Does

For sales and marketing teams using LinkedIn for lead generation, manually copying and pasting prospect information from Dux-Soup into a CRM is a tedious, error-prone, and time-consuming task. Valuable leads can slip through the cracks during busy periods, and delayed follow-up can cost you deals.

This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless bridge between your LinkedIn prospecting activity and your central customer database. Whenever Dux-Soup Turbo captures a new lead—be it from a profile visit, connection, or campaign interaction—it instantly sends that data to Make.com. The workflow then formats the information and creates a fully-fledged contact record in your Macanta CRM, all without you lifting a finger.

The result is a fully automated lead capture pipeline. Your sales team gains immediate visibility into new prospects with accurate data, enabling faster and more personalized outreach. You turn a manual administrative chore into a reliable, hands-off system that works 24/7.

How It Works

The workflow is built on Make.com and acts as a real-time data conduit between Dux-Soup and Macanta.

Step 1: Trigger from Dux-Soup

The automation starts when Dux-Soup Turbo sends a webhook notification. This webhook contains a payload of data about the newly captured lead, including their name, job title, company, LinkedIn profile URL, and any custom notes you've configured.

Step 2: Data Processing in Make.com

Make.com receives the webhook and parses the data. You can use this stage to filter leads based on specific criteria (e.g., only from certain companies or job titles), clean up the data, or add additional information like a lead source tag "From LinkedIn - Dux-Soup".

Pro tip: Use the router module in Make.com to split the workflow. For example, you could send leads from "Director" level titles to a specific sales queue in Macanta, while others go to a general lead list.

Step 3: Create Contact in Macanta

The final module uses Macanta's API to create a new contact. The workflow maps the fields from Dux-Soup (like `firstName`, `company`, `profileUrl`) to the corresponding contact fields in Macanta. The new contact is instantly available in your CRM, ready for your team to engage.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes by following these steps:

  1. Clone the Template: Click "Get This Workflow" to open the template on Make.com. Press the "Copy" button to create your own scenario.
  2. Connect Dux-Soup: In the first module, you'll need to set up the webhook. Make.com will provide a unique URL. Copy this URL.
  3. Configure Dux-Soup Turbo: Log into your Dux-Soup dashboard. Navigate to the webhook settings and paste the Make.com webhook URL. Save the configuration.
  4. Connect Macanta: In the Make.com scenario, open the Macanta module. Click to add a connection and enter your Macanta API credentials when prompted.
  5. Map Data Fields (Optional): Review the data mapping between the Dux-Soup webhook and Macanta's contact fields. Adjust if you want to send different or additional information.
  6. Test & Activate: Turn on the scenario in Make.com. Perform a test action in Dux-Soup (like visiting a profile) and verify that a new contact appears in your Macanta CRM.

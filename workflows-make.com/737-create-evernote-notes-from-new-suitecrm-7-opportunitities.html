What This Workflow Does

Sales teams waste valuable time juggling between their CRM and note-taking apps. Critical details from a new sales opportunity—like the deal size, key contacts, and next steps—often get trapped in SuiteCRM, forcing reps to manually copy information into Evernote for meeting prep and collaboration. This manual process is error-prone, inconsistent, and steals time from actual selling.

This automated workflow solves that by acting as a seamless bridge between SuiteCRM 7 and Evernote. Every time your sales team creates a new opportunity in SuiteCRM, this system instantly generates a corresponding, well-formatted note in a designated Evernote notebook. The note is pre-populated with all the essential deal data, creating a perfect starting point for the sales process. This ensures nothing falls through the cracks and gives every team member immediate, organized access to deal context.

How It Works

The automation is a straightforward, reliable sequence that runs silently in the background, triggered by a single event in your CRM.

Step 1: Detecting a New Opportunity

The workflow constantly monitors your SuiteCRM 7 instance for new "Opportunity" records. Using Make.com's SuiteCRM module, it acts as a watchful assistant, checking for new entries at regular intervals you define (e.g., every 15 minutes, hourly, or in real-time via webhooks if supported).

Step 2: Gathering the Deal Data

Once a new opportunity is spotted, the automation captures all its relevant fields. This typically includes the Opportunity Name, Amount, Stage, Expected Close Date, Associated Account, and Primary Contact. It can also pull in custom fields unique to your sales process, ensuring the note contains exactly the information your team needs.

Step 3: Creating the Structured Note in Evernote

The captured data is then sent to Evernote via the Make.com Evernote module. The workflow creates a new note in a specific notebook you choose (e.g., "Sales Pipeline" or "Active Deals"). It intelligently formats the data, using headings, bullet points, and even tags (like the opportunity stage or account name) to make the note instantly useful and easy to find later.

Pro tip: Use Evernote tags for the opportunity stage (e.g., #Prospecting, #Negotiation). This allows you to create smart views or searches in Evernote to see all deals in a particular phase at a glance, adding a powerful layer of pipeline management on top of your CRM.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 30 minutes by following these steps in your Make.com scenario.

  1. Clone the Template: Click the "Get This Workflow" button above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect SuiteCRM: In the first module, add your SuiteCRM connection by entering your instance URL and API credentials. Test the connection to ensure it works.

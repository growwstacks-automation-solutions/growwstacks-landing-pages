What This Workflow Does

For businesses that rely on printed documents—invoices, reports, shipping labels, or forms—the process of manually checking Google Docs, downloading files, and sending them to a printer is a significant time drain and a source of errors. This workflow solves that by creating a fully automated bridge between your Google Docs and your PrintNode-connected printers.

The template periodically scans a specified Google Drive folder for new documents. When a new document is detected, it automatically retrieves the file, converts it to a print-ready format, and sends it as a print job directly to your designated printer via PrintNode. This happens without any human intervention, ensuring documents are printed the moment they are finalized.

This automation transforms a reactive, manual task into a proactive, reliable system. It's perfect for scenarios where document creation and physical output need to be tightly synchronized, such as order fulfillment centers, accounting departments, or administrative offices that handle high-volume paperwork.

How It Works

The workflow operates on a simple yet powerful trigger-action principle, orchestrated by Make.com.

Step 1: Scheduled Monitoring

A scheduler module triggers the workflow at regular intervals (default is every 15 minutes). This acts as the heartbeat of the automation, constantly checking for new activity in your Google Drive.

Step 2: Detect New Google Docs

The workflow connects to your Google Drive via the Google Docs module. It searches a predefined folder for documents created since the last check. It can filter by document type, name, or other properties to target only the relevant files.

Step 3: Retrieve and Prepare Document

Once a new document is identified, the workflow fetches its content. Make.com handles the conversion, typically transforming the Google Doc into a PDF or other printer-compatible format that preserves formatting.

Pro tip: You can configure the workflow to monitor specific shared folders used by your team. This creates a "print queue" where anyone dropping a document into that folder triggers an automatic print job, perfect for collaborative environments.

Step 4: Send to PrintNode

The prepared file is then sent to the PrintNode module. Here, you specify the exact printer (from your PrintNode account) and any print settings like paper size, orientation, or duplex. PrintNode's API takes over, routing the job directly to the physical printer on your network.

Step 5: Confirmation & Logging

The workflow can be extended to log each print job, send a confirmation notification via email or Slack, or even trigger a follow-up action, creating a complete audit trail for your printing operations.

Quick Setup Guide

Getting this automation live takes about 15 minutes. Follow these steps after clicking "Get This Workflow".

  1. Copy the Template: In Make.com, click the provided link and create a copy of the scenario in your own workspace.
  2. Connect Google Docs: In the first module, authorize the connection to your Google account and select the specific Drive folder to monitor.
  3. Connect PrintNode: In the PrintNode module, add your API key from your PrintNode account and select the target printer from your list.
  4. Adjust the Schedule: Click the clock icon on the scheduler module. You can change the interval from 15 minutes to whatever fits your needs—hourly, daily, or even instantly with a webhook.

  6. Activate & Monitor: Once tested, let the scenario run. You can monitor its history in Make.com to see each executed print job.

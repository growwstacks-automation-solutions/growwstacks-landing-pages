What This Workflow Does

For online businesses using ThriveCart, sales happen at all hours. Manually checking for new orders or forwarding confirmation emails to your team is a slow, error-prone process that delays customer response and misses opportunities for immediate engagement. This workflow solves that by creating a real-time communication bridge between your sales platform and your team's collaboration hub.

Every time a customer completes a successful purchase in your ThriveCart store, this automation instantly captures the order details and posts a formatted, actionable message to a designated Slack channel. Your sales, support, and fulfillment teams see the notification within seconds, complete with key information like the customer's name, product purchased, and order value. This transforms a silent backend transaction into a visible team event, fostering accountability and enabling rapid follow-up.

How It Works

The automation acts as a smart relay, listening for events in ThriveCart and broadcasting them to Slack with precision.

Step 1: Trigger on New ThriveCart Order

The workflow is activated by ThriveCart's "Order Successful" webhook. Make.com monitors this webhook endpoint, and the moment a new order is marked as successful (payment captured), it instantly receives a data packet containing the entire order record.

Step 2: Parse and Format Order Data

Make.com then parses the incoming webhook data. It extracts the most relevant fields for your team, such as the customer's first and last name, email address, product name, order total, and any custom fields from the checkout (like affiliate tags or notes). The system structures this data into a clear, readable format.

Step 3: Send Rich Message to Slack

Finally, the workflow uses the Slack "Send a Message" module to post the formatted notification. You can configure it to send to a specific public or private channel, or even as a direct message. The message can use Slack's Block Kit to create an attractive layout with sections, dividers, and emojis (like a 🎉 for a new sale), making it highly scannable for your busy team.

Pro tip: Use conditional logic to route high-value orders to a dedicated #big-wins Slack channel. This builds team morale and ensures VIP clients get immediate, focused attention.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for digital product creators, coaches, agencies, and e-commerce businesses that sell through ThriveCart and rely on team collaboration in Slack. It's especially valuable for:

  • Small teams wearing multiple hats: When the same person handles sales, support, and fulfillment, instant notifications prevent orders from being missed in a crowded inbox.
  • Businesses with a fulfillment partner or VA: Automatically notifying your external team in Slack creates a seamless handoff without needing to grant them access to your ThriveCart admin.
  • Companies that celebrate sales publicly: Creating a culture of recognition by sharing wins in a team channel boosts motivation and aligns everyone around revenue goals.

What You'll Need

  1. A Make.com account (free or paid plan).
  2. An active ThriveCart account with at least one product and the webhooks feature enabled.
  3. A Slack workspace where you have permission to create incoming webhooks or install apps, and access to the channel where you want notifications posted.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this automation running in under 15 minutes.

  1. Clone the template: Click "Get This Workflow" above to open the template in your Make.com account and create a copy.
  2. Connect ThriveCart: In the first module, authenticate your ThriveCart account with Make.com using your ThriveCart API key. This allows Make to listen for order events.
  3. Connect Slack: In the Slack module, authenticate with your Slack workspace. Select the specific channel (e.g., #sales-notifications) where messages should be sent.
  4. Customize the message: Review the data mapping. Ensure fields like `Customer Name`, `Product`, and `Total` are pulling from the correct ThriveCart data points. You can edit the message text and formatting to match your team's style.
  5. Test and activate: Run the scenario once manually, then complete a test purchase in your ThriveCart store. Check that the notification appears correctly in Slack. Once confirmed, turn the scenario to "ON" to run automatically.

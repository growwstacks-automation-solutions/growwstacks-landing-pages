How It Works

The automation follows a logical, event-driven flow that you can adapt to countless business scenarios.

Step 1: The Trigger Event

Every workflow begins with a trigger. This is the specific event in a connected app that tells Make.com to start the automation. Common triggers include a new form submission on your website, a scheduled time (e.g., every Monday at 9 AM), a new row added to a Google Sheet, or a payment received in Stripe. The template helps you identify and configure the right starting point for your process.

Step 2: Data Processing & Routing

Once triggered, the workflow receives data from the initial event. This step involves processing that data: filtering it based on conditions (e.g., only process orders over $100), routing it down different paths (e.g., if a lead is from the US, go to the US sales team list), and transforming it (e.g., combining a first name and last name field into a full name). This logic determines the "intelligence" of your automation.

Step 3: Executing Connected Actions

This is where the magic happens. Based on the processed data, the workflow performs actions in your other connected apps. For example, it can create a new contact in your CRM, post a message to a Slack channel, generate an invoice in your accounting software, or update a project card in Trello. The template shows you how to map data from the trigger to the correct fields in each action module.

Step 4: Error Handling & Notifications

A robust automation anticipates problems. This final stage involves setting up error handlers to manage issues like an app being down or invalid data. It also includes success notifications, perhaps sending you a daily digest of how many leads were processed or alerting you immediately if a critical failure occurs. This ensures your automations are reliable and you're always in control.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your first automation up and running using the principles in this template:

  1. Duplicate the Template: Use the "Get This Workflow" button to open the template in your Make.com account. Click "Copy" to create your own version you can edit.
  2. Identify Your Trigger: Replace the sample trigger module with your own. Click on it, choose your app (e.g., Google Forms), and select the specific trigger event (e.g., "New Form Response").
  3. Connect Your Accounts: When prompted, authorize Make.com to access your apps. This establishes a secure connection for the workflow to move data.
  4. Map Your Data: In each action module, click into the fields and select the data you want to insert from the trigger. For example, map the "Email" from the form response to the "Email" field in your CRM's "Create Contact" action.
  5. Test with One Record: Before activating, use the "Run once" feature. Submit a test form entry or create a test record to watch the workflow execute step-by-step and verify the outcome.
  6. Activate and Monitor: Turn the workflow on. Monitor the execution history for the first few runs to ensure everything works as expected. Set up a simple error notification to alert you of any issues.

A trigger is the event that starts the workflow, like a new form submission or a scheduled time. An action is what the automation does in response, like creating a record, sending a message, or updating data. Think of it as 'When this happens (trigger), then do these things (actions).' Understanding this cause-and-effect relationship is the foundation of designing effective automations.

Every workflow must have at least one trigger but can have many actions. Triggers are passive—they wait for an event. Actions are active—they perform a task. Common triggers include "new email," "updated database row," or "every day at 9 AM." Common actions include "send an email," "create a task," or "post a Slack message."

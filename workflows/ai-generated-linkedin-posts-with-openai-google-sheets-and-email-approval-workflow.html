What This Workflow Does

Creating consistent, high-quality LinkedIn content is time-consuming. Between brainstorming, writing, getting approvals, and scheduling, marketing teams can spend 10+ hours weekly just on one platform. This workflow automates the entire process from idea to published post.

It connects Google Sheets (where your team adds content ideas) with OpenAI (which generates professional posts) and Gmail (for approval workflows). Once approved, posts can be scheduled directly to LinkedIn. This eliminates manual drafting, endless email threads, and last-minute content scrambling.

The system ensures brand consistency, maintains a regular posting schedule, and gives your team back hours each week for strategic work rather than administrative tasks.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Trigger & Data Collection

The workflow runs on a schedule you define—daily, weekly, or whenever you need new content. It checks your Google Sheet for rows marked "Pending" and pulls the post description, instructions, and any image URLs.

2. AI Content Generation

Using OpenAI's language models, the system transforms basic ideas into polished LinkedIn posts. It follows your brand guidelines, adds relevant hashtags, and formats the content for maximum engagement.

3. Approval Request & Management

Generated posts are sent via email to designated approvers (marketing leads, executives, legal teams). The email includes the content and a simple form to approve, reject, or request edits.

4. Conditional Processing

Based on the approval response, the workflow either updates the Google Sheet with "Approved" status and proceeds to posting, or flags it for revision and notifies the content creator.

5. Publishing & Tracking

Approved posts can be automatically published to LinkedIn or scheduled for optimal times. The Google Sheet is updated with the post link and status, creating a complete audit trail.

What You'll Need

  1. A Google Sheet with columns for: Post Description, Instructions, Image URL, Status (Pending/Approved/Posted)
  2. An OpenAI API key (access to GPT-4 or similar models)
  3. Gmail account for sending approval requests
  4. LinkedIn access (personal or Company Page admin rights)
  5. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with credentials for the above services

Pro tip: Create a template row in your Google Sheet with example content and formatting guidelines. This helps team members submit consistent briefs that AI can work with effectively.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Prepare your Google Sheet with the required columns and add a test row with Status set to "Pending".
  3. Configure credentials in n8n for Google Sheets, Gmail, OpenAI, and LinkedIn.
  4. Customize the AI prompt in the "Generate Post Content" node to match your brand voice and content style.
  5. Set the approval email recipients in the Gmail node to your team's addresses.
  6. Test the workflow manually first to ensure all connections work, then set your preferred schedule.

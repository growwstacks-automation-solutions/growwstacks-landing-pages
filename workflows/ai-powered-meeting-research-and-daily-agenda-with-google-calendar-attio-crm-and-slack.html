What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of walking into meetings unprepared. Instead of spending 15-30 minutes each morning researching who you're meeting with, this workflow automatically gathers comprehensive context about every attendee and delivers a personalized briefing directly to your Slack channel.

The system connects your Google Calendar with multiple data sources to create intelligent meeting preparation. It identifies external meetings, researches attendees using AI, checks your CRM for existing relationships, searches for recent company news, and compiles everything into an actionable agenda. This ensures you have relevant talking points, background information, and strategic context for every meeting without manual effort.

How It Works

1. Daily Schedule Trigger

The workflow activates every weekday morning (default 6 AM, customizable) to analyze your Google Calendar for the day ahead. It identifies all scheduled meetings and filters for external appointments where preparation matters most.

2. Calendar Analysis & Attendee Identification

The system extracts meeting details including titles, times, and attendee email addresses. It distinguishes between internal colleagues and external contacts who require research, focusing preparation efforts where they provide the most value.

3. Multi-Source AI Research

For each external attendee, the workflow queries multiple data sources simultaneously: your Attio CRM for existing contact records and interaction history, Gmail for past email correspondence, previous calendar events for meeting history, and web searches for recent company news and professional updates.

4. CRM Integration & Data Enrichment

The system automatically creates new contact records in Attio for unknown attendees and enriches existing records with meeting context. This builds your institutional knowledge over time, creating a valuable relationship database that improves with each interaction.

5. Intelligent Brief Generation

AI analyzes all gathered information to generate a scannable, actionable meeting brief. This includes key talking points, relevant background information, potential conversation starters, and strategic considerations tailored to each specific meeting.

6. Slack Delivery & Mobile Access

The formatted brief delivers to your designated Slack channel, providing easy mobile access throughout your day. The structured format allows quick scanning between meetings while maintaining depth for important conversations.

What You'll Need

  1. Google Calendar account with OAuth2 access for fetching daily meetings
  2. Slack workspace with bot permissions to receive daily briefs
  3. Gmail account (optional) for searching past email interactions with attendees
  4. OpenRouter API key or similar AI service for intelligent research and summarization
  5. Attio CRM account (optional) for contact management and relationship tracking
  6. Apollo.io API key (optional) for company data and technographic information
  7. Basic n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the automation

Pro tip: Start with just Google Calendar and Slack integration, then add CRM and additional data sources as you validate the value. The workflow is modular, allowing gradual implementation based on your existing tech stack.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this meeting research automation in your n8n environment:

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance using the import workflow function
  2. Configure connections for Google Calendar, Slack, and any optional services you're using
  3. Set your schedule by adjusting the Schedule Trigger node to match your preferred morning preparation time
  4. Designate your Slack channel where daily briefs should be delivered
  5. Test with a single meeting by temporarily adjusting the schedule trigger to run immediately

What This Workflow Does

This automation creates an intelligent supply chain control tower that gives your team instant visibility into logistics operations. Instead of manually querying databases or building complex dashboards, your team can ask natural language questions about shipments, delays, inventory, or carrier performance and receive immediate, AI-generated answers.

The workflow connects Google BigQuery (where your logistics data lives) with OpenAI's GPT-4o to create a conversational interface for your supply chain data. It's designed for logistics managers, supply chain analysts, and operations teams who need quick answers without technical barriers.

By automating the data querying process, this control tower reduces the time spent on manual reporting by 80-90%, enables faster decision-making during disruptions, and democratizes data access across your organization.

How It Works

The workflow follows a clear, step-by-step process to transform natural language questions into actionable supply chain insights.

Step 1: Natural Language Input

A team member asks a question through a chat interface (like Slack, Teams, or a web interface). For example: "Which shipments to Customer X are delayed this week?" or "What's our average delivery time for last month?"

Step 2: AI Query Generation

The workflow sends the question to GPT-4o, which understands the intent and generates the appropriate SQL query for your BigQuery data structure. The AI considers your specific table schemas and business context.

Step 3: Data Retrieval

The generated SQL query executes against your Google BigQuery database, pulling real-time logistics data including shipment statuses, delivery times, carrier information, and inventory levels.

Step 4: AI Analysis & Response

GPT-4o analyzes the query results, provides context-aware insights, and formats the answer in clear, business-friendly language. It can highlight anomalies, suggest actions, or provide summaries.

Step 5: Delivery & Follow-up

The answer is delivered back through the chat interface. The conversation memory allows for follow-up questions like "Why are they delayed?" or "Which carrier has the most delays?"

Pro tip: Start by connecting this to a dedicated Slack channel for your logistics team. This creates a central command center where everyone can ask questions and get instant answers without leaving their communication platform.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement your AI-powered supply chain control tower:

  1. Import the Template: Download the JSON file above and import it into your n8n instance
  2. Configure BigQuery Connection: Add your Google Cloud service account credentials to the BigQuery node
  3. Set Up OpenAI: Add your API key to the GPT-4o node and adjust the system prompt for your business context
  4. Test Basic Queries: Start with simple questions about your data to ensure the SQL generation works correctly
  5. Connect to Chat Platform: Replace the manual trigger with a Slack, Teams, or webhook trigger for production use
  6. Train Your Team: Show your logistics team how to ask effective questions and interpret the AI's responses

Implementation Tip: Begin with a pilot focusing on one specific area like "shipment delays" or "carrier performance." This allows you to refine the AI's understanding before expanding to broader supply chain questions.

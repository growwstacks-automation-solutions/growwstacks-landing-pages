AI Food Ingredient Scanner: Telegram + Airtable

Upload product photos to Telegram, get instant AI analysis against your personal ingredient database, and ensure dietary compliance automatically.

What This Workflow Does

For anyone managing food allergies, dietary restrictions (vegan, gluten-free, halal, keto), or simply avoiding specific additives, reading ingredient labels is a constant, error-prone chore. This automation turns your smartphone into a personal food safety bodyguard.

The workflow solves the problem of manual label scrutiny. You simply send a photo of a product's ingredient list to your private Telegram bot. The system automatically extracts the text using AI vision, cross-references every ingredient against your personal "watchlist" stored in Airtable, and instantly replies with a clear "Safe" or "Unsafe" verdict. It even logs the scan for your records, creating a searchable history of everything you've checked.

How It Works

The automation follows a precise, logical sequence to ensure accurate and personalized results every time.

1. Photo Submission via Telegram

You send a photo message to your dedicated Telegram bot. The workflow is triggered instantly, capturing the image file and the user's details for personalized processing.

2. Fetch Personal Rules from Airtable

The workflow queries your Airtable base to retrieve your active "watchlist" (ingredients to avoid or prefer) and your configured "persona" settings, which can customize the bot's response tone and language.

3. AI-Powered Ingredient Analysis

The image is processed using an AI model (configurable for local LLM or cloud services like Gemini) to perform Optical Character Recognition (OCR), converting the label image into readable text. This text is then analyzed against your watchlist using strict logic (e.g., veto mode where any "bad" ingredient triggers an unsafe result).

4. Instant Telegram Response

The bot replies directly in the Telegram chat with a formatted message. Safe results are highlighted in green, unsafe items are flagged in red, and a summary explains the findings based on your personal rules.

5. Result Logging in Airtable

Finally, a complete record of the scan—including the product image link, extracted text, analysis result, and timestamp—is saved to a "Scan Log" table in Airtable, building your personal food safety database.

Quick Setup Guide

You can have this personal food scanner running in under 30 minutes by following these steps.

  1. Copy the Airtable Base: Use the provided link to duplicate the "Ingredients Brain" base into your Airtable workspace. This contains the necessary tables (Watchlist, Preferences, Scan Log).
  2. Create Your Telegram Bot: Message @BotFather on Telegram, follow the prompts to create a new bot, and securely save the provided HTTP API token.
  3. Import the Template: Download the JSON file using the button above and import it into your n8n workspace.
  4. Configure Credentials: In your n8n workflow, set up the credentials for Telegram Bot, Airtable, and Google Gemini API using the tokens and keys you collected.
  5. Personalize Your Watchlist: Go to your Airtable base and populate the "Watchlist" table with the ingredients you need to avoid or look for, marking their status as "Active".
  6. Test It: Send a clear photo of an ingredient label to your new Telegram bot. You should receive an analysis reply within seconds.

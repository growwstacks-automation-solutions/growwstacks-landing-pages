What This Workflow Does

Every time you host a Zoom meeting, webinar, or training session, valuable attendee data gets lost in the platform. Manually copying names and emails from participant lists is tedious, error-prone, and delays follow-up. This automation solves that problem by instantly capturing every attendee who joins your Zoom meeting and creating a structured record in your Airtable CRM.

The workflow transforms Zoom from a simple video tool into a powerful lead generation engine. It captures not just basic contact information but also meeting context—topic, join time, duration, and participation data—giving your sales and marketing teams rich insights for personalized follow-up.

Instead of spending hours after each meeting on data entry, you get an automatically populated CRM ready for action. This means faster response times to hot leads, better tracking of meeting ROI, and a complete audit trail of who attended which sessions.

How It Works

1. Zoom Webhook Trigger

When a participant joins your Zoom meeting, Zoom sends a real-time webhook notification to the automation. This contains the attendee's name, email address, meeting ID, and join timestamp.

2. Data Processing & Enrichment

The workflow processes the raw data, extracting key information and formatting it for your CRM. It can check for existing contacts, calculate meeting duration, and add contextual tags based on the meeting topic or type.

3. Airtable Record Creation

Processed data is sent to your Airtable base, creating or updating records in your designated "Attendees" table. Each record includes meeting details, contact information, and timestamps for complete tracking.

4. Optional Follow-up Triggers

Once the record is created, you can trigger additional actions like sending welcome emails, adding contacts to email sequences, or notifying sales reps about high-value attendees.

What You'll Need

  1. A Zoom account with admin access to create webhook applications
  2. An Airtable account with a base set up for CRM/contact management
  3. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the automation
  4. API credentials for both Zoom and Airtable
  5. Basic understanding of your Airtable table structure (field names and types)

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure Zoom webhook in your Zoom App Marketplace, pointing it to your n8n webhook URL with the "meeting.participant_joined" event.
  3. Set up Airtable connection in n8n using your API key, and update the base and table IDs in the workflow.
  4. Test with a live meeting by having someone join a Zoom session and verifying the record appears in Airtable.
  5. Customize fields to match your specific Airtable structure and add any additional data processing you need.

Pro tip: Add a "Lead Score" field in Airtable that automatically calculates based on meeting duration. Attendees who stay longer are warmer leads and should get faster follow-up.

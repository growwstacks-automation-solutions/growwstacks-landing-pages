What This Workflow Does

Content creation bottlenecks often start at the ideation stage. Team members have great ideas in Telegram chats, but those ideas get lost in message history or require manual transcription into content calendars. This creates friction, delays, and missed opportunities.

This n8n workflow solves that by creating a seamless pipeline from idea to execution. When someone submits a content request via your Telegram bot, the workflow automatically processes it through Gemini AI to expand the concept, then creates a fully structured entry in your Notion content database—complete with title, draft content, channel assignment, due dates, and categorization.

The system maintains context through conversation memory, allowing the AI to understand follow-up requests and refine ideas based on previous discussions. This transforms spontaneous Telegram messages into actionable content briefs without any manual intervention.

How It Works

1. Telegram Trigger Captures Requests

The workflow listens for messages in your designated Telegram bot. Team members can submit content ideas naturally, as if messaging a colleague. The bot can be configured to accept text, voice messages, or even document attachments for reference materials.

2. AI Processing and Enhancement

Each request passes through Gemini AI with conversation memory. The AI analyzes the request, suggests improvements, generates a content outline, and extracts key metadata like content type, target audience, and suggested channels. This transforms raw ideas into structured briefs.

3. Notion Database Creation

The enhanced content brief automatically creates a new page in your Notion content database. Fields include title, AI-generated draft, assignee, due date, content type, status, and reference links. The entry is immediately visible to your entire content team for planning and assignment.

4. Notification and Confirmation

The workflow sends a confirmation back to the requester via Telegram, showing the created Notion entry with a direct link. Content managers receive notifications in their preferred channel (Slack, email, or Telegram) about new requests requiring attention.

What You'll Need

  1. Telegram Bot Token: Create a bot via @BotFather on Telegram and obtain your API token
  2. Google Gemini API Key: Get a free API key from Google AI Studio for the Gemini model
  3. Notion Integration: Create an internal integration in Notion and share your content database with it
  4. n8n Instance: Self-hosted n8n or n8n.cloud account to run the workflow
  5. Notion Content Database: Pre-configured database with properties for title, content, status, assignee, and due date

Pro tip: Start with a simple Notion database structure, then expand as your workflow matures. The template includes common fields, but you can customize properties to match your existing content planning process.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure credentials in n8n for Telegram, Google Gemini, and Notion
  3. Update the Notion database ID in the workflow to point to your content database
  4. Test with a Telegram message to verify the end-to-end flow works correctly
  5. Share your bot link with team members and start capturing ideas automatically

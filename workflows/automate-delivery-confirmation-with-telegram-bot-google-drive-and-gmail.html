What This Workflow Does

Manual delivery confirmation processes create bottlenecks, errors, and communication gaps between drivers in the field and the logistics office. This free n8n workflow template solves that by automating the entire proof-of-delivery (POD) process. It enables truck drivers to instantly confirm deliveries using a Telegram bot on their smartphones, automatically logs all data (shipment number, GPS location, photo) into a Google Sheet, and sends a professional confirmation email to your team.

For small to medium logistics businesses without a full Transportation Management System (TMS), this automation replaces paper delivery notes, manual phone calls, and error-prone data entry. It provides real-time visibility into delivery status, reduces administrative overhead by up to 80%, and creates a digital audit trail for every shipment. The system guides drivers through a simple chat interface, ensuring consistent, complete data capture for every delivery.

How It Works

The automation creates a seamless, step-by-step interaction between your driver and your backend systems.

Step 1: Driver Initiates Chat

When a delivery is complete, the driver messages your Telegram bot. The bot immediately responds with a welcome message and clear instructions, eliminating confusion and ensuring every driver follows the same procedure regardless of experience level.

Step 2: Capture Shipment Details

The bot asks for the shipment number. The driver types it in, and n8n validates and stores this information. This simple step ensures every delivery is tied to the correct order in your system, preventing misrouted confirmations.

Step 3: Record GPS Location

The bot prompts the driver to share their live location directly from Telegram. n8n extracts the precise latitude and longitude, providing undeniable proof of delivery location. This geospatial data is invaluable for route optimization and resolving delivery disputes.

Step 4: Upload Delivery Photo

The driver takes a photo of the delivered goods and sends it through the chat. n8n automatically uploads this image to a designated Google Drive folder and saves the file link. This visual proof dramatically reduces "item not received" claims and provides immediate verification.

Step 5: Send Automated Confirmation

Once all data is collected, n8n composes and sends a detailed email via Gmail to your logistics team. The email includes the shipment number, timestamp, Google Maps link to the location, and the photo attachment—everything needed to close the delivery loop instantly.

Pro tip: You can extend this workflow to also send SMS notifications to customers, update internal dashboards, or trigger invoice generation—all automatically upon delivery confirmation.

What You'll Need

  1. A Telegram account and a bot token from BotFather
  2. A Google Cloud Platform project with the Drive API, Sheets API, and Gmail API enabled
  3. API credentials (OAuth 2.0 client ID and secret) from your Google Cloud Console
  4. A Google Drive folder to store delivery photos
  5. A Google Sheet with columns: shipmentNumber, recordTime, gpsLatitude, gpsLongitude, cargoPicture, deliveryTime
  6. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the workflow
  7. Drivers with smartphones capable of running Telegram

Quick Setup Guide

Import this template and follow these steps to get your automated delivery system running in under 30 minutes.

  1. Download and Import: Click the download button above to get the JSON file. In your n8n instance, go to Workflows → Import from File and select the downloaded template.
  2. Configure Telegram Bot: Create a bot via @BotFather on Telegram, copy the API token, and paste it into the Telegram Trigger node in your n8n workflow.
  3. Set Up Google Services: In Google Cloud Console, create OAuth 2.0 credentials. Enable the Drive, Sheets, and Gmail APIs. Add these credentials to the respective n8n nodes.
  4. Prepare Your Google Sheet: Create a new Sheet with the required column headers. Note the Sheet ID (from the URL) and update the Google Sheets node in the workflow.
  5. Create a Drive Folder: Make a folder for delivery photos in Google Drive. Copy the folder ID and update the Google Drive node.
  6. Test the Flow: Activate the workflow and message your Telegram bot. Follow the prompts to complete a test delivery confirmation and verify the data appears in your Sheet and the email sends correctly.
  7. Train Your Team: Share the bot username with your drivers. Provide simple instructions: "Message this bot when a delivery is complete and follow its prompts."

Pro tip: Use n8n's built-in test functionality to simulate a delivery confirmation before going live. This helps you verify all connections and data formatting without involving drivers.

What This Workflow Does

Manual Etsy research is slow, inconsistent, and impossible to scale. Product analysts spend hours browsing listings, comparing prices, and reading reviews—missing trends and wasting valuable time. This automation solves that by systematically extracting Etsy data at scale and transforming it into actionable intelligence.

The workflow combines Bright Data's robust scraping infrastructure with Google Gemini's AI analysis capabilities. It automatically collects product listings, pricing details, reviews, and seller information from Etsy, then processes this data to generate structured reports on market trends, competitor positioning, and customer sentiment.

Instead of manual browsing, you get automated intelligence pipelines that deliver daily competitor updates, pricing alerts, and product opportunity reports. This transforms market research from a sporadic activity into a continuous, data-driven process.

How It Works

Step 1: Configure Target URLs

The workflow starts by receiving Etsy search URLs or category links you want to monitor. These can be static inputs or dynamically fed from a spreadsheet or database. You define exactly which products, niches, or competitors you want to track.

Step 2: Bright Data Scraping

Bright Data's Web Unlocker service accesses Etsy pages, bypassing JavaScript rendering and anti-scraping measures. It extracts complete listing data—product titles, descriptions, prices, images, review counts, seller details, and shipping information—maintaining consistency across thousands of pages.

Step 3: Data Cleaning & Structuring

Raw scraped content is cleaned and formatted for analysis. The workflow removes irrelevant HTML, normalizes pricing formats, extracts key attributes, and prepares the data for AI processing. This ensures Gemini receives structured, readable input.

Step 4: Google Gemini Analysis

Google Gemini processes the cleaned data with custom prompts. It identifies pricing patterns, summarizes review sentiments, extracts common product features, compares listings across sellers, and highlights emerging trends. The AI transforms raw data into interpretable insights.

Step 5: Output & Notification

Analyzed results are saved locally for persistence and sent via webhook to your chosen destination—Google Sheets, Airtable, Slack, email, or internal databases. You receive structured reports without manual intervention.

What You'll Need

  1. Bright Data Account – Sign up for their Web Unlocker service to access Etsy reliably.
  2. Google Gemini API Key – Obtain access through Google AI Studio or Vertex AI.
  3. n8n Instance – Self-hosted n8n or n8n.cloud account to run the workflow.
  4. Target URLs – Etsy search links or category pages you want to monitor.
  5. Output Destination – Google Sheets, webhook endpoint, or database to receive results.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template – Import the JSON file into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure Bright Data credentials – Create Header Authentication credentials with your Web Unlocker token.
  3. Set Gemini API key – Add your Google Gemini credentials in the AI node.
  4. Define Etsy URLs – Update the "Set Etsy Search Query" node with your target links.
  5. Choose output destination – Modify the webhook node to point to your preferred service.
  6. Test and activate – Run the workflow once to verify data extraction, then schedule regular execution.

Pro tip: Start with a single Etsy category to validate the workflow, then expand to multiple niches. Use dynamic inputs from Google Sheets to rotate search URLs automatically.

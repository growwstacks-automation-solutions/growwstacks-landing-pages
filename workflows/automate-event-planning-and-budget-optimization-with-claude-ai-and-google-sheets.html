What This Workflow Does

Event planning is notoriously manual, time-consuming, and prone to budget oversights. This workflow solves that by automating the entire lifecycle—from initial client briefs to post-event reporting—using Claude AI's analytical capabilities and Google Sheets as your central data hub.

It transforms raw client requirements, vendor databases, and budget estimates into optimized, insight-driven event plans. The system automatically calculates cost savings, identifies reliable vendors, assesses risks, and generates comprehensive reports—all without manual intervention.

Whether you're planning corporate conferences, weddings, or marketing events, this automation reduces planning time by 60-70%, cuts vendor costs through intelligent comparisons, and ensures every decision is backed by data-driven insights.

How It Works

Step 1: Data Collection

The workflow reads multiple Google Sheets containing client briefs, budget estimates, actual costs, and vendor databases. It consolidates this information into a unified dataset for analysis.

Step 2: AI Analysis

Claude AI processes the consolidated data, identifying optimal vendor combinations, calculating potential savings, assessing event risks, and generating tailored recommendations based on your specific requirements.

Step 3: Plan Generation

The AI creates a comprehensive event plan including budget allocations, vendor selections, timeline suggestions, and risk mitigation strategies—all formatted as structured JSON for easy implementation.

Step 4: Automated Reporting

The finalized plan is saved back to Google Sheets, shared with your team via Slack, and emailed to stakeholders as an interactive executive report—complete with ROI calculations and savings breakdowns.

What You'll Need

  1. A Google Sheets account with spreadsheets structured for client briefs, budgets, costs, and vendor data.
  2. Access to Claude AI via Anthropic API (claude-3-5-sonnet model recommended).
  3. Slack workspace for team notifications (optional but recommended).
  4. Email service (SMTP, Gmail, or SendGrid) for sending executive reports.
  5. n8n instance (version 1.50+ with LangChain support).

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template JSON file and import it into your n8n workflow editor.
  2. Configure credentials for Google Sheets, Claude AI, Slack, and your email service.
  3. Update spreadsheet IDs to point to your existing Google Sheets.
  4. Set the trigger—either daily schedule (7 AM) or webhook for immediate execution.
  5. Run a manual test to confirm data flow, AI analysis, and reporting outputs.

Pro tip: Start with a single test event to validate the workflow before scaling to multiple events. This ensures your data structures align with the automation logic.

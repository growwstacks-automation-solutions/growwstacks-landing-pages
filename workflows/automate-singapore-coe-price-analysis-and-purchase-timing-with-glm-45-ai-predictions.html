What This Workflow Does

Singapore's Certificate of Entitlement (COE) system creates volatile pricing that can swing thousands of dollars between biweekly bidding rounds. Manually tracking these prices, analyzing trends, and deciding when to purchase a vehicle consumes hours each month and often leads to missed opportunities.

This automation solves that problem by systematically scraping LTA data, processing historical trends, using AI to forecast future prices, and sending intelligent alerts when buying windows appear. It transforms guesswork into data-driven decision making, helping both individual buyers and fleet managers optimize their vehicle purchase timing.

The workflow runs automatically every two weeks (aligning with COE bidding cycles), analyzes the latest results, updates your historical database, generates new predictions, and notifies you via email or Telegram if conditions suggest an optimal purchase period.

How It Works

1. Data Collection & Scraping

A scheduled trigger activates every second and fourth Wednesday of the month (after LTA results are published). The workflow uses HTTP Request nodes to scrape official COE price data from OneMotoring, extracting prices for all categories (A, B, E), quota numbers, and bidding statistics.

2. Data Processing & Storage

Raw data is cleaned, formatted, and enriched with calculations like moving averages, volatility metrics, and seasonal adjustments. Processed data is then stored in Google Sheets, creating a historical database that grows with each bidding round, enabling long-term trend analysis.

3. AI Prediction Generation

The workflow sends the historical dataset to an AI model (GLM-4.5 or similar) via API, requesting forecasts for the next 6 bidding rounds. The AI analyzes patterns, considers economic indicators if available, and returns probability-weighted predictions for each COE category.

4. Analysis & Recommendation Engine

Current prices are compared against predictions and historical averages. Conditional logic evaluates whether current prices represent a dip relative to forecasted trends, generating "Buy," "Wait," or "Monitor" recommendations with confidence scores.

5. Alert Delivery

When conditions meet your configured thresholds (e.g., price 15% below predicted average), the workflow triggers notifications via Gmail, Telegram, or other channels. Alerts include specific price data, recommendation rationale, and suggested action timeframe.

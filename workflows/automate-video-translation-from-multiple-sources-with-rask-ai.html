What This Workflow Does

This n8n workflow solves the time-consuming and expensive problem of manual video translation. Instead of hiring translators, voice actors, and video editors for each language, this automation connects directly to Rask AI's translation API to process videos from multiple sources automatically.

Whether you have training videos on Google Drive, marketing content on YouTube, or internal communications on Vimeo, this workflow can translate them into dozens of languages while preserving voice characteristics, tone, and context. The automation handles the entire process: uploading the video, waiting for processing, creating translation projects, and retrieving the final translated output ready for distribution.

For businesses expanding globally, content creators building multilingual audiences, or organizations needing accessible training materials, this automation reduces translation costs by 90%+ and cuts turnaround time from weeks to hours.

How It Works

Step 1: Video Source Input

The workflow accepts video URLs from supported sources including YouTube, Google Drive, Amazon S3, Vimeo, or direct links. You can trigger it manually, schedule regular translations, or connect it to other systems that generate video content.

Step 2: Authentication & Upload

Using OAuth2 authentication with Rask AI, the workflow securely uploads your video to their processing platform. The system validates credentials and prepares the media for translation while maintaining data security throughout the transfer.

Step 3: Translation Processing

Rask AI's advanced AI analyzes the video content, transcribes speech, translates the text while preserving context, and generates new audio with voice cloning technology that matches the original speaker's characteristics and emotional tone.

Step 4: Status Monitoring & Retrieval

The workflow includes intelligent waiting and status checking to monitor translation progress. Once complete, it automatically retrieves the translated video file and makes it available for download or forwards it to your preferred storage or distribution channels.

Pro tip: Combine this workflow with content management systems or learning platforms to automatically publish translated videos to different regional channels, creating a fully automated multilingual content pipeline.

What You'll Need

  1. Rask AI Account: You'll need API access with OAuth2 credentials (Client ID and Client Secret) from your Rask account settings.
  2. n8n Instance: Either self-hosted n8n or n8n.cloud account with workflow execution capabilities.
  3. Video Sources: Accessible video URLs from supported platforms (YouTube, Google Drive, S3, Vimeo, or direct links).
  4. Target Languages: Determine which languages you need translations for, ensuring they're supported by Rask AI's language options.
  5. Storage Destination: A place to store translated videos (cloud storage, CMS, or distribution platforms).

Quick Setup Guide

1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.

2. Configure Rask AI authentication in n8n under the name "raskOAuth2" using your Client ID and Secret from app.rask.ai/account.

3. Set your video URL and target language in the workflow trigger or input nodes (e.g., "en-us" for US English).

4. Test with sample values: Use link: "https://static.rask.ai/samples/sample_1.mp4" and dst_lang: "en-us" to verify the full translation cycle.

5. Connect output nodes to your storage or distribution systems (Google Drive, YouTube, CMS, etc.) to automate the entire pipeline.

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of manual lead handling for WordPress websites using Elementor forms. When a visitor submits a contact form, their information often sits unnoticed until someone manually checks and processes it. This delay leads to missed opportunities and poor customer experience.

The workflow instantly captures every Elementor form submission via webhook, stores the lead details (name, email, phone, budget, project details) directly into an Excel or Google Sheets CRM, sends a notification email to the website owner, and automatically replies to the customer with a thank‑you message. Additionally, it can run scheduled promotional email campaigns using the accumulated lead list.

Essentially, it replaces multiple plugins and services (like Mailchimp or separate CRM plugins) with a single, fully automated chain that ensures no lead is ever lost and every inquiry receives immediate professional attention.

How It Works

Step 1: Capture Elementor Form Submission

A webhook trigger listens for submissions from your Elementor Pro form. When someone fills out the form, Elementor sends the data (including custom fields) to the workflow instantly.

Step 2: Store Lead in Spreadsheet CRM

The workflow uses a Sheets/Excel node to append the submission as a new row in your CRM spreadsheet. This creates a live, searchable database of all inquiries with contact history and details.

Step 3: Send Admin Notification Email

A Gmail node sends a formatted email to you or your team containing all the submission details. This enables quick follow‑up and keeps everyone informed without manual checking.

Step 4: Send Auto‑Reply to Customer

Another Gmail node sends a confirmation/thank‑you email to the person who submitted the form. This creates instant acknowledgment, builds trust, and sets professional expectations.

Step 5: Update CRM with Email Status

The workflow writes back to the spreadsheet, marking “Owner Mail Sent” and “Customer Mail Sent” as YES (or logs errors). This provides full auditability and tracking.

Step 6: Run Scheduled Marketing Campaigns

A schedule trigger (daily, weekly, monthly) activates a promotional email batch. It reads the email list from your CRM sheet, loops through each contact, sends personalized emails, and logs “Campaign Email Sent” for each.

What You'll Need

  1. A WordPress website with Elementor Pro (or any form capable of sending webhook data).
  2. An n8n instance (cloud or self‑hosted) to run the workflow.
  3. An Excel file or Google Sheet set up as your CRM (with columns for name, email, phone, etc.).
  4. A Gmail account (or other email service) configured in n8n for sending emails.
  5. The webhook URL from your n8n workflow added to your Elementor form’s webhook action.

Pro tip: Test the webhook connection thoroughly by submitting a test form before going live. Ensure your spreadsheet columns match the data fields from your form to avoid mapping errors.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template JSON file and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure the Webhook node: copy its URL and add it to your Elementor form’s webhook action settings.
  3. Set up the Sheets/Excel node: connect to your spreadsheet (Excel file or Google Sheet) and map the form fields to your column headers.
  4. Configure the Gmail nodes: authenticate your Gmail account and customize the email content for both the admin notification and customer auto‑reply.
  5. Adjust the Schedule Trigger node: set your desired frequency for promotional campaigns (daily, weekly, etc.).
  6. Activate the workflow and test with a real form submission to verify everything works.

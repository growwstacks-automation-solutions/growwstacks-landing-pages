Automated Animal Advocacy News Research & Weekly Digest

Automatically receive weekly email updates summarizing the latest animal rights, welfare, vegetarianism, and veganism news using Claude AI and Serper.

What This Workflow Does

Animal advocacy organizations, activists, and campaigners often spend hours each week manually searching for relevant news articles, policy updates, and scientific developments. This scattered research approach leads to missed opportunities, delayed responses, and inefficient resource allocation.

This automation solves that problem by automatically gathering verified news strictly from the past week, compiling the information into a clean, well-structured HTML email, and sending it to your team. URLs are never altered or omitted, ensuring you can directly access source material. The workflow runs on a weekly schedule but can be easily adjusted for daily updates.

By automating this research process, organizations gain consistent, timely intelligence without manual effort, allowing teams to focus on strategic action rather than information gathering.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Trigger

The workflow activates on a weekly schedule (configurable for daily use). It automatically begins the research process without manual intervention.

2. Multi-Tool Research Agent Execution

It calls a specialized sub-workflow that uses Serper for web search and Claude AI for intelligent summarization. This agent gathers news specifically from the past week based on your configured topics.

3. Information Compilation

The gathered articles, summaries, and source URLs are organized into a structured dataset. The workflow ensures all original URLs remain intact for verification.

4. HTML Email Generation

A clean, branded HTML template is populated with the research findings. The email includes categorized summaries, key points, and direct links to source articles.

5. Automated Delivery

The completed digest is sent via email to your specified recipients using your configured SMTP or email service.

What You'll Need

  1. A running n8n instance with internet access
  2. Valid API keys for Serper (web search) and Claude AI/OpenRouter (summarization)
  3. The Multi-tool Research Agent subworkflow installed in your n8n environment
  4. Configured SMTP or email sending credentials (Gmail, SendGrid, etc.)
  5. Basic understanding of n8n workflow import and configuration

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import this workflow JSON file into your n8n instance.
  2. Add and install the required research subworkflow: "Multi-tool Research Agent for Animal Advocacy."
  3. Configure your API keys in n8n credentials for Serper and your chosen AI provider.
  4. Set your research topics, summary instructions, and recipient email in the "Set Preferences" node.
  5. Adjust the schedule trigger node to control when emails are sent (weekly default).
  6. Test the full workflow with a single execution to ensure proper operation before enabling the schedule.

Automated Blog Content Tracking with RSS Feeds

What This Workflow Does

Manually checking multiple blogs for new content is a repetitive, time-consuming task that often leads to missed updates. This automated workflow solves that by continuously monitoring RSS feeds from your chosen blogs, filtering articles based on publication date, and delivering only the fresh, relevant posts to your preferred apps.

Think of it as your personal content assistant. It runs on a schedule you set, scans all your feeds, and separates new articles from old ones. You can then send these new articles to Slack channels, save them to a Google Sheet for content calendars, add them to a Notion database, or trigger any other action you need—all without lifting a finger.

The template is intentionally open-ended, giving you full control over what happens with the filtered content. Whether you're a marketer curating industry news, a founder tracking competitor blogs, or a developer building a content aggregation tool, this workflow provides the reliable foundation.

How It Works

The automation follows a clear, logical path to ensure you only receive content that matters.

1. Trigger & Feed List

The workflow starts with a manual trigger (easily swapped for a schedule). It reads a list of RSS feed URLs you configure. You can add as many blogs as you need—industry news, competitor sites, or niche publications.

2. RSS Feed Processing

For each feed URL, the workflow fetches the latest articles, extracting key details like title, link, publication date, and description. It handles the parsing so you get clean, structured data ready for use.

3. Date-Based Filtering

This is the core intelligence. Each article's publication date is compared against a configurable time window (default: 60 days). Articles published within that window pass through; older ones are filtered out. This ensures your notifications or databases aren't flooded with outdated content.

4. Output Routing

New articles are routed to the "true" path for your custom integrations. You decide what happens next: post to Slack, append to a spreadsheet, send an email digest, or trigger another workflow. The old articles are routed separately, useful for archiving or analysis.

Pro tip: Replace the manual trigger with a Schedule Trigger to run this automation daily or weekly, ensuring you always have the latest content without manual intervention.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n Instance: A self-hosted n8n setup or n8n.cloud account.
  2. RSS Feed URLs: The blog or news site URLs you want to monitor (usually found as `/rss` or `/feed`).
  3. Destination App Credentials: If you're sending data elsewhere (like Slack, Google Sheets, Airtable), have those API keys or connections ready.
  4. Basic Understanding of n8n: Familiarity with dragging nodes and configuring simple fields is helpful but not required.

Quick Setup Guide

Get this workflow running in your n8n environment in under 10 minutes.

  1. Download & Import: Click the "Download Template" button above and import the JSON file into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure RSS Feeds: In the "blogs to track" node, replace the example URLs with your target RSS feed URLs.
  3. Set Time Window: Adjust the "max_content_age_days" node to your desired freshness filter (e.g., 7 for weekly content).
  4. Add Your Output: Connect a node (like Slack, Google Sheets, or Email) to the "true" output of the filter to handle new articles.
  5. Test & Schedule: Execute once to verify, then replace the manual trigger with a Schedule node for hands‑off automation.

Pro tip: Start with 2–3 RSS feeds to verify the workflow works as expected, then expand your list. This helps avoid configuration errors with a large batch upfront.

Automated Meeting Summaries: Google Meet to Slack with AI

Free n8n workflow that automatically joins meetings, transcribes conversations, and posts AI-generated summaries to Slack—eliminating manual note-taking.

What This Workflow Does

Meetings are essential for collaboration but create a documentation burden. Teams waste hours manually taking notes, transcribing conversations, and distributing summaries. Important action items get missed, decisions aren't properly documented, and team members who couldn't attend remain in the dark.

This automation solves that by creating a completely hands-free meeting documentation system. It monitors your Google Calendar for scheduled Google Meet calls, automatically joins with a bot, transcribes the entire conversation, processes the transcript through AI to extract key decisions and action items, and posts a structured summary to your designated Slack channel—all without any human intervention.

The result is consistent, searchable meeting documentation that ensures accountability, improves alignment, and saves each team member 2-3 hours per week previously spent on manual note-taking and follow-up.

How It Works

1. Meeting Detection & Joining

The workflow monitors your Google Calendar via the Calendar API. When a Google Meet starts, it automatically joins the meeting using Vexa.ai's meeting bot functionality. The bot remains silent in the meeting, only capturing audio for transcription purposes.

2. Transcription Processing

As the meeting progresses, the audio is streamed to transcription services. Once the meeting concludes, the complete transcript is compiled and prepared for analysis. The workflow includes error handling for poor audio quality or meeting disruptions.

3. AI Analysis & Summarization

The raw transcript is sent to OpenAI's GPT-4o model with specific prompts to extract: key decisions made, action items with assignees, discussion topics covered, unresolved questions, and next steps. The AI structures this information into a clear, concise summary format.

4. Slack Delivery & Archiving

The formatted summary is posted to your designated Slack channel, tagged with relevant participants, and includes the meeting title, date, and duration. The summary becomes instantly searchable within Slack, creating a living knowledge base of team decisions.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (self-hosted version required for community nodes)
  2. Google Calendar API credentials with access to the calendar containing your meetings
  3. Vexa.ai account and API key for the meeting bot functionality
  4. OpenAI API credentials with GPT-4o access for summarization
  5. Slack bot token with permissions to post to your desired channel
  6. Basic understanding of n8n workflow configuration

Pro tip: Start with a test calendar containing dummy meetings to verify the workflow functions correctly before applying it to your actual work calendar. This prevents accidental meeting joins during important calls.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance
  2. Configure Google Calendar node with your calendar ID and appropriate meeting detection filters
  3. Set up Vexa.ai credentials in the HTTP Request nodes that handle meeting joining
  4. Add your OpenAI API key to the AI summarization nodes
  5. Connect Slack with your bot token and specify the target channel
  6. Test with a scheduled meeting to verify end-to-end functionality
  7. Activate the workflow and let it run automatically

Automated Patient Vitals Monitoring & Alerts with Philips IntelliVue & Google Sheets

Continuously track patient health data, detect critical conditions instantly, and notify clinical teams—all automated.

What This Workflow Does

Manual patient monitoring creates dangerous gaps in clinical care. Nurses juggle multiple patients, handwritten charts introduce errors, and critical changes can go unnoticed until it's too late. This automation solves these problems by creating a continuous, intelligent monitoring system that never sleeps.

The workflow connects Philips IntelliVue patient monitoring devices directly to your clinical operations. It automatically collects vital signs—heart rate, oxygen saturation, blood pressure, respiratory rate—every 30 seconds, analyzes the data against clinical thresholds, and instantly alerts the appropriate staff when values become concerning. All data is simultaneously logged to Google Sheets for trend analysis, shift reports, and compliance documentation.

This isn't just about replacing paper charts; it's about creating a proactive safety net that identifies deteriorating patients earlier, reduces clinical workload, and provides data-driven insights for better treatment decisions.

How It Works

Step 1: Continuous Data Collection

The workflow polls connected Philips IntelliVue devices at regular intervals (configurable from 30 seconds to 5 minutes). It retrieves current vital sign readings through the device's API or gateway interface, ensuring real-time data capture without manual intervention.

Step 2: Data Validation & Enrichment

Each reading is validated for completeness and accuracy. The system enriches the raw data with patient context—matching device IDs to patient records, adding timestamps, and calculating trends from previous readings to provide clinical context.

Step 3: Clinical Logic Application

Configurable clinical rules evaluate each reading. Basic thresholds (like SpO2 below 90%) trigger immediately, while trend-based rules (gradual heart rate increase over 2 hours) identify subtler deterioration. The logic can be customized for different patient populations—post-op, ICU, or general ward settings.

Step 4: Intelligent Alert Routing

When conditions are met, alerts are routed based on severity and context. Critical alerts go directly to assigned nurses via instant messaging. Non-critical trends might notify charge nurses for review. All alerts include patient details, vital values, and suggested actions.

Step 5: Comprehensive Data Logging

Every reading—normal or abnormal—is logged to Google Sheets with full metadata. This creates searchable records for clinical rounds, quality audits, and population health analysis. Automated reports can be generated for shift handovers or physician review.

What You'll Need

  1. Philips IntelliVue monitoring devices with network connectivity and API access
  2. n8n instance (self-hosted or cloud) with network access to your medical devices
  3. Google Sheets for data logging (free Google account works)
  4. Alert channel configured (Email, Slack, Microsoft Teams, or SMS via Twilio)
  5. Patient-device mapping (simple spreadsheet linking device IDs to patient records)
  6. Clinical threshold guidelines for your patient population

Pro tip: Start with broader thresholds and refine based on actual alert patterns. Too many false alerts cause alarm fatigue; too few miss real issues. Review the first week's alerts with clinical staff to calibrate the system to your specific needs.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance
  2. Configure the Philips IntelliVue node with your device gateway URL and authentication credentials
  3. Connect the Google Sheets node to your designated spreadsheet for data logging
  4. Set up your alert channel (Email, Slack, etc.) with appropriate recipient addresses
  5. Adjust clinical thresholds in the Switch node to match your protocols
  6. Test with simulated data to verify alert triggers and data logging
  7. Go live with one patient, monitor for 24 hours, then expand to additional patients

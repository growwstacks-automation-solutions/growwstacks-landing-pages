What This Workflow Does

Manual resume screening is one of the most time-consuming tasks in recruitment. HR teams spend hours reviewing hundreds of applications, often leading to inconsistent evaluations, unconscious bias, and missed qualified candidates. This automation solves that problem by leveraging AI to handle the initial screening at scale.

The workflow automatically monitors your Google Drive for new resumes, extracts their content, compares them against your job description using Llama 4 AI, and generates matching scores with clear rationales. It categorizes candidates as "Shortlisted," "Rejected," or "Keep in View" based on objective criteria, and updates tracking spreadsheets in real-time. This transforms a days-long manual process into minutes of automated analysis.

Pro tip: This workflow is particularly valuable during high-volume hiring periods or for roles with standardized requirements. It ensures every candidate gets a fair, consistent evaluation based solely on job-relevant criteria.

How It Works

1. Resume Collection & Monitoring

The workflow starts by monitoring a designated Google Drive folder for new resume uploads. This can be configured to watch specific folders where candidates submit applications or where your team collects resumes from various sources.

2. Content Extraction & Processing

When a new resume is detected, the system extracts text content from PDFs, Word documents, or other formats. It simultaneously retrieves the current job description from a Google Doc, ensuring the evaluation criteria are always up-to-date.

3. AI Analysis & Scoring

The core intelligence comes from Llama 4 AI via Groq. The AI compares the resume against the job description, generating three key outputs: a matching score (1-10), a decision (Shortlisted/Rejected/KIV), and a detailed rationale explaining the assessment.

4. Results Organization & Notification

Results are automatically logged in a Google Sheets tracker with candidate names, scores, verdicts, and reasons. The system can also move resumes to categorized folders (Shortlisted/Rejected) and send email notifications to recruiters when processing is complete.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in under 30 minutes:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure Google Workspace credentials in n8n for Drive, Docs, and Sheets access.
  3. Set up your Groq API connection by adding your API key to the AI node.
  4. Create the folder structure in Google Drive: main folder with "New," "Shortlisted," and "Rejected" subfolders.
  5. Prepare your job description in Google Docs and note its document ID.
  6. Update the workflow nodes with your specific folder IDs, document IDs, and email addresses.
  7. Test with sample resumes to verify scoring accuracy and adjust AI prompts if needed.
  8. Activate the workflow and start processing real applications automatically.

Pro tip: Start with a small batch of historical resumes to calibrate the AI scoring against your team's previous decisions. This helps fine-tune the prompts for your specific hiring criteria.

Automated Smart Price Monitor with AI Prediction

Track product prices 24/7, analyze trends with artificial intelligence, and get intelligent email alerts when it's the optimal time to buy.

What This Workflow Does

Manual price tracking is time-consuming, inconsistent, and often misses optimal buying opportunities. This automation solves that by creating a 24/7 intelligent monitoring system that watches product prices, learns pricing patterns, and tells you exactly when to make your purchase.

The workflow functions as your personal procurement assistant. It continuously checks prices for your specified products, analyzes historical data using AI to predict future trends, maintains a complete price history in Google Sheets, and sends you targeted email notifications only when the conditions are truly favorable—not just when a price drops temporarily.

Beyond simple alerts, the system provides strategic insights. It calculates urgency scores, predicts whether prices will continue dropping or start rising, and gives you confidence levels for each recommendation. This transforms reactive price checking into proactive, intelligent purchasing strategy.

How It Works

Step 1: Product List Management

The workflow begins by reading your product monitoring list from Google Sheets. This includes product URLs, your target prices, and notification email addresses. The system is designed to scale—you can monitor 5 products or 500 with the same automation.

Step 2: Price Data Collection

For each product, the automation extracts current pricing information from retailer websites. It handles different website structures and can be configured for various e-commerce platforms beyond the initial Amazon setup.

Step 3: Historical Analysis & AI Prediction

This is where the intelligence happens. The system retrieves previous price data from your Google Sheets history, then sends this information to OpenAI's AI models. The AI analyzes patterns, identifies trends, and predicts whether the current price represents a good buying opportunity.

Step 4: Decision Making & Notification

Based on the AI's analysis and your predefined criteria, the workflow decides whether to send an alert. If conditions are met, it generates a detailed email with the price analysis, prediction confidence, and recommended action. All data is then updated back to your Google Sheets for future reference.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for e-commerce businesses monitoring competitor pricing, procurement teams tracking supplier costs, retailers managing inventory purchase timing, and individuals who want to optimize personal shopping for electronics, travel, or seasonal items.

Marketing agencies managing client ad spend will find it valuable for tracking platform pricing fluctuations. SaaS companies can monitor their own subscription tiers and competitor pricing. What You'll Need

  1. Google Account – For accessing Google Sheets and Gmail services
  2. OpenAI API Key – A paid account is required for the AI analysis (costs are minimal, typically pennies per analysis)
  3. n8n Instance – Either self-hosted or using n8n.cloud
  4. Product URLs – Links to the items you want to monitor (initially configured for Amazon but adaptable)
  5. Google Cloud Project – With Sheets API enabled (free tier is sufficient)

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your price monitoring system running in under 30 minutes:

  1. Prepare Your Google Sheet – Create a spreadsheet with "Products" and "Price_History" tabs using the column structure provided in the template.
  2. Get API Credentials – Set up your Google Cloud project and obtain an OpenAI API key.
  3. Import the Workflow – Download the template and import it into your n8n instance.
  4. Configure Connections – Connect the Google Sheets, Gmail, and OpenAI nodes with your credentials.
  5. Add Your Products – Populate your Google Sheet with the items you want to monitor.
  6. Test & Activate – Run a manual test, then replace the manual trigger with a schedule trigger for automatic operation.

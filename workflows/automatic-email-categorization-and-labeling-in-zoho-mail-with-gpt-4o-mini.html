What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the universal business problem of email overload. Instead of manually sorting through dozens or hundreds of daily emails, this workflow uses AI to automatically read, understand, and categorize every incoming email in your Zoho Mail account. It analyzes both the subject line and body content to determine whether an email belongs to support inquiries, billing questions, HR matters, or other custom categories you define.

The system then applies the appropriate Zoho Mail label automatically, ensuring emails are organized from the moment they arrive. This means your support team sees only support emails, your finance department gets billing questions immediately, and HR receives job applications without manual forwarding. The result is faster response times, reduced human error, and significant time savings across your organization.

How It Works

1. Email Trigger & Authentication

When a new email arrives in your Zoho Mail inbox, the workflow triggers automatically. It first authenticates with Zoho's API using OAuth credentials to securely access your email data without storing passwords.

2. Label Mapping

The workflow fetches all existing labels from your Zoho Mail account and creates a mapping between label names and their internal IDs. This ensures the correct label is applied regardless of your account's regional settings (.com, .eu, or .in domains).

3. AI Analysis & Classification

The email's subject and body are sent to an AI text classification model (like GPT-4o-mini via OpenRouter). The AI analyzes the content's intent and context, then predicts the most appropriate category from your predefined list.

4. Routing & Label Application

Based on the AI's classification, the workflow routes the email through the appropriate branch and applies the corresponding Zoho Mail label. The final labeling step is deactivated by default for testing, then activated once you're confident in the system's accuracy.

What You'll Need

  1. A Zoho Mail account with API access enabled
  2. Pre-created labels in Zoho Mail for each category (Support, Billing, HR, etc.)
  3. Zoho OAuth credentials configured in n8n
  4. Your Zoho Mail account region (.com, .eu, or .in) for correct API endpoints
  5. Access to an AI model provider (OpenRouter or similar) for text classification
  6. Basic familiarity with n8n for initial configuration

Pro tip: Start by testing with a small folder of historical emails before applying to your entire inbox. This lets you fine-tune category definitions and verify accuracy without disrupting live communications.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template into your n8n instance
  2. Create labels in Zoho Mail for each category you want to use
  3. Configure Zoho OAuth credentials in the "Get Access Token" node
  4. Update the Account ID in the "Set Account ID" node with your Zoho Mail account ID
  5. Adjust API base URL to match your Zoho account region (.com, .eu, or .in)
  6. Test with sample emails by temporarily activating the workflow
  7. Activate the final label application node once you're satisfied with accuracy

What This Workflow Does

Manual customer service is slow, error-prone, and doesn't scale. This automation solves that by connecting Google Forms, WhatsApp Business, and Google Sheets into a seamless system that handles inquiries, sends confirmations, and manages follow-ups automatically.

When a customer submits a Google Form (for appointments, support requests, or bookings), they instantly receive a WhatsApp notification using pre-approved message templates. If they reply to the bot, their message and user data are logged in a Google Sheet. Your team can then review pending replies, prepare responses in the sheet, and with one status change, the bot delivers personalized replies via WhatsApp—all within the 24-hour messaging window.

This transforms a multi-step manual process into a hands-off workflow that operates 24/7, ensuring no customer message goes unanswered and every form submission triggers immediate, professional communication.

How It Works

The workflow orchestrates communication between three core platforms: your customer-facing form, your internal data sheet, and WhatsApp's messaging API.

Step 1: Form Submission Trigger

A Google Form submission acts as the starting trigger. The workflow captures all form data—name, contact info, service requested, preferred time—and immediately prepares a WhatsApp message.

Step 2: WhatsApp Template Message Delivery

Using the WhatsApp Business Cloud API, the system sends a pre-approved template message to the customer's phone number from the form. This could be a booking confirmation, appointment reminder, or inquiry acknowledgment.

Step 3: Incoming Message Handling

If the customer replies to the message, the WhatsApp Trigger node captures their response. The workflow extracts the message text, sender info, and timestamp, then appends this conversation to a dedicated Google Sheet for tracking.

Step 4: Response Management & Delivery

Your team reviews the "Pending Replies" sheet, writes responses in the "ReplyText" column, and marks the status as "Ready." The workflow periodically checks for ready replies, sends them via WhatsApp one by one, and updates the sheet status to "Sent."

Pro tip: Use Google Sheets' filter views to create a "Ready to Send" tab for your support team, making it easy to see which customer replies need attention without scrolling through the entire sheet.

What You'll Need

  1. Meta Business Account: Access to the Meta Business Suite with WhatsApp Business API enabled.
  2. WhatsApp Business API Credentials: App ID, App Secret, Access Token, and Business Account ID from the Meta Developers Portal.
  3. Approved Message Templates: At least one pre-approved WhatsApp message template for sending notifications (like appointment confirmations).
  4. Google Workspace Account: To create and manage the Google Form and connected Google Sheet.
  5. n8n Instance: Either n8n.cloud (hosted) or a self-hosted n8n installation with webhook capabilities.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in your business:

  1. Import the Template: Download the JSON file above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure WhatsApp Credentials: In the WhatsApp Business node, add your Access Token and Business Account ID from the Meta Developers Portal.
  3. Set Up the WhatsApp Trigger: Add your App ID and App Secret to the trigger node, then copy its webhook URL back into your Meta app settings as the callback URL.
  4. Connect Google Sheets: Authenticate n8n with your Google account and update the spreadsheet ID in the Google Sheets nodes to point to your own sheet.
  5. Update Message Templates: Replace the template name in the WhatsApp node with your approved template name from Meta.
  6. Test the Flow: Submit a test form entry and verify the WhatsApp notification arrives. Reply to the bot and check that it appears in your Google Sheet.

Important: WhatsApp only allows free-form messages within 24 hours of a customer's last message. For follow-ups beyond 24 hours, you must use approved template messages. Design your conversation flow accordingly.

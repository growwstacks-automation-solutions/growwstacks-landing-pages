What This Workflow Does

Manual customer onboarding and segmentation is a time-consuming, error-prone process that delays marketing campaigns and creates inconsistent customer experiences. Every time a new user signs up, makes a purchase, or completes an action, someone has to manually add them to Customer.io and assign them to the right segments.

This n8n workflow automates that entire process. It listens for new customer data from your forms, payment processors, or databases, automatically creates a customer profile in Customer.io, and adds them to predefined segments based on their attributes or behavior. This ensures instant, accurate segmentation for timely email campaigns, personalized messaging, and streamlined customer journeys.

By eliminating manual steps, you reduce administrative overhead, minimize human error, and ensure every customer enters your marketing funnel immediately—increasing engagement and conversion rates while freeing your team to focus on strategy.

How It Works

The workflow acts as a bridge between your customer data sources and Customer.io, orchestrating the flow of information with precision.

Step 1: Trigger on New Customer Data

The automation starts when new customer information arrives. This could be triggered by a webhook from your signup form, a new row in a Google Sheet, a completed Typeform submission, or a payment confirmation from Stripe. The workflow captures essential details like email, name, signup source, plan type, or any custom attributes you need for segmentation.

Step 2: Create Customer in Customer.io

Using the captured data, the workflow calls Customer.io's API to create or update a customer profile. It maps the incoming fields to Customer.io's customer attributes, ensuring the profile is complete and accurate. If the customer already exists, the workflow can update their profile with new information instead of creating duplicates.

Step 3: Add Customer to Segments

Based on the customer's data, the workflow determines which segments they belong to—such as "Free Trial Users," "Enterprise Customers," "High-Value Leads," or "Inactive Users." It then makes a second API call to add the customer to those specific segments in Customer.io. Logic can be applied here: if they signed up through a webinar, add to "Webinar Attendees"; if they purchased a specific product, add to "Product X Buyers."

Step 4: Trigger Follow-up Actions

Once the customer is created and segmented, the workflow can trigger additional automations. This might include sending a welcome email series, notifying your sales team in Slack, updating a CRM like HubSpot, or scheduling a task in your project management tool. This creates a fully automated onboarding and engagement pipeline.

What You'll Need

  1. A running n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted).
  2. A Customer.io account with API credentials (Site ID and API Key).
  3. A source of customer data (webhook, form submission, database, spreadsheet, or payment processor).
  4. Segments already created in your Customer.io workspace (or knowledge of how to create them via API).
  5. Basic understanding of how to import a JSON workflow into n8n.

Pro tip: Test the workflow with a single customer first to verify your field mappings and segment logic before activating it for all new signups.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and open it in your n8n editor.
  2. Configure your trigger node to match your data source (e.g., set up a webhook URL or connect to your Google Sheet).
  3. Add your Customer.io credentials in the Customer.io nodes (Site ID and API Key).
  4. Map your data fields to ensure customer attributes (email, name, custom fields) are passed correctly.
  5. Define your segment IDs in the "Add to Segment" node. You can find these in your Customer.io segment settings.
  6. Test the workflow with sample data, then activate it to start automating your customer segmentation.

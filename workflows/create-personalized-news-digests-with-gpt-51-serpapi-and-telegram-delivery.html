What This Workflow Does

Staying current with fast-moving topics like AI, machine learning, or your specific industry is overwhelming. You either drown in daily noise or miss crucial developments between weekly digests. This AI News Agent workflow solves that by delivering a curated newsletter only when there's genuinely relevant news—not on a fixed schedule that often sends fluff or misses timing.

The system acts as your personal editorial assistant. It fetches fresh news twice daily, uses AI to evaluate whether the content is worth sending, enriches articles with additional context, and delivers a clean, formatted digest via Telegram. It remembers what it has sent previously to avoid repetition and respects your configured frequency preferences.

This transforms news consumption from a time-consuming chore into an efficient, value-driven process. You get signal without noise, insights without effort, and stay informed without being overwhelmed.

How It Works

The workflow is organized into five intelligent stages that mimic how a human editor would curate content, but with automation scale and consistency.

Stage 1: Schedule & Configuration

A schedule trigger runs the workflow twice daily (configurable). A Set node defines your topics, output language, and frequency guardrails—minimum and maximum days between sends. This ensures the system checks regularly but only interrupts you when it has something valuable.

Stage 2: Fetch & Store News

The workflow splits your topics into individual queries and uses SerpAPI's DuckDuckGo News engine to fetch real-time articles. Each article is stored in a persistent data table with automatic deduplication, creating a searchable history of all discovered content.

Stage 3: Filtering & Frequency Guardrails

This is where the system gets smart. It retrieves your last newsletter timestamp, filters out articles published before that date, and checks if you're still within your minimum days-between-sends window. If it's too soon, the workflow stops gracefully—no unnecessary processing.

Stage 4: AI Editorial Decision

The core intelligence. GPT-5.1 evaluates today's filtered articles against your previous newsletters and a comprehensive set of editorial criteria: relevance, novelty, informational value, and duplication. It decides whether to send and selects the top 3-5 articles. A fallback rule ensures you get at least something if it's been too long since your last update.

Stage 5: Content Enrichment & Delivery

Selected articles are enriched using Tavily web search for fact-checking and additional context. The AI rewrites titles and summaries for clarity and impact. Finally, a formatted newsletter is sent to your Telegram chat or channel, and the content is stored for future deduplication.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure credentials for SerpAPI, Tavily, OpenAI, and Telegram in n8n's credential management.
  3. Edit the "Set topics and language" node with your interests, preferred language, and frequency preferences.
  4. Update the Telegram node with your chat or channel ID for delivery.
  5. Adjust the schedule trigger if you prefer different check-in times than the default 9 AM and 5 PM.
  6. Activate the workflow and let it run for a few days to build up its memory of what it has sent.

Pro tip: Start with broad topics (like "AI" or "your industry") and narrow down based on what the AI selects. The system will show you what it considers valuable, helping you refine your interests over time.

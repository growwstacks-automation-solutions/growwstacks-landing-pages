What This Workflow Does

This workflow solves a critical business problem: information trapped in documents. Companies accumulate thousands of PDFs—research papers, manuals, contracts, reports—that become digital filing cabinets. Finding specific information requires manual searching, which is slow, inconsistent, and misses connections across documents.

The Document Q&A with RAG automation transforms this static content into an interactive knowledge base. It uses Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to understand questions in natural language, searches through all uploaded documents for relevant information, and generates accurate, cited answers. This isn't just search—it's comprehension and synthesis.

Think of it as giving every team member a personal research assistant who has read every document in your organization and can instantly answer questions with precise references. Legal teams can query contract clauses, support teams can find technical specifications, and researchers can extract insights from papers without reading hundreds of pages.

How It Works

The automation follows a three-stage process that mimics how a human researcher would work, but at machine speed and scale.

1. Document Processing & Embedding

When you upload a PDF, the workflow extracts all text content and breaks it into logical chunks. Each chunk is converted into a numerical representation (embedding) using AI models. These embeddings capture semantic meaning, so "customer support protocol" and "client assistance procedure" are recognized as similar concepts even if the wording differs.

2. Vector Storage & Indexing

The embeddings are stored in Weaviate, a vector database designed for AI applications. This creates a searchable knowledge graph of your document content. Unlike traditional keyword search, vector search understands context and meaning, finding relevant passages even when the exact keywords don't match.

3. Intelligent Question Answering

When someone asks a question, the system converts it to an embedding, searches Weaviate for the most relevant document chunks, and feeds both the question and retrieved context to OpenAI's language model. The AI generates a concise answer grounded in your actual documents, complete with source references. This prevents hallucinations and ensures accuracy.

What This Workflow Does

This automation solves a critical problem for fashion e‑commerce: showing customers how clothing actually looks and moves on a body, without expensive photoshoots or physical samples. It uses the Kling AI API (via PiAPI) to generate professional 360° virtual try‑on videos from just two images: a model photo and a clothing item photo.

The workflow automatically processes these inputs, calls the AI video generation service, and delivers a downloadable video file showing the garment realistically fitted onto the model with a smooth rotating view. This transforms static product listings into dynamic, engaging content that reduces purchase hesitation and cuts return rates significantly.

Beyond basic try‑ons, you can configure it for separates (upper + lower clothing), batch process entire catalogs, or trigger generation whenever new products are added to your store. It turns weeks of manual content creation into minutes of automated processing.

How It Works

Step 1: Input Configuration

You provide your PiAPI authentication key and specify the input image URLs. The workflow supports both dress‑type garments (single clothing image) and separates (upper + lower images). These URLs can come from your CMS, e‑commerce platform, or cloud storage.

Step 2: AI Processing

The workflow sends the images to the Kling API, which uses advanced diffusion models to understand garment geometry, texture, and how it should drape on the human form. The AI generates a coherent video sequence showing the clothing from all angles.

Step 3: Output Delivery

Once processing completes, the workflow captures the generated video URL and makes it available for download. You can extend the automation to automatically upload this video to your product pages, social media scheduler, or digital asset management system.

Pro tip: Use consistent lighting and neutral backgrounds in your model photos for best results. The AI handles various poses, but front‑facing, full‑body shots yield the most accurate garment fitting.

Quick Setup Guide

1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n workspace.

2. Configure the PiAPI node with your X‑API‑Key from your PiAPI dashboard.

3. Replace the sample image URLs in the workflow with your actual model and product image URLs.

4. Test the workflow with one product to verify the output quality matches your expectations.

5. Automate the trigger by connecting it to your e‑commerce platform's webhook or schedule it for batch processing.

Implementation note: Start with a small batch of 5‑10 products to fine‑tune your input image quality and workflow settings before scaling to your entire catalog.

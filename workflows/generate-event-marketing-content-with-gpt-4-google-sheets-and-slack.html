What This Workflow Does

Event marketing teams spend countless hours crafting emails, social posts, and advertisements for every campaign. This repetitive work often leads to burnout, inconsistent messaging, and missed opportunities for personalization. Manual content creation simply doesn't scale when you're managing multiple events, audience segments, and distribution channels simultaneously.

This n8n workflow solves that problem by automating the entire content generation process. It connects your event data in Google Sheets with AI (GPT-4) to produce tailored marketing materials in seconds. The system reads event details, audience preferences, and brand guidelines, then outputs polished content ready for email campaigns, social media scheduling, and ad platforms—all while notifying your team in Slack.

Beyond just generating text, the workflow ensures brand consistency, enables rapid A/B testing at scale, and creates a repeatable system that improves with each event. You maintain complete control while eliminating the grunt work of starting from a blank page for every campaign.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence that transforms raw event data into marketing-ready content across multiple channels.

1. Data Collection & Trigger

The workflow begins when new event details are added to your designated Google Sheet. This could be a new row for an upcoming webinar, conference, or product launch. The system captures all relevant information: event name, date, location, target audience, key messaging points, and speaker details.

2. AI Content Generation

Using GPT-4 or your preferred AI model, the workflow analyzes the event data against your stored brand voice guidelines. It then generates multiple content variations including email sequences (save-the-date, reminder, follow-up), social media posts optimized for different platforms (LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram), and ad copy for paid campaigns.

3. Content Formatting & Organization

The raw AI output is structured into usable formats. Email content gets proper HTML formatting with clear calls-to-action. Social posts are separated by platform with appropriate hashtags and character limits. All content is timestamped and categorized for easy review.

4. Distribution & Notification

The finalized content is saved back to Google Sheets for tracking and future reference. Simultaneously, a summary is posted to your designated Slack channel, giving your team visibility and the opportunity to provide immediate feedback before anything goes live.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with access to the necessary nodes
  2. Google Sheets with your event data structured (columns for event name, date, description, audience, etc.)
  3. OpenAI API key or access to another LLM service (GPT-4, Claude, etc.)
  4. Slack workspace with permissions to post to your desired channel
  5. Basic understanding of your brand voice and key messaging pillars to guide the AI

Pro tip: Before running the workflow live, create a "test event" in your Google Sheet. This lets you verify the AI output matches your brand tone and that all integration connections work properly without affecting real campaigns.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in your n8n environment.

  1. Import the template: Download the JSON file using the button above and import it into your n8n instance via the workflow import function.
  2. Configure Google Sheets: Update the "Read Event Details" node with your specific Google Sheet ID and worksheet name. Ensure your sheet has the expected column headers.
  3. Set up AI credentials: In the "AI Agent for Event Plan" node, add your OpenAI API key (or equivalent). You may want to adjust the temperature and other parameters to control creativity vs. consistency.
  4. Connect Slack: Add your Slack bot token to the notification node and specify the channel where content summaries should be posted.
  5. Test with sample data: Add a test event row to your Google Sheet and manually trigger the workflow. Review the generated content in Slack and the output sheet.
  6. Go live: Once satisfied with the test output, activate the workflow's webhook or schedule trigger to run automatically when new events are added.

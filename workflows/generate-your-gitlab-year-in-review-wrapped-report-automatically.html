What This Workflow Does

For developers and engineering teams, tracking yearly contributions across GitLab projects can be a manual, time-consuming process. This automation solves that by automatically generating a personalized “GitLab Wrapped” report—a visually engaging year-in-review that summarizes your commits, merges, reviews, activity stats, and overall impact.

The workflow forks (or finds your existing fork) of the gitlab-wrapped project, configures CI/CD environment variables, triggers the GitLab pipeline, and monitors completion—delivering your wrapped report at a predictable URL without any manual steps. It turns scattered GitLab data into a cohesive, shareable achievement summary.

How It Works

Step 1: Fork & Configure

The workflow first checks if you already have a fork of the gitlab-wrapped repository. If not, it creates one automatically. It then sets up the necessary CI/CD environment variables—including your GitLab Personal Access Token (PAT), username, target year, and instance URL.

Step 2: Trigger Pipeline

Once the repository is ready, the automation triggers the GitLab CI/CD pipeline that runs the wrapped generation script. This pipeline pulls your contribution data from GitLab’s API, processes it, and builds the static report.

Step 3: Monitor & Deliver

The workflow polls the pipeline status every two minutes until completion. When done, your personalized wrapped report is published at https://YOUR-USERNAME.gitlab.io/gitlab-wrapped. You receive a notification with the live link.

Pro tip: This workflow works with both GitLab.com and self-hosted GitLab instances. You can generate reports for any past year—not just the current one.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for individual developers who want a retrospective of their yearly work, engineering team leads who need visibility into team contributions, and DevOps managers who require automated reporting for performance reviews or stakeholder updates. It’s also valuable for open-source contributors and tech companies that prioritize developer recognition and data-driven retrospectives.

What You'll Need

  1. A GitLab account (on GitLab.com or your self-hosted instance).
  2. A GitLab Personal Access Token with api, read_repository, and write_repository scopes.
  3. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) to run the workflow.
  4. Basic familiarity with GitLab pipelines (no deep expertise required).

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template JSON file and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Create your GitLab PAT with the required scopes and keep it secure.
  3. Fill the workflow’s form nodes with your GitLab username, PAT, instance URL (defaults to gitlab.com), and target year.
  4. Activate the workflow—it will handle everything automatically.
  5. Your wrapped report will be available at the generated URL within minutes.

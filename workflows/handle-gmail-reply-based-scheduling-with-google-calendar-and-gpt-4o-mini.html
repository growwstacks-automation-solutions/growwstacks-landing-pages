What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the tedious back-and-forth of scheduling meetings via email. Instead of manually reading replies, checking calendar availability, and creating events, this workflow listens for email responses, uses AI to understand the intent, and automatically books the meeting in Google Calendar.

It's designed for businesses that prefer personal email communication over impersonal booking pages. The system handles the complete scheduling loop: sending availability options, monitoring replies, parsing free-text responses, detecting confirmations or requests for alternatives, creating calendar events, and sending confirmation emails—all without manual intervention.

How It Works

1. Email Monitoring

The workflow continuously monitors your Gmail inbox for replies to scheduling emails. It identifies emails in specific threads or with particular labels, ensuring only relevant scheduling conversations are processed.

2. AI-Powered Response Analysis

Using GPT-4o-mini, the system analyzes the email content to determine the user's intent. It can distinguish between confirmations ("Yes, 2 PM works"), requests for alternatives ("Can we do Thursday instead?"), and ambiguous responses that need clarification.

3. Calendar Event Creation

When a confirmation is detected, the workflow automatically creates an event in Google Calendar with all relevant details: participants, time, duration, meeting title, and description. It handles time zone conversions and checks for conflicts.

4. Automated Follow-up

Based on the analysis, the system sends appropriate follow-up emails: calendar invitations for confirmed meetings, alternative time options for requests, or clarification questions for ambiguous responses.

Pro tip: Configure the AI to recognize your specific scheduling phrases and preferences. Train it on past email exchanges to improve accuracy for your communication style.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in your business:

  1. Download the template using the button above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Connect your accounts by adding credentials for Gmail, Google Calendar, and OpenAI in n8n's credential management.
  3. Configure email filters to specify which emails should trigger the workflow (specific labels, subjects, or senders).
  4. Customize AI prompts to match your scheduling language and common response patterns.
  5. Test with sample emails to ensure the system correctly interprets confirmations and requests.
  6. Activate the workflow and start automating your scheduling process immediately.

Pro tip: Start with a small volume of emails to test the system before fully automating all scheduling. Monitor the first 10-20 automated bookings to ensure accuracy and make adjustments as needed.

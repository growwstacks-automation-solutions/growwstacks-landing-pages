What This Workflow Does

This template solves a critical but often overlooked challenge in modern business automation: the environmental impact of AI operations. As companies increasingly integrate AI into their workflows, the carbon footprint of these operations remains invisible and unmeasured. This workflow makes that impact tangible and actionable.

By implementing a simple calculation step based on the open Ecologits.ai methodology, you can now estimate the grams of CO₂ equivalent generated by each AI call in your automations. This transforms abstract energy consumption into concrete sustainability metrics that can inform decision-making, support environmental reporting, and drive more efficient AI usage across your organization.

The solution empowers businesses to build responsible AI applications while maintaining operational efficiency. It provides the data needed to balance technological advancement with environmental stewardship—a growing concern for customers, investors, and regulatory bodies alike.

How It Works

The workflow implements a standardized approach to AI carbon accounting that can be integrated into any existing automation.

Step 1: Configure Conversion Factors

A dedicated node stores the gCO₂e per token conversion factor specific to your AI model and server region. You update this once with values from Ecologits.ai's latest data, ensuring calculations reflect actual infrastructure emissions.

Step 2: Capture AI Output

Your existing AI node (OpenAI, Anthropic, or other LLM) generates text content as part of your business process. The workflow captures this output for analysis without disrupting your core automation logic.

Step 3: Estimate Token Usage

The system calculates an approximate token count by dividing the character length of the AI output by 4. This provides a reasonable estimation when exact token counts aren't available from the AI provider.

Step 4: Calculate Carbon Footprint

The estimated token count multiplies by your configured conversion factor, producing the carbon footprint in grams of CO₂ equivalent for that specific AI operation. This metric can then be logged, reported, or used to trigger efficiency optimizations.

Pro tip: For maximum accuracy, use actual token counts from AI providers when available (like OpenAI's completion_tokens). This improves precision from ~80% to ~95% compared to character-based estimation.

Quick Setup Guide

Integrating carbon tracking into your AI workflows takes about 15 minutes with this template:

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance
  2. Update conversion factors in the configuration node with values from ecologits.ai/latest for your AI models
  3. Connect the calculation nodes after your existing AI operations in each relevant workflow
  4. Map the AI output field to capture text from your specific AI nodes
  5. Test with sample executions to verify carbon calculations match expected ranges
  6. Deploy to production and begin collecting sustainability metrics alongside your business data
  7. Optional: Add aggregation nodes to sum emissions across multiple AI operations for comprehensive reporting

Implementation note: Start with your highest-volume AI workflows first. Even basic tracking on 20% of AI calls typically captures 80% of emissions, providing immediate visibility with minimal implementation effort.

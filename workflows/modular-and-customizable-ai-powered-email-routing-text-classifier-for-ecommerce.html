AI-Powered Email Routing for eCommerce

Automatically classify contact form messages and route them to the correct department—sales, support, or logistics—instantly.

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

For eCommerce businesses, customer inquiries pour in daily: "Where's my order?", "Can I get a quote?", "This product is broken." Manually reading and forwarding each email is slow, error-prone, and delays critical responses. This automation solves that.

This n8n workflow acts as an intelligent mailroom for your digital business. It connects to your contact form, uses AI (like GPT-4) to instantly understand the intent of each message, and automatically routes it to the correct team's inbox or spreadsheet. A quote request goes to sales, an order issue goes to logistics, and a general question goes to support—all without human intervention.

How It Works

The process is a seamless, multi-step pipeline that transforms a raw customer message into a directed action.

1. Form Submission Trigger

The workflow is activated whenever a customer submits your website contact form. It captures all data: name, email, and their message.

2. AI-Powered Text Classification

The core of the workflow. The customer's message is sent to an AI model pre-configured with your business categories (e.g., "Request Quote," "Order Issue," "Product Info"). The AI analyzes the text and assigns the most relevant category with a high degree of accuracy.

3. Intelligent Email Routing

Based on the AI's classification, the workflow branches. It prepares a tailored email—including the original message and customer details—and sends it to the pre-defined email address for that department (e.g., sales@yourcompany.com, support@yourcompany.com).

4. Centralized Logging

Every interaction is logged. The workflow appends a record to a Google Sheet (or similar), creating a searchable audit trail with timestamp, customer info, assigned category, and destination. This allows for easy reporting and quality checks.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for any business that receives a steady stream of customer inquiries and wants to improve response times and organization.

  • eCommerce Stores: Handle order status, returns, and pre-sales questions efficiently.
  • B2B Service Companies: Route quote requests to sales and project inquiries to the correct team lead.
  • Small & Medium Businesses: Appear more professional and responsive without hiring a dedicated support person to sort emails.
  • Agencies: Manage incoming client requests and route them to project managers, designers, or developers.

Pro tip: Start with 4-5 broad categories. You can always add more later as you analyze the logs and see common query patterns.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted).
  2. Access to an AI/LLM provider like OpenAI (for GPT-4) or a similar service for the classification step.
  3. A contact form that can trigger a webhook (most modern form builders can) or an email inbox you can monitor.
  4. Email sending credentials (SMTP) for a service like Gmail, SendGrid, or your company email server.
  5. A Google Sheets document (or another database) for logging the classified inquiries.

Quick Setup Guide

Get this automation running in your environment in under 30 minutes.

  1. Import the Template: Download the JSON file above. In your n8n dashboard, go to "Workflows" and use the "Import from file" option to load it.
  2. Configure the Trigger: Set up the "On Form Submission" node to connect to your specific contact form provider (e.g., Typeform, Google Forms) using the provided credentials or webhook URL.
  3. Set Up AI Classification: In the "Text Classifier" node, connect your OpenAI (or other LLM) API credentials. Define your categories (e.g., Quote, Order, Support) with clear descriptions to guide the AI.
  4. Connect Your Email & Sheets: For each "Email Send" node, enter the SMTP details for your email service and the specific recipient address for each department. Connect the "Google Sheets" node to your log sheet.
  5. Test & Activate: Use a test form submission with a message like "I need a price quote for 100 units." Verify the AI classifies it correctly, an email is sent to your sales address, and a row is added to your log sheet. Then activate the workflow.

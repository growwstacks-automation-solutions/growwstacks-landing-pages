Monitor Lead Response Time SLA Breaches with Google Sheets & Telegram Alerts

Automatically track un-replied leads and get instant alerts when response times exceed your service level agreement.

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

Missed leads mean lost revenue. This automation solves a critical business problem: ensuring every lead gets a timely response according to your Service Level Agreement (SLA). The workflow continuously monitors your Google Sheets lead tracker, identifies any "Un-replied" leads that have exceeded your predefined response window (e.g., 15 minutes), and sends immediate, actionable alerts to your team via Telegram.

By automating SLA monitoring, you eliminate manual spreadsheet checking, reduce human error, and ensure your sales or support team can act on hot leads while they're still engaged. The alert includes key lead details and a direct link to the specific row in Google Sheets, allowing for one-click access to follow up.

How It Works

The workflow follows a logical sequence to detect and alert on SLA breaches efficiently.

Step 1: Scheduled Check

A scheduler node triggers the workflow to run at regular intervals (e.g., every 5 minutes). This ensures near real-time monitoring without constant manual execution.

Step 2: Fetch Lead Data

The workflow connects to your Google Sheets and retrieves all current lead entries, including columns for name, contact info, status, and timestamp.

Step 3: Filter for SLA Breaches

A logic node filters the data. It looks for leads where the status is "Un-replied" AND the time difference between now and the lead's timestamp exceeds your SLA threshold (e.g., 15 minutes).

Step 4: Enrich Alert Data

For each breached lead, the workflow generates a direct URL link to that specific row in Google Sheets. This saves your team from searching and gets them to the right data instantly.

Step 5: Send Telegram Alert

A formatted message containing the lead's name, contact details, time since submission, and the direct sheet link is sent to your designated Telegram group or channel, prompting immediate action.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for sales teams, customer support departments, agencies, and any business that receives inbound leads and has committed to a response time SLA. It's particularly valuable for small to medium-sized businesses using Google Sheets as a lightweight CRM, startups needing to maximize conversion rates from limited leads, and teams distributed across different time zones who need a centralized alert system.

What You'll Need

  1. A Google Sheets document with columns for Lead Name, Email/Phone, Status (Replied/Un-replied), and a Timestamp.
  2. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with the Google Sheets and Telegram nodes installed.
  3. Google Service Account credentials or OAuth setup to allow n8n to read your sheet.
  4. A Telegram Bot created via @BotFather and added to your team's chat, along with its API token.
  5. Basic understanding of your desired SLA window (e.g., 15 min, 30 min, 1 hour).

Pro tip: Use a dropdown data validation rule in Google Sheets for the "Status" column to ensure only "Replied" or "Un-replied" are entered. This prevents workflow errors due to typos.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your SLA monitoring live in under 15 minutes.

  1. Import the Template: Download the JSON file above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure Google Sheets: In the Google Sheets node, replace the placeholder Spreadsheet ID and Sheet name with your own. Set up your Google account connection in n8n's credentials.
  3. Set Your SLA Window: In the IF node, adjust the time comparison logic. Change "15 minutes" to your preferred SLA duration.
  4. Connect Telegram: In the Telegram node, add your Bot Token and the Chat ID of the group where alerts should be sent.
  5. Test and Activate: Click "Execute Workflow" to test with your live sheet data. Once confirmed, activate the workflow and set the Schedule trigger to run every 5 minutes.

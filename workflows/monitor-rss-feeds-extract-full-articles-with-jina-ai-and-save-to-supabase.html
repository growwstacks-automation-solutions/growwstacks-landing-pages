What This Workflow Does

This workflow solves a fundamental problem with RSS feeds: they typically provide only short snippets or summaries, forcing you to visit each website manually to read full articles. For content creators, marketers, researchers, and businesses tracking industry trends, this manual process wastes hours each week and creates inconsistent data collection.

The automation monitors your specified RSS feeds on a schedule, filters for new content based on publication date, uses Jina AI Reader to extract the complete article text from each webpage, and saves structured data (title, URL, full content, author, publication date) directly to your Supabase database. It transforms fragmented snippets into a searchable, organized knowledge base automatically.

Whether you're building a content curation system, conducting competitive analysis, creating training data for AI models, or simply staying updated without constant manual checking, this workflow eliminates the copy-paste grind and ensures you capture complete information from your sources.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Trigger & RSS Feed Fetching

The workflow begins with a schedule trigger (default: daily) that activates the automation. It then fetches RSS feeds from your configured list of blogs or news sources, parsing each feed for new article entries with titles, URLs, publication dates, and authors.

2. Content Age Filtering

A filtering step checks each article's publication date against your configured maximum age (e.g., "last 60 days"). Only articles published within your specified timeframe proceed, preventing outdated content from clogging your database and ensuring you capture only recent, relevant information.

3. Full Article Extraction with Jina AI

For each qualifying article, the workflow uses the Jina AI Reader service (https://r.jina.ai/) to visit the original article URL and extract clean, structured text. This AI-powered extraction removes ads, navigation, sidebars, and other webpage clutter, delivering just the valuable article content that RSS feeds typically withhold.

4. Data Structuring & Database Storage

The extracted data is organized into consistent fields and saved to your Supabase table. Each record includes the article title, source URL, complete content snippet, publication date, author, and customizable status/type fields. Your knowledge base grows automatically with structured, query-ready data.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted) with workflow execution capabilities
  2. List of RSS feed URLs for blogs, news sites, or publications you want to monitor
  3. Supabase account with a project and table configured to receive article data (or alternative storage like Google Sheets/Airtable)
  4. Basic understanding of RSS feeds and how to find them (often /rss or /feed at domain root)

Pro tip: Use a web-browsing enabled AI assistant (like Perplexity or ChatGPT with browsing) to quickly find RSS feed URLs for any website. Simply ask "What is the RSS feed URL for [website]?"

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure your RSS feed list in the "blogs to track" Set node—replace example URLs with your actual feed URLs.
  3. Set content age filter in the "max_content_age_days" node—adjust from default 60 days to your preferred timeframe (e.g., 7 for weekly collection).
  4. Connect your Supabase database in the final node using your Project URL and Service Role Key, mapping fields to your table columns.
  5. Adjust schedule trigger frequency if needed (daily is recommended for most use cases).
  6. Activate the workflow and test with a manual run to verify data flows correctly into your database.

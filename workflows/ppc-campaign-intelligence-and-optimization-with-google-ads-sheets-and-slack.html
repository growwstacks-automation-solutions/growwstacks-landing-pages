What This Workflow Does

Manual Google Ads reporting is a time sink that leads to delayed decisions and missed opportunities. Marketing teams often spend hours each day compiling spreadsheets, leaving little time for actual optimization. This workflow solves that by automating the entire monitoring and intelligence process.

It acts as a daily AI analyst for your paid search campaigns. Every morning, it connects to your Google Ads account, pulls performance data for all active campaigns, and evaluates them using a smart scoring system. High-performing campaigns trigger scaling recommendations sent directly to Slack, while underperformers flag urgent issues. Everything is logged to a central Google Sheets dashboard, creating a searchable history of your account's health.

The result is a proactive system that identifies budget waste and scaling opportunities in real-time, turning reactive campaign management into a strategic, data-driven operation. You move from looking at yesterday's data to acting on today's insights.

How It Works

The automation runs on a scheduled trigger, ensuring you start each day with a fresh performance snapshot.

Step 1: Data Fetch & Aggregation

The workflow connects to the Google Ads API and retrieves key metrics—clicks, impressions, costs, conversions, and conversion rates—for all campaigns configured in your account. It structures this raw data for analysis.

Step 2: AI-Powered Performance Scoring

Each campaign is scored (0-100) across four dimensions: Click-Through Rate (ad relevance), Conversion Rate (landing page effectiveness), Cost per Conversion (profitability), and Traffic Volume (scale readiness). The AI engine weights these based on your business goals to calculate an overall performance grade.

Step 3: Intelligent Routing & Alerting

Campaigns are categorized as Excellent (75+), Good (55-74), Fair (35-54), or Underperforming (0-34). Excellent campaigns trigger a Slack alert to your growth channel with specific scaling suggestions. Underperforming campaigns send an urgent alert to your troubleshooting channel with root-cause analysis.

Step 4: Dashboard Update & Historical Logging

All campaign data, scores, and categories are written to a "Daily Performance" tab in Google Sheets for at-a-glance review. A separate "Campaign Log" tab maintains a timestamped history, enabling trend analysis and quarter-over-quarter reporting.

Step 5: Automated Reporting & Action Plans

Finally, personalized email reports are generated. Top performers receive a "Scale Plan" email with projected ROI from increased spend. Struggling campaigns get a "Troubleshooting Guide" email with step-by-step optimization actions for the assigned manager.

What You'll Need

  1. A Google Ads account with active campaigns and API access enabled.
  2. A Google account with permissions to create and edit a Google Sheets spreadsheet.
  3. A Slack workspace where you can create incoming webhooks for alerts (one for opportunities, one for issues).
  4. An SMTP email provider (like Gmail, SendGrid, or Amazon SES) to send performance reports.
  5. Basic familiarity with n8n to configure node credentials and map your data fields.

Pro tip: Before you start, create your Google Sheets dashboard with two tabs: "Daily Performance" and "Campaign Log." Pre-populate the headers (Campaign Name, Date, Clicks, Cost, Conversions, Score, Category, Notes) to make the setup smoother.

Quick Setup Guide

Import this template and be up and running in under 30 minutes.

  1. Download & Import: Click the "Download Template" button above and import the JSON file into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure Credentials: In the "Google Ads" node, authenticate with your Google Ads account. Do the same for the "Google Sheets" and "Email Send" nodes.
  3. Set Up Slack Webhooks: Create two incoming webhooks in your Slack workspace—one for #ppc-opportunities and one for #ppc-issues—and paste the URLs into the respective Slack nodes.
  4. Adjust Scoring Thresholds: Review the "AI Performance Analysis" node. Modify the point values and grade thresholds (Excellent: 75+, Good: 55-74, etc.) to match your account's historical performance.
  5. Map Your Spreadsheet: In the "Update Campaign Dashboard" and "Log All Campaigns" nodes, select your Google Sheets file and the correct tab names.
  6. Test & Activate: Run the workflow once manually to ensure all connections work and data flows correctly. Then, activate the schedule trigger for daily execution at your preferred time (e.g., 9 AM).

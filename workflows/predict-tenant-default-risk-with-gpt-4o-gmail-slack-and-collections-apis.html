Predict Tenant Default Risk with AI & Automation

What This Workflow Does

Manual tenant screening is time-consuming, subjective, and often misses early warning signs of financial distress. Property managers and landlords spend hours checking references, verifying employment, and reviewing credit reports, only to make gut-feel decisions that can lead to costly defaults and evictions.

This intelligent automation solves that by turning screening into a consistent, data-driven process. It automatically gathers payment history from systems like PayPal or Stripe, pulls credit bureau data, verifies current employment, and feeds everything into an AI agent (GPT-4o) for analysis. The AI calculates a predictive risk score and automatically routes alerts—high-risk cases trigger immediate email interventions, medium-risk updates go to Slack for monitoring, and all results are logged for review. It transforms a reactive, manual chore into a proactive, scalable risk management system.

How It Works

The workflow acts as a central decision engine for your rental portfolio, running on a schedule you set (e.g., daily for new applicants, monthly for existing tenants).

Step 1: Data Aggregation

The system starts by fetching data from multiple sources simultaneously. It connects to payment gateways to pull rent payment history, queries credit bureaus via their APIs for financial health scores, and verifies employment status through integrated HR platforms. This creates a unified financial profile for each tenant.

Step 2: AI-Powered Risk Analysis

All aggregated data is sent to an AI model like GPT-4o. The AI doesn't just check boxes; it interprets the data. It looks for patterns—like a gradual increase in late payments coupled with a new credit inquiry—that suggest rising financial stress. It outputs a clear risk score (e.g., Low, Medium, High) and a concise reasoning summary.

Step 3: Intelligent Alert Routing

Based on the risk score, the workflow triggers different actions. A "High" score sends an immediate, detailed email alert to the property manager with the tenant's profile and AI reasoning. A "Medium" score posts a summary to a designated Slack channel for team awareness. Every result, including "Low" risk scores, is saved to a database like Airtable for a complete audit trail and portfolio overview.

Step 4: Proactive Collections Initiation

For high-risk flags, the workflow can automatically initiate pre-collections processes. This might include sending a formal payment reminder via Gmail, updating the tenant's status in your property management software, or scheduling a follow-up task in your CRM—all before a payment is officially missed.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n instance: A self-hosted n8n setup or a n8n.cloud account.
  2. Data Source Credentials: API keys or login access for your chosen payment system (e.g., Stripe), a credit/background check service, and an employment verification API.
  3. OpenAI API Key: For accessing the GPT-4o model to perform the risk analysis.
  4. Communication Platform Access: Gmail/Google Workspace credentials for sending email alerts and a Slack webhook URL for posting to channels.
  5. Database (Optional but recommended): An Airtable base or similar database to store screening results and history.

Quick Setup Guide

Import and configure this workflow in under 15 minutes to start automating your tenant screenings.

  1. Download & Import: Click the "Download Template" button above to get the JSON file. In your n8n instance, go to "Workflows", click "Import from file", and select the downloaded file.
  2. Configure Credentials: In the n8n workflow, click on each node that requires external data (like "HTTP Request" for credit checks, "Gmail", "Slack"). Set up the required credentials for each service in n8n's settings.
  3. Set Your Thresholds: Locate the "Switch" node that routes alerts based on risk score. Adjust the numerical thresholds (e.g., what score constitutes "High" vs. "Medium" risk) to match your risk tolerance.
  4. Test with Sample Data: Use the "Manual Trigger" node to run the workflow once with dummy tenant data. Verify that the AI analysis runs, the score is generated, and alerts are sent to the correct destinations (your email, Slack).
  5. Activate & Schedule: Once tested, activate the workflow. Set the "Schedule Trigger" node to run daily for new applications or monthly for portfolio monitoring.

Pro tip: Start by testing the workflow with a few known tenant profiles (with permission) to calibrate the AI's scoring against your own experience. Adjust the prompt instructions in the "OpenAI Chat Model" node to emphasize factors most important to your decision-making.

What This Workflow Does

Manual Amazon product research is time-consuming and inconsistent. This automation solves that by intelligently scraping keyboard product data—titles, URLs, categories, and more—using AI-powered extraction, then automatically organizing everything into Google Sheets for analysis.

The workflow runs on a schedule you set, ensuring your market data is always fresh. It transforms hours of manual browsing into a fully automated process that delivers structured, actionable intelligence about competitor offerings, pricing trends, and market gaps.

Whether you're tracking specific brands, monitoring price changes, or conducting category-wide research, this template provides the automated foundation for data-driven e-commerce decisions.

How It Works

1. Scheduled Trigger

The workflow activates automatically based on your preferred schedule—daily, weekly, or custom intervals. This ensures your research data remains current without manual intervention.

2. AI-Powered Scraping with ScrapeGraphAI

Using natural language instructions, the AI navigates Amazon search results, understands page structure, and extracts precise product information even from complex, dynamic layouts.

3. Data Processing & Structuring

Raw scraped data is cleaned, formatted, and organized into consistent columns. The system handles variations in product listings to deliver uniform, analysis-ready information.

4. Google Sheets Integration

Processed data automatically appends to your designated Google Sheet with proper column mapping. Each execution adds new rows with timestamps, creating a historical research database.

Pro tip: Start with a focused product category (like "mechanical keyboards") before expanding. This helps validate data quality and refine your scraping parameters.

Quick Setup Guide

Estimated time: 10-15 minutes

  1. Install the template: Download the JSON file above and import it into your n8n instance.
  2. Configure credentials: Add your ScrapeGraphAI API key and Google Sheets OAuth2 credentials in n8n's credential management.
  3. Customize the search: Update the Amazon URL in the ScrapeGraphAI node to target your specific keyboard category or search terms.
  4. Set your spreadsheet: Point the Google Sheets node to your target spreadsheet and worksheet name.
  5. Adjust the schedule: Configure the Schedule Trigger node to run at your preferred frequency (consider Amazon's rate limits).
  6. Test and validate: Run the workflow once manually to verify data flows correctly into your Google Sheet.

Pro tip: Implement a 2-3 second delay between scrapes when testing to avoid rate limiting. Once validated, you can adjust for production use.

What This Workflow Does

Manual lead research is time-consuming and inconsistent. This automation solves that by systematically scraping Google Maps for businesses based on your search terms (like "restaurants in North Holland") and compiling the data into a Google Sheet. It captures business names, addresses, phone numbers, websites, and locations—creating a ready-to-use lead list in minutes instead of hours.

The workflow bridges the gap between discovery and outreach. Instead of copying and pasting from multiple search results, you get a structured dataset that can be immediately imported into your CRM or used for email campaigns. It turns a sporadic manual task into a repeatable, scalable process that runs on autopilot.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence to gather, process, and store lead data efficiently.

1. Trigger & Search Execution

The workflow starts manually or on a schedule. It sends your search query to the Apify Google Maps Scraper actor, which simulates a user search and extracts business listings from the map results.

2. Data Processing & Wait

A short wait node ensures the scraping task completes fully, especially for larger result sets. The system then retrieves the dataset from Apify's storage, ensuring all records are captured.

3. Mapping & Spreadsheet Update

The raw data is mapped to specific columns in your Google Sheet—Title, Street, Postcode, City, Website, and Phone. Each new lead is appended as a row, maintaining a clean, organized database that grows with each run.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (self-hosted due to community nodes).
  2. An Apify account with access to the Google Maps Scraper actor.
  3. A Google Cloud project with Sheets API enabled and credentials.
  4. A Google Sheet prepared with column headers: Title, Street, Postcode, City, Website, Phone.
  5. Clear search terms for your target businesses and locations.

Pro tip: Start with specific, niche search terms (e.g., "HVAC contractors Miami") rather than broad categories. This yields higher-quality leads and reduces data noise in your spreadsheet.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to implement this automation in your n8n environment.

  1. Import the template: Download the JSON file and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. Configure Apify: In the "Run Apify scraper" node, select your Google Maps Scraper actor and input your search terms.
  3. Connect Google Sheets: Authenticate with your Google account in the "Google Sheets" node and select your target spreadsheet and worksheet.
  4. Map the fields: Ensure the data mapping aligns with your sheet's column structure (Title, Street, Postcode, etc.).
  5. Test and activate: Run the workflow once manually to verify data flows correctly, then schedule it for regular execution.

What This Workflow Does

Manually copying product details from websites is a tedious, error-prone task that consumes valuable hours. This automation solves that by turning a simple Google Sheet into a powerful product research engine. Whenever you or your team adds a website URL to a designated column, the workflow instantly visits the page, uses AI to intelligently identify and extract key product information (name, price, description), and neatly organizes it into another sheet.

It's designed for business users who need structured data without writing a single line of code. Whether you're tracking competitor prices, building a product catalog, or enriching sales leads with detailed information, this system eliminates the manual grunt work. The AI component ensures reliable extraction even from websites with complex or frequently changing layouts.

The result is a self-updating database of product information, giving you a competitive edge through faster insights and freeing your team to focus on analysis and strategy instead of data entry.

How It Works

The workflow is a four-step pipeline that transforms a URL into structured data.

Step 1: Trigger on New URL

The automation is initiated by the "Google Sheets Trigger" node. It constantly watches a specific sheet (your "input" sheet) for new rows. When a new website URL is pasted into the designated "Website" column, the workflow instantly activates, passing that URL to the next step. This setup allows for batch processing—you can add dozens of URLs at once, and the system will process them sequentially.

Step 2: AI-Powered Data Extraction

The core magic happens in the "Dumpling AI" node. The workflow sends the captured URL to Dumpling AI's API. Instead of relying on fragile HTML selectors, the AI analyzes the page content semantically. It's instructed via a pre-defined schema to look for and return specific product attributes: productName, price, productDescription, and optionally review data. This AI approach handles diverse website designs with high accuracy.

Step 3: Handling Multiple Products

Many product pages list multiple items. The "Split Out" node acts as a smart filter. If Dumpling AI returns an array of several products from a single URL (like a category page), this node separates them into individual, distinct data items. This ensures each product gets its own row in the final output sheet, maintaining clean, granular data.

Step 4: Populate Your Database

The final step uses the "Google Sheets" node to insert the newly extracted and split data. It appends each product's details as a new row in your designated "product details" output sheet. The columns are automatically mapped, creating a ready-to-use database of product information that updates automatically whenever a new source URL is provided.

Pro tip: For high-volume scraping, add a small delay node between steps to be respectful to target websites and avoid being blocked.

Quick Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get your automated product scraper running in under 10 minutes:

  1. Download & Import: Click the "Download Template" button above to get the JSON file. In your n8n instance, create a new workflow and use the "Import from File" function to load it.
  2. Configure Google Sheets: In the trigger node, replace the placeholder Spreadsheet ID and Sheet name with your own. Ensure your source sheet has a column with the header "Website". Create your output sheet with headers: productName, price, productDescription.
  3. Connect Your APIs: Create credentials in n8n for both Google Sheets (using OAuth2) and Dumpling AI (using your API key). Connect these credentials to the respective nodes in the workflow.
  4. Test with a URL: Add a product page URL (e.g., an Amazon listing, a Shopify store product) to your input Google Sheet. Activate the workflow and watch as the data populates your output sheet automatically.
  5. Activate & Scale: Once tested, toggle the workflow to "Active". You can now add URLs in bulk. The system will process them one by one, building your product database hands-free.

Pro tip: Use the "Split Out" node's settings to handle pages that list many products. You can set a limit (e.g., first 10 products) to control the volume of data extracted from a single URL.

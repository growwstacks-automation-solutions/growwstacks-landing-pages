AI Resume Screening with OpenAI, Google Sheets & Gmail

What This Workflow Does

Manual resume screening is one of the most time-consuming tasks in recruitment. HR teams spend hours reviewing hundreds of applications, often leading to inconsistent evaluations, unconscious bias, and missed ideal candidates. This workflow solves that problem by automating the entire initial screening process with artificial intelligence.

The system extracts content from uploaded resumes (PDF format), sends it to OpenAI GPT-4o for intelligent evaluation against your specific job criteria, classifies candidates as accepted or rejected based on a configurable score threshold, logs detailed results to Google Sheets for audit trails, and automatically sends personalized follow-up emails via Gmail. It transforms a multi-hour manual process into a fully automated pipeline that runs in minutes.

How It Works

1. Resume Submission & Extraction

Candidates upload their resumes through a form interface. The workflow extracts text content from PDF files, preparing it for AI analysis while preserving formatting and structure.

2. AI Evaluation with OpenAI GPT-4o

The extracted resume content is sent to OpenAI's GPT-4o model with a custom prompt that evaluates candidates against your specific criteria: must-have qualifications, technical skills, experience levels, and soft skills. The AI assigns a numerical score and provides detailed justification.

3. Classification & Decision Routing

Based on your configured score threshold (e.g., 80/100), candidates are automatically classified as "Accepted" or "Rejected." The workflow routes each candidate to the appropriate next step without manual intervention.

4. Data Logging to Google Sheets

All evaluation data—including candidate details, AI score, justification, and classification—is logged to Google Sheets. Accepted candidates go to an "Accepted" tab, rejected to a "Rejected" tab, creating a searchable talent database.

5. Automated Follow-up via Gmail

Personalized emails are automatically sent: interview invitations to accepted candidates and polite rejection notices to rejected candidates. Templates can be customized with candidate-specific details from the evaluation.

Pro tip: Start with a conservative score threshold (like 75/100) and adjust based on the quality of candidates that pass through. You can always lower the threshold if you're not getting enough qualified applicants.

Who This Is For

This workflow is ideal for recruitment agencies, HR departments in growing companies, tech startups hiring technical roles, and any business that receives more than 20 applications per position. It's particularly valuable for roles with specific technical requirements where manual screening is complex and time-consuming.

Small businesses can use it to compete with larger companies by implementing professional, automated recruitment processes without hiring additional staff. Enterprises can deploy it across multiple departments to ensure consistent, unbiased initial screening at scale.

  4. Gmail account or Google Workspace for sending emails

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the template into your n8n instance using the downloaded JSON file.
  2. Configure credentials for OpenAI, Google Sheets, and Gmail in n8n's credentials management.
  3. Customize the AI prompt by replacing placeholder tags with your company name, role details, evaluation criteria, and score threshold.
  4. Prepare your Google Sheet with columns for: Candidate Name, Email, Resume Summary, AI Score, Evaluation Justification, and Status.
  5. Test with sample resumes to ensure the AI scoring aligns with your expectations before going live.
  6. Deploy the workflow and connect it to your resume submission form (via webhook or form trigger).

Pro tip: Create a "Maybe" category in your Google Sheets for candidates scoring within 5 points of your threshold. These are worth a quick manual review—sometimes the AI misses nuanced experience.

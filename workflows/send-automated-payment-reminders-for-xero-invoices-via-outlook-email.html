What This Workflow Does

Late payments are one of the most common cash flow problems for small businesses, freelancers, and professional service providers. Manual invoice follow-up consumes valuable administrative time, creates inconsistent communication, and often leads to awkward customer interactions. This automation solves these problems by systematically monitoring your Xero invoices and sending polite, timely payment reminders via Outlook email.

The workflow runs daily, checking all unpaid invoices, calculating days until due, and sending personalized reminders for invoices approaching their due date. It creates a complete audit trail in Xero's invoice history, ensuring you have documentation of every communication. This transforms your accounts receivable process from a reactive, manual task into a proactive, automated system that improves payment speed while maintaining customer relationships.

How It Works

Step 1: Daily Invoice Check

A schedule trigger initiates the workflow every day at noon (configurable). It connects to your Xero account via API and fetches all current invoices, filtering out those already marked as paid to avoid unnecessary communication.

Step 2: Due Date Calculation

A code node calculates how many days remain until each invoice's due date. This logic allows you to customize reminder thresholds—for example, sending reminders 7 days before due date, or immediately when an invoice becomes overdue.

Step 3: Customer Email Delivery

For invoices meeting your criteria, the workflow sends personalized email reminders via Outlook. Each email includes the invoice number, due date, amount, and polite payment request. You can customize the template with your branding, payment instructions, and contact details.

Step 4: Audit Trail Creation

After sending each reminder, the workflow logs the activity back into Xero's invoice history. This creates a transparent record showing when reminders were sent, providing accountability for your team and clarity for customer service inquiries.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for small business owners, freelance professionals, accounting teams, bookkeepers, and service-based companies that use Xero for invoicing. If you send regular invoices to clients and experience delayed payments, this workflow eliminates the manual follow-up burden. It's particularly valuable for businesses with recurring clients, subscription services, project-based billing, or any scenario where consistent payment reminders improve cash flow predictability.

What You'll Need

  1. A Xero account with API access enabled (available to all Xero users)
  2. A Microsoft Outlook or Office 365 account for sending emails
  3. Valid email addresses configured for all customers in Xero contact records
  4. An n8n instance (self-hosted or cloud) with OAuth2 credentials configured for Xero and Outlook
  5. Basic understanding of n8n workflow import and credential setup

Pro tip: Before activating the workflow, send test reminders to yourself to verify email formatting and tone. Adjust the template to match your brand voice—a polite, professional reminder often yields better results than an aggressive demand.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template file using the button above.
  2. Import the JSON file into your n8n instance.
  3. Configure OAuth2 credentials for Xero (with invoice read and history write permissions) and Outlook.
  4. Assign these credentials to the corresponding nodes in the workflow.
  5. Customize the email template in the Outlook node with your signature, branding, and payment instructions.
  6. Adjust the reminder threshold in the code node if desired (default is 7 days before due date).
  7. Test the workflow manually with your actual Xero data before activating the schedule.
  8. Activate the workflow—it will now run automatically daily.

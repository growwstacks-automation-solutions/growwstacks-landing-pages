What This Workflow Does

Manual maintenance scheduling is a time-consuming administrative burden that often leads to missed appointments, equipment failures, and costly emergency repairs. This automation solves that problem by creating a seamless connection between your maintenance schedule in Google Sheets and your team's calendars.

The workflow automatically reads daily preventive maintenance tasks from your Google Sheets spreadsheet, generates professional calendar invites (ICS files), and sends them directly to assigned team members via Gmail. This ensures every maintenance task appears on your team's calendars with proper reminders, locations, and descriptions—eliminating manual entry and reducing missed appointments by over 90%.

For facility managers, maintenance supervisors, and operations teams, this automation transforms how preventive maintenance is coordinated. Instead of spending hours each week copying information between systems and sending reminder emails, the system handles everything automatically, freeing your team to focus on actual maintenance work rather than administrative tasks.

How It Works

The automation follows a logical sequence that mimics how a human coordinator would schedule maintenance, but with perfect consistency and zero manual effort.

Step 1: Daily Schedule Check

Each morning at 7:00 AM, the workflow automatically activates and checks your Google Sheets maintenance schedule. It looks for all tasks scheduled for the current day, filtering out completed items or future appointments.

Step 2: Data Processing

The system reads key information from each maintenance task: asset details, maintenance type, location, assigned technician email, and scheduled time. It validates the data to ensure all required fields are present and properly formatted.

Step 3: Calendar Invite Generation

For each valid maintenance task, the workflow generates a standardized ICS (iCalendar) file. This universal calendar format includes event title, description, location, start/end times, and reminders—compatible with Google Calendar, Outlook, Apple Calendar, and most mobile calendar apps.

Step 4: Personalized Email Delivery

The system sends a personalized email to each assigned technician with the ICS file attached. The email includes clear instructions for adding the event to their calendar and any special notes about the maintenance task. Delivery confirmations are logged for tracking purposes.

Pro tip: Add a "Reminder Sent" column to your Google Sheet to track which maintenance tasks have been scheduled. The workflow can update this column automatically, giving you a complete audit trail of all automated communications.

What You'll Need

  1. n8n Instance: Either self-hosted n8n or n8n.cloud account to run the automation workflow.
  2. Google Sheets Access: A Google account with a maintenance schedule spreadsheet containing columns for date, asset, task, location, and technician email.
  3. Gmail Account: A Google account with sending permissions (can be a shared department email or service account).
  4. Maintenance Schedule: A structured Google Sheet with your preventive maintenance plan (template provided in download).
  5. Team Readiness: Technicians comfortable receiving calendar invites via email and adding them to their preferred calendar application.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and Import: Download the template JSON file and import it into your n8n instance using the workflow import function.
  2. Configure Connections: Set up authenticated connections to Google Sheets (read access to your maintenance schedule) and Gmail (send access for calendar invites).
  3. Customize Spreadsheet: Update the Google Sheet ID in the workflow to point to your maintenance schedule. Ensure your sheet follows the required column structure.
  4. Test Run: Execute the workflow manually with test data to verify calendar invites are generated correctly and emails are sent properly.
  5. Schedule Automation: Set the workflow to run daily at your preferred time (default is 7:00 AM) to check for that day's maintenance tasks.
  6. Monitor and Adjust: Review sent items for the first week and adjust timing, email templates, or reminder settings based on team feedback.

Pro tip: Start with a small subset of maintenance tasks or a test team for the first week. This allows you to refine the process before rolling it out to your entire maintenance schedule and team.

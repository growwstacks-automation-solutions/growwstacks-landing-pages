What This Workflow Does

This automation solves the common problem of email overload by automatically applying labels to incoming Gmail messages. Instead of manually sorting hundreds of emails daily, this workflow scans new messages as they arrive and categorizes them based on rules you define.

Whether you're separating client communications from internal team discussions, flagging urgent requests, or organizing emails by project, this template handles the repetitive task of inbox organization so you can focus on what matters. The workflow connects to your Gmail account, monitors for new emails, evaluates them against your criteria, and applies the appropriate labels automatically.

How It Works

Step 1: Trigger on New Emails

The workflow starts when a new email arrives in your Gmail inbox. You can configure it to run immediately or on a schedule that fits your workflow.

Step 2: Evaluate Email Content

Each email is analyzed based on your predefined rules. You can check the sender's address, subject line keywords, email body content, attachments, or any combination of these factors.

Step 3: Apply Appropriate Labels

Based on the evaluation results, the workflow applies one or more Gmail labels to the email. You can create new labels automatically or use existing ones in your Gmail account.

Step 4: Optional Notifications & Logging

The workflow can optionally send you notifications about labeled emails or log the actions to a spreadsheet for tracking and auditing purposes.

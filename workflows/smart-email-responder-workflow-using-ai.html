What This Workflow Does

This intelligent email management system solves the common business problem of email overload. Instead of manually reading and responding to every incoming message, this workflow uses Google's Gemini 2.0 AI to automatically process emails as they arrive.

The system categorizes emails into three main types: customer queries, project updates, and feedback. For each category, it generates appropriate, context-specific responses that maintain your brand voice and professional standards. Beyond just replying, it can schedule calendar invitations for meetings, mark emails as read, apply organizational labels, and log correspondence—all without human intervention.

The result is a significant reduction in manual email workload while ensuring timely, consistent communication with clients, partners, and team members. It transforms email from a time-consuming administrative task into an automated business process that enhances operational productivity.

How It Works

1. Email Trigger & Capture

The workflow starts by monitoring your connected email account (Gmail, Outlook, etc.) for new messages. When an email arrives, it captures the full content including subject, body, sender information, and any attachments.

2. AI Analysis & Categorization

The email content is sent to Google's Gemini AI model, which analyzes the intent, tone, and key elements. The AI categorizes the email into predefined types: queries (questions needing answers), project updates (status reports), or feedback (comments/suggestions).

3. Response Generation

Based on the category and specific content, the AI generates an appropriate response. For queries, it provides helpful information; for updates, it acknowledges receipt; for feedback, it thanks the sender and notes the input. The response maintains professional tone and can include personalized elements.

4. Action Execution

The workflow then executes appropriate actions: sending the generated reply, scheduling calendar events if meetings are requested, applying organizational labels, marking the email as processed, and logging the interaction for tracking purposes.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the template JSON file into your n8n instance using the import function

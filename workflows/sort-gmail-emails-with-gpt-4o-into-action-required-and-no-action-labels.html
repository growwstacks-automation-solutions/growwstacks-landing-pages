AI-Powered Gmail Email Sorting with GPT-4o

Automatically classify unread emails as 'Action Required' or 'No Action' using AI. Eliminate inbox noise and focus only on what matters.

What This Workflow Does

Email overload is a universal business problem. Professionals spend hours each week manually sifting through their inboxes, trying to separate urgent emails from newsletters, updates, and promotional content. This mental sorting is inefficient, stressful, and leads to missed opportunities.

This automation solves that by using GPT-4o to intelligently scan your unread Gmail emails and classify them into two clear categories: Action Required (emails that need your reply, review, scheduling, or response) and No Action (informational or promotional emails that can be read later or ignored). The workflow then labels emails accordingly and removes them from your primary inbox view, creating a clean, actionable list to start your day.

Unlike archiving or deleting, this system keeps all emails in your account—just organized intelligently. You regain control over your attention and time, focusing only on what truly matters.

How It Works

Step 1: Scheduled Trigger

The workflow runs on a customizable schedule (e.g., every morning at 8 AM, or every hour). It activates automatically, so you don't need to manually trigger it.

Step 2: Fetch Unread Emails

It connects to your Gmail account via secure OAuth2 and fetches all unread emails from your inbox. The automation respects your email privacy and only accesses what's necessary.

Step 3: AI Classification with GPT-4o

Each email's content, sender, subject, and context is sent to GPT-4o (or GPT-4o-mini) with a prompt asking: "Does this email require action from the recipient?" The AI analyzes intent, urgency, and relevance, returning a clear classification.

Step 4: Apply Gmail Labels

Based on the AI's decision, the workflow applies the "Action Required" label to emails needing attention and the "No Action" label to informational ones. It also removes the "Inbox" label, moving emails out of your primary view while keeping them accessible via their respective labels.

Step 5: Clean Daily Routine

You start your day by opening the "Action Required" label in Gmail, reviewing only emails that matter. The "No Action" label serves as a reading folder for later. Your inbox remains empty, reducing psychological clutter.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (self-hosted or n8n.cloud account).
  2. A Gmail account with OAuth2 access enabled.
  3. An OpenAI API key (for GPT-4o or GPT-4o-mini).
  4. Two Gmail labels created manually: "Action Required" and "No Action".
  5. Basic understanding of n8n credential setup (guided steps provided).

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Import the workflow: Download the template JSON file and import it into your n8n instance via the "Import from File" option.
  2. Configure credentials: Set up Gmail OAuth2 credentials and add your OpenAI API key in n8n's credential management.
  3. Create Gmail labels: In your Gmail account, manually create two labels named exactly "Action Required" and "No Action".
  4. Adjust schedule: Modify the Schedule Trigger node to run at your preferred time (e.g., daily at 8 AM).
  5. Activate and test: Turn on the workflow and let it run once with a few test emails to verify classification accuracy.

Pro tip: Fine-tune the AI prompt in the workflow if you have specific email types (like support tickets or contract reviews) that always require action. Adding examples improves accuracy.

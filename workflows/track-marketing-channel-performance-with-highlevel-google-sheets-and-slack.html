What This Workflow Does

This automation template solves the critical business challenge of understanding which marketing channels actually drive conversions. Many businesses struggle with wasted ad spend because they can't accurately track which channels generate closed deals.

The workflow fetches all opportunities from HighLevel (GHL), filters for "Closed Won" deals, computes lead-to-sale conversion metrics per source, and sends a summary report to Slack while logging raw data into Google Sheets for ongoing analysis. It provides marketing teams, growth analysts, and sales managers with data-driven insights to optimize budget allocation.

How It Works

Step 1: Fetch Opportunity Data

The workflow begins by pulling every deal record from your GHL CRM, including status, amount, and lead source fields. This creates a complete dataset for analysis.

Step 2: Filter for Closed-Won Deals

The automation separates deals by outcome - only "Won" deals are used for conversion tracking, while others trigger Slack alerts for team review of lost opportunities.

Step 3: Log Data to Google Sheets

Every successful deal's details are saved into a structured Google Sheet, creating a historical record for long-term performance tracking and trend analysis.

Step 4: Calculate Channel Metrics

The workflow aggregates results by lead source, automatically calculating total deals, conversion rate, and total revenue per channel to identify top performers.

Step 5: Send Slack Summary

A formatted summary of conversion metrics is posted to a dedicated Slack channel (like #lead-source-report), ensuring visibility for marketing and sales teams.

Tracking marketing channel performance helps identify which channels drive actual conversions, allowing businesses to optimize ad spend and focus on high-performing channels. Without proper tracking, companies risk wasting budget on ineffective marketing efforts.

For example, a company might discover their expensive paid ads generate lots of leads but few sales, while organic search brings fewer leads but higher conversions. This insight enables smarter budget reallocation.

Automating marketing performance tracking saves hours of manual reporting work, provides real-time visibility into conversion metrics, and ensures consistent data collection across all channels.

Automation also eliminates human error in manual reporting and enables faster decision-making based on accurate, up-to-date performance data rather than outdated spreadsheets.

Integrating HighLevel with Google Sheets creates a centralized data repository for long-term performance tracking and analysis. This allows for custom reporting, historical comparisons, and sharing data with stakeholders who may not have direct access to HighLevel.

The combination also enables advanced analysis through Google Sheets formulas, pivot tables, and data visualization that might not be available in HighLevel's native reporting.

This workflow can track any marketing channel captured in HighLevel's opportunity data, including paid ads, organic search, social media, email campaigns, referrals, and offline channels.

The key is ensuring your lead source data is properly captured in HighLevel. We recommend standardizing channel naming conventions to make analysis more accurate and actionable.

Most businesses benefit from weekly performance reports to monitor trends and make timely adjustments. However, the ideal frequency depends on your sales cycle length and marketing activity volume.

Daily reports work best for high-volume campaigns with short sales cycles, while monthly reports may suffice for businesses with longer sales cycles and fewer marketing touches.

Key metrics include conversion rate (leads to sales), cost per acquisition, average deal size by channel, and ROI. Also track velocity metrics like time from lead to conversion to understand channel efficiency beyond just volume.

Secondary metrics like lead volume and engagement rates can provide context but shouldn't be primary decision drivers. Focus on metrics that tie directly to revenue impact.

  • Conversion rate by channel
  • Revenue per channel
  • Customer acquisition cost
  • Sales cycle length by source

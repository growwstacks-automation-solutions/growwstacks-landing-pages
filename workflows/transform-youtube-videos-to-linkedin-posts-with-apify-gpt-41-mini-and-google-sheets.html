What This Workflow Does

Content creators and marketing teams often struggle to repurpose video content across platforms. YouTube videos contain valuable insights, but manually rewriting them for LinkedIn takes hours. This automation solves that by creating a seamless pipeline from YouTube to LinkedIn.

The workflow monitors any YouTube channel for new uploads. When a video is published, it extracts the full transcript using Apify, sends it to GPT-4.1 Mini to generate two thought-leader LinkedIn posts, and saves the structured content to a Google Sheet for review and scheduling. This turns video production into multi-platform content without extra work.

How It Works

Step 1: YouTube Channel Monitoring

The RSS Feed Trigger node watches a specific YouTube channel's RSS feed. When a new video is uploaded, it captures the video URL and metadata, initiating the automation.

Step 2: Transcript Extraction

Apify scrapes the video's transcript, converting spoken content into text. This provides the raw material for AI processing.

Step 3: AI Content Generation

GPT-4.1 Mini analyzes the transcript, identifies core themes, and generates two LinkedIn posts formatted with hooks, body content, CTAs, and hashtags—tailored to professional audiences.

Step 4: Google Sheets Storage

The generated posts are saved as new rows in Google Sheets, organized by video link, theme, hook, body, CTA, and hashtags. This creates a ready-to-use content repository.

What You'll Need

  1. An n8n instance (cloud or self-hosted)
  2. Apify account and API key for transcript scraping
  3. OpenAI API key for GPT-4.1 Mini access
  4. Google account with a prepared Google Sheet (with columns: Video Link, Theme, Hook, Body, CTA, Hashtags)
  5. The YouTube channel ID you want to monitor

Pro tip: Use multiple RSS Feed Trigger nodes to monitor several YouTube channels simultaneously. Connect all triggers to the same Apify node to create a scalable multi-channel repurposing system.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download the template and import it into your n8n workspace.
  2. In the RSS Feed Trigger node, insert your YouTube channel's RSS URL (format: https://www.youtube.com/feeds/videos.xml?channel_id=YOUR_ID).
  3. Add your Apify and OpenAI API credentials in their respective nodes.
  4. Prepare your Google Sheet with the six column headers exactly as listed, then authenticate and select it in the Google Sheets node.
  5. Activate the workflow. It will now automatically process new videos.

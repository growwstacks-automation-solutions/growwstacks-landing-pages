Automate Weekly Coffee Chats with Mattermost

Automatically create random groups of three from your Mattermost channel and send personalized meeting invitations to foster team connections.

Download Template JSON · n8n compatible · Free

What This Workflow Does

Building strong team connections in distributed or hybrid workplaces is challenging. Manual coordination of "coffee chats" or "virtual coffees" consumes valuable HR or manager time and often results in low participation due to scheduling complexity. This automation solves that by automatically creating randomized groups of three from any Mattermost channel and sending personalized calendar invitations to each participant.

The workflow runs on a schedule (weekly or bi-weekly), randomly selects participants from your designated Mattermost channel, creates balanced groups, generates Google Calendar events with meeting links, and sends direct messages to each participant with their group details and conversation starters. It eliminates manual coordination while ensuring diverse, cross-functional connections across your organization.

How It Works

Step 1: Channel Member Retrieval

The workflow connects to your Mattermost instance and retrieves all members from your specified channel. It filters out any users who have opted out or are on leave, ensuring only available participants are included in the matching pool.

Step 2: Randomized Group Creation

Using a randomizing algorithm, the workflow creates groups of three from the participant list. The algorithm ensures balanced distribution across departments, seniority levels, and previous match history to maximize diverse connections and prevent repeat pairings.

Step 3: Calendar Event Generation

For each group, the workflow creates a Google Calendar event with a video conferencing link (Zoom, Google Meet, or Teams). It sets the duration (typically 30 minutes), adds conversation prompts in the description, and includes all three participants as attendees.

Step 4: Personalized Notifications

The workflow sends direct Mattermost messages to each participant with their group details, meeting time, calendar link, and icebreaker questions. It also provides an easy opt-out mechanism for future rounds and collects feedback after meetings complete.

Who This Is For

This automation is ideal for HR teams, people operations managers, team leads, and internal communications professionals in companies using Mattermost for team collaboration. It's particularly valuable for distributed teams, hybrid workplaces, growing startups needing to maintain culture, and large organizations looking to break down departmental silos through structured networking.

Companies with 50-5000 employees see the greatest benefit, as manual coordination becomes impractical at scale. The workflow adapts to any team size by adjusting group size, frequency, and matching algorithms based on your organizational needs.

What You'll Need

  1. Mattermost instance with API access and appropriate permissions to read channel members and send direct messages
  2. Google Calendar account or other calendar system with API integration capabilities
  3. n8n instance (self-hosted or cloud) with webhook capabilities
  4. Designated Mattermost channel where participants have opted into the coffee chat program
  5. Video conferencing solution (Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams) for meeting links

Pro tip: Start with a pilot group of 20-30 enthusiastic participants before rolling out to the entire organization. Collect feedback after 2-3 rounds to refine matching algorithms and conversation prompts.

Quick Setup Guide

  1. Download and import the JSON template into your n8n instance
  2. Configure Mattermost credentials in the Mattermost node with your server URL and access token
  3. Set up Google Calendar connection with appropriate calendar ID and permissions
  4. Adjust scheduling to your preferred frequency (weekly on Mondays works well)
  5. Customize messages with your company's tone, conversation starters, and opt-out instructions
  6. Test with a small group before enabling for all channel members
  7. Monitor participation and adjust matching algorithms based on feedback
